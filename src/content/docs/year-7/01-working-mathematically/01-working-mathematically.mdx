## 1. The Mathematician's Toolkit
Mathematics is more than just getting the right answer; it is a way of thinking and communicating. To "work mathematically," you need to move between the **concrete** (things you can touch or see) and the **abstract** (symbols and equations).

### Key Vocabulary 
* **Investigate:** To carry out a systematic inquiry to discover facts.
* **Explore:** To try different methods to see how a pattern works.
* **Evidence:** The data or facts that support your findings.
* **Justify:** To provide a reason or "proof" for why your answer is correct.
* **Method:** The specific steps or techniques you used to solve the problem.

---

## 2. The Cycle of Inquiry
When faced with a new problem, mathematicians follow a strategic process to find a solution:



1.  **See Patterns:** Look for relationships between numbers, shapes, or symbols.
2.  **Make Conjectures:** Create a "mathematical guess" about what the pattern might be.
3.  **Test & Prove:** Look for **proofs** that support your idea or **counter-examples** that show your idea might be wrong.
4.  **Communicate:** Express your ideas using a mix of words, diagrams, graphs, and symbols.

---

## 3. Communicating Your Ideas
A huge part of Year 7 math is learning to "speak the language." You are expected to explain your thinking in three ways:

| Method | What it looks like |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Orally** | Explaining your steps out loud to a peer or teacher. |
| **Visually** | Using diagrams, tables, or graphs to show a relationship. |
| **In Writing** | Using mathematical symbols ($+$, $-$, $\times$, $\div$, $\therefore$) and precise words. |

<Aside type="note" title="Reflective Thinking">
Always ask yourself: "Was the approach I took to this problem appropriate?" If it wasn't, what would you change next time? 
</Aside>

## Practice Investigation

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 The Handshake Problem">
    **Investigate:** If there are 4 people in a room and everyone shakes hands with everyone else exactly once, how many handshakes happen in total?
    
    1. **Explore:** Draw a diagram (dots for people, lines for handshakes).
    2. **Evidence:** Count the lines.
    3. **Justify:** Explain why your answer is correct.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution & Communication">
    **1. Visual Representation:**
    Imagine 4 dots in a square. 
    * Person A shakes with B, C, and D (3 handshakes).
    * Person B has already shaken with A, so they shake with C and D (2 handshakes).
    * Person C has already shaken with A and B, so they shake with D (1 handshake).
    * Person D has already shaken with everyone.
    
    **2. Mathematical Expression:**
    $$3 + 2 + 1 = 6 \text{ handshakes}$$
    
    **3. Justification:**
    Each of the $n$ people shakes hands with $n-1$ other people. However, we divide by 2 because one handshake involves two people (A shaking B is the same as B shaking A).
  </TabItem>
