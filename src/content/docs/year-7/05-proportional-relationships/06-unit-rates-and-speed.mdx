## 1. Inquiry: The Meaning of "Per"
**Inquiry Questions:** *What does the slash (/) in km/h actually mean? How can we compare two runners if they ran different distances in different times?*

A **Unit Rate** is a ratio that compares a quantity to **one unit** of another quantity. Whenever you see the word "per," you are looking at a unit rate.
* **Speed:** Kilometers **per** hour (km/h)
* **Price:** Dollars **per** kilogram ($/kg)
* **Heart Rate:** Beats **per** minute (bpm)



---

## 2. Calculating Unit Rates
To find a unit rate, you simply divide the first quantity by the second quantity.

### The Speed Formula
Speed is one of the most common unit rates in mathematics.
$$\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$$

**Example:**
A cyclist travels 45 km in 3 hours. What is their average speed?
1. $45 \div 3 = 15$
2. The unit rate is **15 km/h**.

---

## 3. Comparing Rates
Unit rates allow us to compare two different situations fairly.

**Example: Who is the faster typist?**
* **Typist A:** 200 words in 5 minutes
* **Typist B:** 126 words in 3 minutes

1. Rate A: $200 \div 5 = 40$ words per minute.
2. Rate B: $126 \div 3 = 42$ words per minute.
* **Typist B** is faster.

### Q2: The Speed Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A train travels 210 km in 2.5 hours. What is its average speed?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $210 \div 2.5 = 84 \text{ km/h}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Comparing Value
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Which is the better "Unit Price"?
    
    A: 4 apples for 2.40 dollars
    B: 6 apples for 3.30 dollars
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    A: $2.40 \div 4 = 0.60$ dollars per apple.
    B: $3.30 \div 6 = 0.55$ dollars per apple.
    
    **B** is the better value.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: Distance from Rate
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If a person walks at a constant rate of 5 km/h, how far will they walk in 3.5 hours?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $5 \times 3.5 = 17.5 \text{ km}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
