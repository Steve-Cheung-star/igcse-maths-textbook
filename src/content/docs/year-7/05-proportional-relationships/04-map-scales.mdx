## 1. Inquiry: Shrinking the World
**Inquiry Questions:** *How can a single sheet of paper represent an entire city? Why is it important that every part of a map is shrunk by the exact same ratio?*

A map scale is a ratio that compares the distance on a map to the actual distance on the ground. For example, a scale of **1:100** means that 1 cm on the map represents 100 cm (or 1 meter) in real life.



---

## 2. Using Scale Ratios
There are two main ways to use a scale:

### Scaling Up (Map to Real Life)
To find the actual distance, multiply the map measurement by the scale factor.
* **Example:** Scale is 1:50,000. Map distance is 4 cm.
* Actual distance = $4 \times 50,000 = 200,000 \text{ cm}$.
* Convert to km: $200,000 \text{ cm} = 2,000 \text{ m} = 2 \text{ km}$.

### Scaling Down (Real Life to Map)
To find the distance for a drawing, divide the actual distance by the scale factor.
* **Example:** Scale is 1:100. Real wall is 5 meters (500 cm).
* Map distance = $500 \div 100 = 5 \text{ cm}$.

---

## 3. Representative Fractions
Sometimes scales are written as fractions, such as $\frac{1}{25,000}$. This is the same as the ratio **1:25,000**. It means the real object is 25,000 times larger than the drawing.

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Simple Scaling
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A map has a scale of 1:10,000. Calculate the real-world distance for the following map measurements:
    
    a) 2 cm
    
    b) 5.5 cm
    
    c) 10 cm
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $2 \times 10,000 = 20,000 \text{ cm}$ (200 meters)
    
    b) $5.5 \times 10,000 = 55,000 \text{ cm}$ (550 meters)
    
    c) $10 \times 10,000 = 100,000 \text{ cm}$ (1 kilometer)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Drawing to Scale
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    You are drawing a plan of your bedroom using a scale of 1:50. If your bed is 200 cm long in real life, how long should it be on your drawing?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $200 \div 50 = 4 \text{ cm}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Large Scale Maps
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    On a map with a scale of 1:250,000, the distance between two cities is 8 cm. What is the actual distance in kilometers?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. $8 \times 250,000 = 2,000,000 \text{ cm}$
    2. Convert to meters: $20,000 \text{ m}$
    3. Convert to km: **20 km**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: Model Cars
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A model car is built to a scale of 1:24. If the model car is 15 cm long, how long is the actual car in meters?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. $15 \times 24 = 360 \text{ cm}$
    2. Convert to meters: **3.6 meters**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
