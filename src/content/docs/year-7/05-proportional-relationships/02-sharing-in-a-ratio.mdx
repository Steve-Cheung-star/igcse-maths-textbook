## 1. Inquiry: Proportional Parts
**Inquiry Question:** *If two people work different hours on a project, how can we share the profit fairly using a ratio?*

Sharing in a ratio allows us to divide a total into unequal parts that still follow a specific relationship. For example, if we share 10 dollars in the ratio **3:2**, one person gets 6 dollars and the other gets 4 dollars.



---

## 2. The Four-Step Method
To share a total amount into a given ratio, follow these four steps (Add, Divide, Multiply, Check):

1.  **ADD** the parts of the ratio to find the total number of "shares."
2.  **DIVIDE** the total amount by the number of shares to find the value of **one share**.
3.  **MULTIPLY** each part of the original ratio by the value of one share.
4.  **CHECK** that your answers add back up to the original total.

**Example: Share 60 dollars in the ratio 3:2:1**
1.  $3 + 2 + 1 = 6$ shares total.
2.  $60 \div 6 = 10$ dollars per share.
3.  $3 \times 10 = 30$, $2 \times 10 = 20$, $1 \times 10 = 10$.
4.  $30 + 20 + 10 = 60$. (Correct!)

## 3. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Basic Sharing
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Share the following amounts in the given ratios:
    
    a) 20 dollars in the ratio 1:4
    
    b) 35 kg in the ratio 3:4
    
    c) 100 meters in the ratio 7:3
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 1+4=5 shares. 20/5=4. Results: **4 dollars and 16 dollars**.
    
    b) 3+4=7 shares. 35/7=5. Results: **15 kg and 20 kg**.
    
    c) 7+3=10 shares. 100/10=10. Results: **70 m and 30 m**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Three-Way Ratios
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Divide these totals into three parts:
    
    a) 24 sweets in the ratio 1:2:3
    
    b) 120 dollars in the ratio 2:3:5
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 1+2+3=6 shares. 24/6=4. Results: **4, 8, and 12 sweets**.
    
    b) 2+3+5=10 shares. 120/10=12. Results: **24, 36, and 60 dollars**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Real-World Business
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Two business partners, Amy and Ben, invest money in a shop in the ratio 3:5. At the end of the year, they make a profit of 4000 dollars. 
    
    How much profit does Ben receive?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Total shares: 3 + 5 = 8
    2. Value of one share: 4000 / 8 = 500
    3. Ben's share (5 parts): 5 × 500 = **2500 dollars**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: The Concrete Mix
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Concrete is made by mixing cement, sand, and gravel in the ratio 1:2:4. A builder needs to make 350 kg of concrete. 
    
    How much sand does he need?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Total shares: 1 + 2 + 4 = 7
    2. Value of one share: 350 / 7 = 50 kg
    3. Sand (2 parts): 2 × 50 = **100 kg**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
