## 1. Inquiry: Comparing Parts
**Inquiry Questions:** *How is a ratio different from a fraction? Where do we see ratios used in construction, cooking, or chemistry?*

A **ratio** is a way to compare two or more quantities of the same kind. We use a colon (**:**) to separate the numbers. 

For example, if a bag contains 2 red marbles and 3 blue marbles:
* The ratio of red to blue is **2:3**.
* The ratio of blue to red is **3:2**.



---

## 2. Simplifying Ratios
Just like fractions, ratios can be simplified by dividing all parts of the ratio by their **Highest Common Factor (HCF)**. A simplified ratio is easier to read and use in calculations.

**Example:**
Simplify the ratio **10:15**.
1. The HCF of 10 and 15 is 5.
2. $10 \div 5 = 2$ and $15 \div 5 = 3$.
3. The simplified ratio is **2:3**.

---

## 3. Ratios with Different Units
To write a ratio correctly, the units must be the same. If they are different, convert them to the smaller unit first.

**Example:**
Write a ratio for 2 cm to 10 mm.
1. Convert 2 cm to mm: $2 \text{ cm} = 20 \text{ mm}$.
2. The ratio is **20:10**.
3. Simplify: **2:1**.
