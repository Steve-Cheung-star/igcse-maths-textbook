## 1. Inquiry: The Balance of Change
**Inquiry Questions:** *If you double the number of workers on a job, does the time taken also double? Why do some relationships grow together while others pull apart?*

In mathematics, we look at how one variable affects another. 

### Direct Proportion
Two quantities are in **direct proportion** if they increase or decrease at the same rate. If you double one, you double the other.
* **Example:** The number of apples you buy and the total cost.
* **Graph:** A straight line passing through the origin (0,0).



### Inverse Proportion
Two quantities are in **inverse proportion** if an increase in one leads to a decrease in the other. If you double one, the other is halved.
* **Example:** The speed of a car and the time it takes to reach a destination.
* **Graph:** A curve that gets closer to the axes but never touches them (a hyperbola).



---

## 2. Solving Inverse Proportion
The key to inverse proportion is that the **product** of the two variables remains constant.
$$\text{Value A} \times \text{Value B} = \text{Constant}$$

**Example:**
It takes 3 workers 8 hours to paint a fence. How long would it take 4 workers?
1. Find the total "work hours" needed: $3 \times 8 = 24 \text{ hours}$.
2. Divide by the new number of workers: $24 \div 4 = 6 \text{ hours}$.

### Q2: Inverse Worker Problem
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If 5 people can pick a harvest in 12 days, how long would it take 6 people to pick the same harvest?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Total work days: $5 \times 12 = 60 \text{ days}$.
    2. Divide by new staff: $60 \div 6 = 10 \text{ days}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Speed and Time
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A journey takes 2 hours at a speed of 60 km/h. How long would the same journey take at a speed of 80 km/h?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Total distance: $2 \times 60 = 120 \text{ km}$.
    2. Divide by new speed: $120 \div 80 = 1.5 \text{ hours}$ (or 1 hour 30 mins).
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: The Constant Product
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    $x$ and $y$ are inversely proportional. When $x = 4$, $y = 10$. 
    
    Find $y$ when $x = 8$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Constant product: $4 \times 10 = 40$.
    2. $40 \div 8 = 5$.
    
    **Result: y = 5**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
