## 1. Inquiry: Angles in Parallel
**Inquiry Questions:** *What methods can be used to find angles on parallel lines? * *How can we prove two lines are parallel using only angle measurements? *

When a straight line (called a **transversal**) intersects two parallel lines, several pairs of equal angles are created. We identify these using specific names based on their position.



---

## 2. Key Angle Relationships
There are three main "rules" to remember when dealing with parallel lines:

### Corresponding Angles (The "F" Shape)
Angles in the same relative position at each intersection are equal.
* **Rule:** Corresponding angles are **equal**.



### Alternate Angles (The "Z" Shape)
Angles on opposite sides of the transversal, but inside the parallel lines.
* **Rule:** Alternate angles are **equal**.



### Co-interior Angles (The "C" or "U" Shape)
Angles on the same side of the transversal, inside the parallel lines.
* **Rule:** Co-interior angles add up to **180°** (supplementary).

## 3. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Identifying the Relationship
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If two angles form a "Z" shape between parallel lines, what is the mathematical name for this relationship? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    **Alternate angles.**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Solving for $x$
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A transversal crosses two parallel lines. An angle of 115° is identified. 
    
    a) What is the size of its **corresponding** angle?
    
    b) What is the size of its **co-interior** angle? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **115°** (Corresponding angles are equal).
    
    b) $180 - 115 = \mathbf{65°}$ (Co-interior angles sum to 180°).
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Complex Parallel Lines
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    In a diagram of parallel lines, an alternate angle is labeled as $2x$ and its pair is 70°. Solve for $x$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Since alternate angles are equal: $2x = 70$.
    2. Divide by 2: $\mathbf{x = 35}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
