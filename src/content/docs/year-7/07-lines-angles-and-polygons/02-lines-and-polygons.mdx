## 1. Inquiry: The Logic of Intersection
**Inquiry Questions:** *What methods can be used to find angles? How does geometry shape our world?* 

When lines cross or meet, they create predictable patterns:
* **Supplementary Angles:** Angles on a straight line always add up to **180°**.
* **Complementary Angles:** Angles that add up to **90°** (forming a right angle).
* **Vertically Opposite Angles:** When two lines intersect, the angles opposite each other are **equal**.



---

## 2. Properties of Quadrilaterals
A quadrilateral is any polygon with four sides. The sum of the interior angles of any quadrilateral is always **360°**.

| Shape | Key Properties  |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Square** | 4 equal sides; 4 right angles (90°). |
| **Rectangle** | Opposite sides equal; 4 right angles (90°). |
| **Parallelogram** | Opposite sides equal and parallel; opposite angles equal. |
| **Rhombus** | 4 equal sides; opposite angles equal. |
| **Trapezium** | At least one pair of parallel sides. |



---

## 3. Angle Relationships in Intersecting Lines
Using the "Straight Line Rule," we can find unknown values without a protractor.

**Example:**
Two lines intersect. One angle is 110°. What is the size of the angle next to it on the straight line?
1.  Total for a straight line = 180°.
2.  $180° - 110° = 70°$.
3.  **Result:** The adjacent angle is 70°.

### Q3: Quadrilateral Angle Sum
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A quadrilateral has angles of 90°, 90°, and 100°. What is the size of the fourth angle? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1.  Sum of angles = 360°.
    2.  $360 - (90 + 90 + 100) = 360 - 280$.
    3.  **Result:** $\mathbf{80°}$.
  </TabItem>
