## 1. Inquiry: Parts of a Whole
**Inquiry Questions:** * How can we represent the same value in different ways?
* Why is a common denominator necessary for comparing fractions?

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of a **numerator** (the number of parts we have) and a **denominator** (the total number of parts that make up the whole).

### Types of Fractions
* **Proper Fraction:** The numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g., $\frac{3}{4}$).
* **Improper Fraction:** The numerator is larger than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $\frac{5}{2}$).
* **Mixed Number:** A whole number combined with a proper fraction (e.g., $2\frac{1}{2}$).



---

## 2. Equivalent Fractions and Simplifying
Equivalent fractions have the same value even though they look different. You find them by multiplying or dividing both the numerator and denominator by the same number.

**Simplifying (Reducing)** a fraction means dividing both the top and bottom by their **Highest Common Factor (HCF)** until they cannot be divided further.



---

## 3. Comparing Fractions
To compare fractions like $\frac{2}{3}$ and $\frac{3}{4}$, you must find a **Common Denominator** using the **Lowest Common Multiple (LCM)** of the denominators.

**Example:**
To compare $\frac{2}{3}$ and $\frac{3}{4}$:
1. The LCM of 3 and 4 is 12.
2. $\frac{2}{3}$ becomes $\frac{8}{12}$ (multiply top and bottom by 4).
3. $\frac{3}{4}$ becomes $\frac{9}{12}$ (multiply top and bottom by 3).
4. Since 9 is greater than 8, $\frac{3}{4} > \frac{2}{3}$.
