## 1. Inquiry: The Common Unit
**Inquiry Question:** *Why can't we simply add the numerators and denominators together?*

If you have 1 half of a pizza and 1 quarter of a pizza, you cannot say you have 2 "sixths." To add or subtract fractions, they must be "like" terms—meaning they must have the same denominator.



---

## 2. Fractions with the Same Denominator
When the denominators are already the same, you simply add or subtract the **numerators** and keep the **denominator** the same.

**Example:**
$$\frac{3}{7} + \frac{2}{7} = \frac{5}{7}$$

---

## 3. Fractions with Different Denominators
If the denominators are different, follow these steps:
1.  Find the **Lowest Common Multiple (LCM)** of the denominators.
2.  Convert both fractions into **equivalent fractions** using that LCM as the new denominator.
3.  Add or subtract the numerators.
4.  Simplify the final answer if possible.

**Example: $\frac{1}{4} + \frac{2}{3}$**
1. LCM of 4 and 3 is 12.
2. $\frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{12}$ and $\frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{12}$.
3. $\frac{3}{12} + \frac{8}{12} = \frac{11}{12}$.

    b) 11/12 - 5/12

    c) 7/10

### Q2: Different Denominators (Addition)
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Calculate the following:
    
    a) 1/2 + 1/4
    
    b) 2/3 + 1/6
    
    c) 3/5 + 1/2
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 2/4 + 1/4 = 3/4
    
    b) 4/6 + 1/6 = 5/6
    
    c) 6/10 + 5/10 = 11/10 (or 1 and 1/10)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Different Denominators (Subtraction)
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Calculate the following:
    
    a) 3/4 - 1/2
    
    b) 5/6 - 1/3
    
    c) 7/8 - 1/4
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 3/4 - 2/4 = 1/4
    
    b) 5/6 - 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2
    
    c) 7/8 - 2/8 = 5/8
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: Multi-Step Problems
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A cake is cut into slices. James eats 1/8 of the cake, and Sarah eats 3/8.
    
    a) What fraction of the cake have they eaten altogether?
    
    b) What fraction of the cake is left?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 1/8 + 3/8 = 4/8 = 1/2
    
    b) 1 - 1/2 = 1/2
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
