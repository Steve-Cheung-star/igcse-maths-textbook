## 1. Inquiry: Three Names for One Value
**Inquiry Questions:** *Why is it sometimes easier to work with 0.5 than 1/2? When is a percentage more useful than a fraction?*

Imagine a pizza cut into four equal slices. If you eat one slice, you could say:
* I ate **1/4** of the pizza.
* I ate **0.25** of the pizza.
* I ate **25%** of the pizza.

All three represent the exact same amount. Being able to convert between them allows you to choose the easiest form for any calculation.



---

## 2. Conversion Methods

### Decimal to Percentage (and vice versa)
* **Decimal → Percentage:** Multiply by 100 (move the decimal point 2 places right).
  * *Example:* $0.45 = 45\%$
* **Percentage → Decimal:** Divide by 100 (move the decimal point 2 places left).
  * *Example:* $7\% = 0.07$

### Fraction to Decimal
Divide the numerator by the denominator.
* *Example:* For $3/5$, calculate $3 \div 5 = 0.6$.

### Fraction to Percentage
Convert the fraction so the denominator is 100, or convert to a decimal first and then multiply by 100.
* *Example:* $4/5 = 80/100 = 80\%$

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Completing the Set
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Convert these values into the other two forms:
    
    a) 0.35 (to Fraction and %)
    
    b) 4/5 (to Decimal and %)
    
    c) 12% (to Fraction and Decimal)
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 35/100 = 7/20 and 35%
    
    b) 0.8 and 80%
    
    c) 12/100 = 3/25 and 0.12
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Comparison Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Which value is the largest? 
    
    3/5, 0.62, or 58%
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Convert all to decimals:
    3/5 = 0.6
    0.62 = 0.62
    58% = 0.58
    
    Result: **0.62** is the largest.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
