## 1. Inquiry: The Power of the Point
**Inquiry Questions:** *How does a decimal point change the value of a digit? Why do we align the decimal points when adding or subtracting?*

Decimals are another way of writing fractions. The position of a digit relative to the decimal point determines its value.

### Decimal Place Value

* **Tenths ($0.1$):** 1 part of a whole divided into 10.
* **Hundredths ($0.01$):** 1 part of a whole divided into 100.
* **Thousandths ($0.001$):** 1 part of a whole divided into 1,000.

---

## 2. Operations with Decimals

### Addition and Subtraction
The golden rule is to **align the decimal points**. This ensures you are adding tenths to tenths and units to units.
* *Tip:* Use "placeholder" zeros to make the numbers the same length.
    * Example: $5.2 - 1.45$ becomes $5.20 - 1.45$.

### Multiplication
1.  Ignore the decimal points and multiply the numbers as if they were whole numbers.
2.  Count the total number of decimal places in the original numbers.
3.  Place the decimal point in your answer so it has that same number of decimal places.
    * Example: $0.2 \times 0.03$. (Whole numbers: $2 \times 3 = 6$). There are 3 decimal places total, so the answer is $0.006$.

### Division
When dividing by a whole number, keep the decimal point in the same position in the result.
* Example: $4.8 \div 2 = 2.4$.
