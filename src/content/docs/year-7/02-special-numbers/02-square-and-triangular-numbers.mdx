## 1. Inquiry: Numbers as Shapes
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we represent numbers visually using dots and grids?*

In mathematics, some numbers are named after the geometric shapes they form. When we "square" a number, we are calculating the number of units needed to fill a physical square.

### Square Numbers ($n^2$)
A **square number** is the result of multiplying an integer by itself.
* $1^2 = 1 \times 1 = 1$
* $2^2 = 2 \times 2 = 4$
* $3^2 = 3 \times 3 = 9$



### Triangular Numbers
A **triangular number** can be represented as an equilateral triangle of dots. You find the next one by adding the next row of dots.
* $T_1 = 1$
* $T_2 = 1 + 2 = 3$
* $T_3 = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6$
* $T_4 = 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 = 10$

## 2. The Inverse: Square Roots ($\sqrt{x}$)
If squaring a number is "growing" a square, the **square root** is finding the length of one side of that square.

<Aside type="note" title="Inverse Operations">
Squaring and Square Roots are **inverse operations**. They undo each other.
$$\sqrt{25} = 5 \text{ because } 5^2 = 25$$
</Aside>

    8, 16, 24, 49, 60, 81, 100, 125

### Q2: Triangular Sequences
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    The first five triangular numbers are 1, 3, 6, 10, and 15. 
    
    Find the next two triangular numbers in the sequence.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    To find the 6th triangular number, add 6 to the 5th number: $15 + 6 =$ 21
    
    To find the 7th triangular number, add 7 to the 6th number: $21 + 7 =$ 28
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Square Root Calculations
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Evaluate the following:
    
    a) $\sqrt{64}$
    
    b) $\sqrt{121}$
    
    c) $\sqrt{400}$
    
    d) $\sqrt{1}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 8 ($8 \times 8 = 64$)
    
    b) 11 ($11 \times 11 = 121$)
    
    c) 20 ($20 \times 20 = 400$)
    
    d) 1 ($1 \times 1 = 1$)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: Combined Operations
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Calculate the final value for the following:
    
    a) $3^2 + 4^2$
    
    b) $\sqrt{100 - 36}$
    
    c) $5^2 - \sqrt{25}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $9 + 16 =$ 25
    
    b) $\sqrt{64} =$ 8
    
    c) $25 - 5 =$ 20
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q5: The Pattern Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Investigate: What happens when you add two consecutive triangular numbers together?
    
    Example: 1 + 3 or 3 + 6 or 6 + 10.
    
    What kind of numbers do you always get?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $1 + 3 = 4$
    
    $3 + 6 = 9$
    
    $6 + 10 = 16$
    
    **Result:** Adding two consecutive triangular numbers always creates a Square Number.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
