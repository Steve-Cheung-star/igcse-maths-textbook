## 1. Inquiry: Mathematical Shorthand
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we represent very large numbers efficiently?*

When we multiply a number by itself multiple times, we use **Indices** (also called powers or exponents). This makes our calculations much cleaner and easier to read.

### The Parts of a Power
In the expression $5^3$:
* **5** is the **Base** (the number being multiplied).
* **3** is the **Index** or exponent (how many times the base appears).

$$5^3 = 5 \times 5 \times 5 = 125$$

---

## 2. Powers of 10
Powers of 10 are particularly important because they align with our decimal place value system. 



* $10^1 = 10$
* $10^2 = 10 \times 10 = 100$
* $10^3 = 10 \times 10 \times 10 = 1,000$
* $10^6 = 1,000,000$ (One Million)

<Aside type="note" title="The Zero Rule">
Any number raised to the power of 0 is equal to 1.
$$10^0 = 1$$
$$5^0 = 1$$
</Aside>

## 3. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Evaluating Powers
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Calculate the value of the following:
    
    a) $2^4$
    
    b) $3^3$
    
    c) $10^5$
    
    d) $7^2$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 =$ **16**
    
    b) $3 \times 3 \times 3 =$ **27**
    
    c) **100,000** (1 followed by 5 zeros)
    
    d) $7 \times 7 =$ **49**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Powers of 10
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Write the following numbers as a power of 10:
    
    a) 100
    
    b) 10,000
    
    c) 1,000,000
    
    d) 1
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **$10^2$**
    
    b) **$10^4$**
    
    c) **$10^6$**
    
    d) **$10^0$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Comparing Values
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Which value is larger?
    
    a) $2^3$ or $3^2$
    
    b) $5^2$ or $2^5$
    
    c) $10^3$ or $30^2$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **$3^2$** (since $9 > 8$)
    
    b) **$2^5$** (since $32 > 25$)
    
    c) **$10^3$** (since $1000 > 900$)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: Expanded Form
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Write these expressions in index form:
    
    a) $4 \times 4 \times 4 \times 4 \times 4$
    
    b) $9 \times 9$
    
    c) $10 \times 10 \times 10$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **$4^5$**
    
    b) **$9^2$**
    
    c) **$10^3$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q5: Thinking Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A square has a side length of $10^2$ cm. What is the area of the square? 
    
    Give your answer as a power of 10.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Area = Side $\times$ Side
    
    Area = $100 \times 100 = 10,000$
    
    Result = **$10^4$** $\text{cm}^2$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
