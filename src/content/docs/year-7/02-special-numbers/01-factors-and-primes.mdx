## 1. Inquiry: The Building Blocks of Numbers
**Inquiry Questions:** * How can number patterns help us learn more about numbers?
* Why is every number greater than 1 either a prime or made of primes?

### Key Vocabulary
* **Integer:** A whole number (e.g., -3, 0, 5).
* **Factor (Divisor):** A number that divides into another exactly. For example, 3 is a factor of 12.
* **Multiple:** The product of a number and any integer. For example, 10, 20, and 30 are multiples of 10.
* **Prime Number:** A number with exactly two factors: 1 and itself.
* **Composite Number:** A number with more than two factors.

---

## 2. Prime Factor Decomposition
Every **composite number** can be uniquely expressed as a product of prime factors. We call this "Prime Factor Decomposition."

**Example: Decompose 42**
To find the prime factors of 42, we break it down into smaller factors until we reach only prime numbers:
* $42 = 2 \times 21$
* $21 = 3 \times 7$
* So, $42 = 2 \times 3 \times 7$

<Aside type="note" title="The Factor Tree Strategy">
When building a factor tree, it doesn't matter which factors you start with; as long as you keep branching until you hit primes, the final "leaves" will always be the same.
</Aside>

---

## 3. HCF and LCM
Once we have the prime factors, we can solve complex problems involving shared numbers.

* **Highest Common Factor (HCF):** The largest number that is a factor of two or more numbers.
* **Lowest Common Multiple (LCM):** The smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.

**Example: 12 and 18**
* $12 = 2 \times 2 \times 3$
* $18 = 2 \times 3 \times 3$
* **HCF:** The common factors are one '2' and one '3'. $2 \times 3 = 6$.
* **LCM:** Every factor must be represented. $2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 = 36$.

### Q3: Prime Factor Trees
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Write the prime factorization (using index notation where possible) for:
    
    a) 24
    
    b) 100
    
    c) 72
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    **a) 24:** $2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 = 2^3 \times 3$
    
    **b) 100:** $2 \times 2 \times 5 \times 5 = 2^2 \times 5^2$
    
    **c) 72:** $2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 = 2^3 \times 3^2$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
