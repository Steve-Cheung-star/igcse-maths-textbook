## 1. Inquiry: The Number Line and Direction
**Inquiry Question:** *How do we represent values below zero, and how do signs change when we perform operations?*

Integers are whole numbers that can be positive, negative, or zero. To work with them effectively, we visualize them on a horizontal or vertical number line.



[Image of a horizontal number line with positive and negative integers]


### Rules for Addition and Subtraction
When you see two signs next to each other, you can simplify them into a single operation:
* **Same signs** become a **plus (+)**
  * $5 + (+3) = 8$
  * $5 - (-3) = 8$
* **Different signs** become a **minus (-)**
  * $5 + (-3) = 2$
  * $5 - (+3) = 2$

### Rules for Multiplication and Division
* If the signs are the **same**, the result is **positive**.
* If the signs are **different**, the result is **negative**.

## 2. Order of Operations (BIDMAS)
Mathematics is a universal language. To ensure everyone arrives at the same answer, we follow the **BIDMAS** hierarchy:

1. **B**rackets: Solve everything inside symbols like $(\quad)$ first.
2. **I**ndices: Solve powers ($2^3$) or roots ($\sqrt{16}$) next.
3. **D**ivision and **M**ultiplication: Solve these from left to right.
4. **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction: Solve these from left to right.



<Aside type="caution" title="Left to Right Rule">
Division does not always come before Multiplication. They have the same priority! If you see $12 \div 3 \times 2$, work from left to right: $4 \times 2 = 8$.
</Aside>

### Q5: The Sign Mystery
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Insert the correct operations ($+$, $-$, $\times$, or $\div$) to make this statement true:
    
    $6 \quad (-2) \quad 4 = -8$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $6 + (-2) \times 4$  
    Using BIDMAS: $6 + (-8) = -2$ (Incorrect)
    
    Let's try: $6 - (-2) + 4$ (Incorrect)
    
    **Correct answer:** $(6 + (-2)) \times (-2)$ ... wait, the numbers were 6, -2, and 4.
    
    **Actual Solution:** $6 \times (-2) + 4 = -12 + 4 = -8$.
  </TabItem>
