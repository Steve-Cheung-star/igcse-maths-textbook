## 1. Inquiry: The Logic of Layers
**Inquiry Question:** *How do we maintain accuracy when a problem has multiple layers of operations and signs?*

In advanced mathematics, expressions often look like a puzzle. The key is to peel back the layers one by one, starting from the "inside" and working out.

### Nested Brackets
When you see multiple sets of brackets, such as $[ \quad ]$ and $(\quad)$, always solve the **innermost** set first.

**Example:**
$10 - [ 5 + (2 \times -3) ]$
1. Solve innermost: $(2 \times -3) = -6$
2. Rewrite: $10 - [ 5 + (-6) ]$
3. Solve remaining bracket: $5 - 6 = -1$
4. Final step: $10 - (-1) = 10 + 1 = 11$

## 2. Negatives and Indices
A common trap involves squaring negative numbers. The position of the bracket changes everything.

* **$(-3)^2$** means $(-3) \times (-3)$. The result is **9**.
* **$-3^2$** means $-(3 \times 3)$. The result is **-9**.

<Aside type="tip" title="Even vs. Odd Powers">
* A negative base raised to an **even** power ($2, 4, 6...$) results in a **positive** answer.
* A negative base raised to an **odd** power ($3, 5, 7...$) results in a **negative** answer.
</Aside>

### Q1: Nested Brackets
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve the following:
    
    a) $20 + [ 10 - (2 \times 3) ]$
    
    b) $5 \times [ -2 + (12 \div 4) ]$
    
    c) $[ (15 + 5) \times -2 ] \div 10$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $20 + [10 - 6] = 20 + 4 = 24$
    
    b) $5 \times [-2 + 3] = 5 \times 1 = 5$
    
    c) $[20 \times -2] \div 10 = -40 \div 10 = -4$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: The "Mega" BIDMAS
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Combine everything you have learned to solve this expression:
    
    $(-4)^2 - [ 5 + (-2 \times 3) ]$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Calculate power: $(-4)^2 = 16$
    2. Solve inner bracket: $(-2 \times 3) = -6$
    3. Solve outer bracket: $[5 + (-6)] = -1$
    4. Final calculation: $16 - (-1) = 16 + 1 = 17$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
