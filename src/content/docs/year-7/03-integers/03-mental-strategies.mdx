## 1. Inquiry: Making Math Manageable
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we rearrange or simplify numbers to make calculations easier to perform mentally?*

Mathematicians don't always calculate from left to right. By understanding how numbers "behave," we can move them around or round them to find answers faster.

---

## 2. Rounding and Estimation
Rounding is the process of replacing a number with a simpler value that is approximately the same. 



* **Rounding to the nearest 10, 100, or 1000:** Look at the digit to the right of your target place value.
* **Estimation:** Rounding numbers *before* calculating to get a "ballpark" answer. 
  * *Example:* $29 \times 31$ is approximately $30 \times 30 = 900$.

---

## 3. Properties of Arithmetic
These three properties are the "rules of movement" for numbers.

### Commutative Property (The "Swap" Rule)
The order of the numbers does not change the result. This works for **Addition** and **Multiplication**.
* $5 + 3 = 3 + 5$
* $4 \times 7 = 7 \times 4$

### Associative Property (The "Grouping" Rule)
How you group numbers in brackets doesn't change the result.
* $(2 + 8) + 5 = 2 + (8 + 5)$
* $(3 \times 2) \times 5 = 3 \times (2 \times 5)$

### Distributive Property (The "Break-Apart" Rule)
Multiplying a sum by a number is the same as multiplying each addend individually.
* $3 \times (10 + 2) = (3 \times 10) + (3 \times 2) = 36$

### Q3: Using the Commutative & Associative Properties
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Rearrange these numbers to make the mental calculation easier:
    
    a) $25 + 17 + 75$
    
    b) $4 \times 13 \times 25$
    
    c) $2 \times 9 \times 50$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $(25 + 75) + 17 = 100 + 17 = 117$
    
    b) $(4 \times 25) \times 13 = 100 \times 13 = 1300$
    
    c) $(2 \times 50) \times 9 = 100 \times 9 = 900$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: The Distributive Property
<Tabs>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
