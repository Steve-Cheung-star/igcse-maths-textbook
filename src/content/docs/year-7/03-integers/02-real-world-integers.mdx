## 1. Inquiry: Integers in Action
**Inquiry Question:** *How do negative numbers help us describe the world around us?*

In the previous lesson, we learned the rules for calculating with integers. Now, we look at how these numbers appear in everyday life. Understanding "directed numbers" allows us to track changes that go below a starting point or zero.

### Common Real-World Scenarios
* **Temperature:** Degrees above or below freezing ($0^\circ\text{C}$).
* **Money:** Having a balance in the bank (positive) vs. owing money or an "overdraft" (negative).
* **Elevation:** Height above sea level (positive) vs. depth below sea level (negative).



---

## 2. Calculating the "Difference"
When finding the difference between a positive and a negative number, we are calculating the total distance between them on a number line.

**Example: Temperature Change**
If the temperature is $-5^\circ\text{C}$ and it rises to $12^\circ\text{C}$, what is the total increase?
1. From $-5$ to $0$ is **5 units**.
2. From $0$ to $12$ is **12 units**.
3. Total increase: $5 + 12 = 17^\circ\text{C}$.

Mathematically, this is expressed as: $12 - (-5) = 12 + 5 = 17$.

## 3. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Temperature Gauges
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Find the new temperature for each scenario:
    
    a) Starts at 4°C and falls by 9 degrees.
    
    b) Starts at -10°C and rises by 15 degrees.
    
    c) Starts at -2°C and falls by 7 degrees.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 4 - 9 = -5°C
    
    b) -10 + 15 = 5°C
    
    c) -2 - 7 = -9°C
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Sea Level Elevation
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Calculate the difference in height between the following:
    
    a) A bird flying 20m above sea level and a fish swimming 5m below sea level.
    
    b) A mountain peak at 1200m and a valley floor at -150m.
    
    c) A diver at -30m and a shark at -12m.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 20 - (-5) = 25m
    
    b) 1200 - (-150) = 1350m
    
    c) -12 - (-30) = 18m (The shark is 18m higher than the diver)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Bank Balances
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve the following financial problems:
    
    a) Sarah has 40 dollars. She spends 55 dollars. What is her new balance?
    
    b) Mark's bank account is at -20 dollars. He deposits 100 dollars. What is his new balance?
    
    c) A company lost 5000 dollars in January and lost another 2500 dollars in February. What is the total loss?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) 40 - 55 = -15 dollars
    
    b) -20 + 100 = 80 dollars
    
    c) -5000 + (-2500) = -7500 (Total loss of 7500 dollars)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q4: The Elevator Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    An elevator starts on the 10th floor. It goes down 12 floors, then up 5 floors, then down 7 floors. What floor is the elevator on now?
    
    (Note: Use 0 for the Ground Floor and negative numbers for the basement).
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    10 - 12 + 5 - 7
    
    -2 + 5 - 7
    
    3 - 7 = -4
    
    The elevator is on **Basement Level 4**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
