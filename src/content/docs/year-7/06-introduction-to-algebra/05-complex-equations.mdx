## 1. Inquiry: Simplifying Before Solving
**Inquiry Question:** *What is the best way to solve linear equations that look messy?* 

Sometimes, an equation isn't ready to be solved immediately. If you see brackets, your first job is to "expand" them to see the individual terms clearly. 



---

## 2. Equations with Brackets
To solve these, apply the **Distributive Law** first, then use your inverse operations. 

**Example:** Solve $3(x + 4) = 21$
1. **Expand:** $3x + 12 = 21$ 
2. **Subtract 12:** $3x = 9$ 
3. **Divide by 3:** $x = 3$ 

---

## 3. Unknowns on Both Sides (Extension)
Your outline includes an extension for equations where the variable appears on both sides of the equals sign.  The goal is to "collect" all the variables on one side and all the numbers on the other.

**Example:** Solve $5x - 4 = 3x + 6$
1. **Move the smaller $x$ term:** Subtract $3x$ from both sides $\rightarrow 2x - 4 = 6$ 
2. **Move the constant:** Add 4 to both sides $\rightarrow 2x = 10$ 
3. **Isolate $x$:** Divide by 2 $\rightarrow x = 5$

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Solving with Brackets
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve the following equations:
    
    a) $2(x + 5) = 16$
    
    b) $4(y - 3) = 8$
    
    c) $5(2 + n) = 30$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $2x + 10 = 16 \rightarrow 2x = 6 \rightarrow \mathbf{x = 3}$
    
    b) $4y - 12 = 8 \rightarrow 4y = 20 \rightarrow \mathbf{y = 5}$
    
    c) $10 + 5n = 30 \rightarrow 5n = 20 \rightarrow \mathbf{n = 4}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Variables on Both Sides
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve these extension problems:
    
    a) $4x + 2 = 2x + 10$
    
    b) $7y - 5 = 3y + 11$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) Subtract $2x$: $2x + 2 = 10 \rightarrow 2x = 8 \rightarrow \mathbf{x = 4}$
    
    b) Subtract $3y$: $4y - 5 = 11 \rightarrow 4y = 16 \rightarrow \mathbf{y = 4}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: The Ultimate Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve $2(3x - 1) = 10$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Expand: $6x - 2 = 10$
    2. Add 2: $6x = 12$
    3. Divide by 6: $\mathbf{x = 2}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
