## 1. Inquiry: What is a Variable?
**Inquiry Question:** *How can algebra be used to represent real-life situations?*  *What is a language?* 

In mathematics, a **variable** is a symbol (usually a letter) used to represent a value that we don't know yet or a value that can change. 

### The Vocabulary of Algebra
* **Variable:** A letter (like $x$ or $n$) representing a quantity.
* **Coefficient:** The number multiplied by a variable (e.g., in $3x$, **3** is the coefficient).
* **Expression:** A mathematical phrase involving numbers, variables, and operations (e.g., $3x + 1$).
* **Equation:** A statement that two expressions are equal (e.g., $3x + 1 = 10$).



---

## 2. Translating Phrases into Algebra
Algebra allows us to translate English phrases into mathematical shorthand.

| English Phrase | Algebraic Expression |
| :--- | :--- |
| "A number $n$ plus 5" | $n + 5$  |
| "Three times a number" | $3x$  |
| "One more than three times a number" | $3x + 1$  |
| "The difference between $y$ and 10" | $y - 10$ |

---

## 3. Substitution
One of the most important skills is **evaluating** an expression. This means replacing the variable with a specific "natural number" to find the total value.

**Example:**
Evaluate $3x + 1$ if $x = 4$.
1. Replace $x$ with 4: $3(4) + 1$
2. Multiply: $12 + 1$
3. **Result:** 13
