## 1. Inquiry: What are Like Terms?
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we make a long mathematical sentence shorter and easier to read?* 

In algebra, **like terms** are terms that have the exact same variable (letter) and exponent. We can only add or subtract terms if they are "like" each other.

* **Like Terms:** $3x$ and $5x$ (Both have $x$), or $2y^2$ and $7y^2$.
* **Unlike Terms:** $4x$ and $4y$ (Different variables), or $5$ and $5x$ (One is a constant, one has a variable).



---

## 2. Collecting Like Terms
To simplify an expression, we group the coefficients of the like terms together. 

**Example 1:**
Simplify $3x + 5 + 2x - 1$.
1. Group the $x$ terms: $3x + 2x = 5x$.
2. Group the constants: $5 - 1 = 4$.
3. **Result:** $5x + 4$.

**Example 2:**
Simplify $4a + 2b - a + 3b$.
1. Group the $a$ terms: $4a - a = 3a$.
2. Group the $b$ terms: $2b + 3b = 5b$.
3. **Result:** $3a + 5b$.

---

## 3. Expanding Brackets (Distributive Law)
Your outline requires learning how to multiply a single term over a bracket.  This is often called the **Distributive Law** because you "distribute" the outside number to everything inside.

**The Rule:** $a(b + c) = ab + ac$

**Example:**
Simplify $3(x + 4)$.
1. Multiply 3 by $x$: $3x$.
2. Multiply 3 by 4: $+12$.
3. **Result:** $3x + 12$.
