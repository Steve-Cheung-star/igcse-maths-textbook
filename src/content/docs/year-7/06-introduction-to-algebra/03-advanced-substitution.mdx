## 1. Inquiry: Precision in Algebra
**Inquiry Question:** *How do our rules for negative numbers and fractions change the value of an expression?* 

In real-world science and engineering, variables are rarely nice whole numbers. To "evaluate" an expression means to find its total numerical value by replacing letters with given numbers.



---

## 2. Handling Different Number Types
When substituting, always place the number in **brackets** to keep signs and operations clear, especially with negatives and exponents.

### Substituting Integers (Negatives)
Remember: A positive times a negative is a negative.
* **Example:** Evaluate $5x - 2$ when $x = -3$.
* $5(-3) - 2$
* $-15 - 2 = -17$

### Substituting Decimals and Fractions
* **Example:** Evaluate $4y + 10$ when $y = 0.5$.
* $4(0.5) + 10$
* $2 + 10 = 12$

---

## 3. Complex Expressions
Your outline includes evaluating expressions with multiple variables.

**Example:** Evaluate $3x + 4y - 2z$ where $x = \frac{1}{2}$, $y = 0.6$, and $z = -1$.
1. **Substitute:** $3(\frac{1}{2}) + 4(0.6) - 2(-1)$
2. **Calculate:** $1.5 + 2.4 + 2$
3. **Result:** $5.9$

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Integer Substitution
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Evaluate these expressions for $n = -4$:
    
    a) $n + 10$
    
    b) $3n$
    
    c) $n^2$ (Hint: negative $\times$ negative) 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $-4 + 10 = 6$
    
    b) $3 \times (-4) = -12$
    
    c) $(-4) \times (-4) = 16$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Decimal and Fraction Substitution
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Evaluate the following:
    
    a) $10x$ when $x = 0.25$
    
    b) $\frac{1}{2}y$ when $y = 20$
    
    c) $4a + 1$ when $a = \frac{3}{4}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $10 \times 0.25 = 2.5$
    
    b) $0.5 \times 20 = 10$
    
    c) $4(\frac{3}{4}) + 1 = 3 + 1 = 4$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Multi-Variable Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Evaluate $2a - b + 5c$ where $a = 3$, $b = -2$, and $c = 0.4$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. $2(3) - (-2) + 5(0.4)$
    2. $6 + 2 + 2$
    3. **Result:** 10
  </TabItem>
