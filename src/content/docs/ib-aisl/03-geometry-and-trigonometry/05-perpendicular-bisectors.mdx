In geometry, a **perpendicular bisector** is a line that cuts a line segment into two equal halves at a $90^\circ$ angle. This concept is vital in Voronoi diagrams (which you will study in Chapter 3.6), urban planning, and coordinate geometry.

To find the equation of a perpendicular bisector, you must combine your knowledge of **midpoints**, **gradients**, and **linear equations**.

---

## 1. The Anatomy of a Perpendicular Bisector

A perpendicular bisector must satisfy two strict geometric conditions:
1. **It must be Perpendicular:** The gradient of the bisector ($m_{\perp}$) must be the negative reciprocal of the gradient of the original line segment ($m$).
2. **It must Bisect:** The line must pass through the **midpoint** of the original line segment.

## 2. The Three-Step Process

To find the equation of the perpendicular bisector between two points $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$, follow these steps:

<Steps>
1. **Find the Midpoint ($M$):**
   Use the midpoint formula to find the point the bisector passes through.
   $$M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right)$$

2. **Find the Perpendicular Gradient ($m_{\perp}$):**
   * First, find the gradient of the segment $AB$: $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$
   * Then, find the perpendicular gradient: $m_{\perp} = -\frac{1}{m}$

3. **Construct the Equation:**
   Use the point-gradient form $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ with the midpoint $M$ and the perpendicular gradient $m_{\perp}$.
</Steps>

<Aside type="caution" title="Parallel and Perpendicular Gradients">
Recall from Section 2.1: 
* If two lines are parallel, $m_1 = m_2$.
* If two lines are perpendicular, $m_1 \times m_2 = -1$.
</Aside>

## Practice Questions

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Given Two Points">
    Points $A$ and $B$ have coordinates $(2, 5)$ and $(8, -3)$ respectively.
    Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of the line segment $AB$. Give your answer in the form $ax + by + d = 0$ where $a, b, d \in \mathbb{Z}$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    <Steps>
    1. **Find the Midpoint ($M$)**
       $$M = \left( \frac{2 + 8}{2}, \frac{5 + (-3)}{2} \right) = (5, 1)$$

    2. **Find the Gradient of $AB$ ($m$)**
       $$m = \frac{-3 - 5}{8 - 2} = \frac{-8}{6} = -\frac{4}{3}$$

    3. **Find the Perpendicular Gradient ($m_{\perp}$)**
       $$m_{\perp} = \frac{3}{4}$$

    4. **Form the Equation**
       Using $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ with $(5, 1)$:
       $$y - 1 = \frac{3}{4}(x - 5)$$
       $$4(y - 1) = 3(x - 5)$$
       $$4y - 4 = 3x - 15$$
       $$3x - 4y - 11 = 0$$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Given Midpoint and Gradient">
    A line segment $PQ$ has a midpoint $M(4, 2)$. The line segment itself has the equation $y = 2x - 6$.
    Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of $PQ$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    <Steps>
    1. **Identify the Gradient ($m$)**
       From $y = 2x - 6$, the gradient $m = 2$.

    2. **Find the Perpendicular Gradient ($m_{\perp}$)**
       $$m_{\perp} = -\frac{1}{2}$$

    3. **Form the Equation**
       We already have the midpoint $(4, 2)$ and the perpendicular gradient $-\frac{1}{2}$:
       $$y - 2 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - 4)$$
       $$y - 2 = -0.5x + 2$$
       $$y = -0.5x + 4$$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

    Two cell towers are located at $T_1(0, 0)$ and $T_2(6, 4)$ on a coordinate map where units are in kilometres. A boundary line is to be drawn such that any point on the line is equidistant from both towers.
    
    a) Find the equation of this boundary line.
    
    b) A road follows the line $y = x + 1$. Find the coordinates of the point on the road that is equidistant from both towers.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    **Part a)**
    The line equidistant from two points is their perpendicular bisector.
    * **Midpoint:** $\left(\frac{0+6}{2}, \frac{0+4}{2}\right) = (3, 2)$
    * **Gradient of $T_1T_2$:** $\frac{4-0}{6-0} = \frac{2}{3}$
    * **Perpendicular Gradient:** $-\frac{3}{2}$
    * **Equation:** $y - 2 = -\frac{3}{2}(x - 3) \Rightarrow y = -1.5x + 6.5$

    **Part b)**
    We find the intersection of the road ($y = x + 1$) and the boundary ($y = -1.5x + 6.5$):
    $$x + 1 = -1.5x + 6.5$$
    $$2.5x = 5.5$$
    $$x = 2.2$$
    Substitute $x$ into $y = x + 1$:
    $$y = 2.2 + 1 = 3.2$$
    The point is $(2.2, 3.2)$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
