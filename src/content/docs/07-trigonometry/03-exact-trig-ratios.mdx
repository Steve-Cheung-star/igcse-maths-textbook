## 2. Rationalised vs. Unrationalised Forms

You will often see two different versions of the same exact value, depending on the textbook or marking scheme. 

Mathematically, it is traditionally considered "messy" to leave a surd (square root) in the denominator of a fraction. To fix this, we **rationalise the denominator** by multiplying the top and bottom by that surd.

* **For 45ยฐ:** $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \times \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}} = \mathbf{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}}$
* **For 30ยฐ:** $\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}} = \mathbf{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}}$

<Aside type="tip" title="Which one should you use?">
For Cambridge IGCSE, **both forms are completely acceptable** and will score full marks unless the question specifically says "Give your answer with a rational denominator." Don't let the different formats confuse youโthey are exactly the same number!
</Aside>

## 3. The "0-1-2-3-4" Table Generation Trick

If you struggle with visualizing triangles, use this foolproof trick to generate the entire table from scratch the moment your exam starts. 

<Steps>
1. **Write the angles:** 0ยฐ, 30ยฐ, 45ยฐ, 60ยฐ, 90ยฐ.
2. **Write the numbers 0 to 4:** Write 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 under the angles.
3. **Square root them all:** $\sqrt{0}, \sqrt{1}, \sqrt{2}, \sqrt{3}, \sqrt{4}$.
4. **Divide them all by 2:** This gives you the entire **Sine** row! (Simplify where possible: $\sqrt{4}/2 = 2/2 = 1$).
5. **Reverse the row for Cosine:** Just write the Sine row backwards.
6. **Divide Sin by Cos for Tangent:** Because $\tan(\theta) = \frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)}$.
</Steps>

### The Master Table of Exact Values

| Angle ($\theta$) | $0^\circ$ | $30^\circ$ | $45^\circ$ | $60^\circ$ | $90^\circ$ |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **$\sin(\theta)$** | $0$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ *(or $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$)* | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $1$ |
| **$\cos(\theta)$** | $1$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ *(or $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$)* | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $0$ |
| **$\tan(\theta)$** | $0$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}$ *(or $\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}$)* | $1$ | $\sqrt{3}$ | **Undefined** |

*(Note: $\tan(90^\circ)$ is undefined because dividing by zero ($\frac{1}{0}$) is mathematically impossible).*

## 4. The Viral "Finger Method"



Youโve likely seen this on social media. While it is purely a memorization gimmick and doesn't teach you *why* the math works, it is undeniably useful for some students in a high-pressure exam scenario.

1. Hold out your **left hand**, palm facing you. 
2. Assign angles to your fingers starting from the pinky: Pinky = 0ยฐ, Ring = 30ยฐ, Middle = 45ยฐ, Index = 60ยฐ, Thumb = 90ยฐ.
3. Put down the finger for the angle you want.
4. **For Sine:** Count the fingers **BELOW** the folded finger. Put that number in a square root and divide by 2. $\frac{\sqrt{\text{bottom fingers}}}{2}$
5. **For Cosine:** Count the fingers **ABOVE** the folded finger. Put that number in a square root and divide by 2. $\frac{\sqrt{\text{top fingers}}}{2}$
6. **For Tangent:** Put the square root of bottom fingers over the square root of top fingers. $\frac{\sqrt{\text{bottom fingers}}}{\sqrt{\text{top fingers}}}$

*Example for sin(60ยฐ): Put down your index finger. There are 3 fingers below it. So, $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.*
