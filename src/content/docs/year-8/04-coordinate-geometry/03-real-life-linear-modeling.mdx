## 1. Inquiry: Modeling the World
**Inquiry Questions:** *How do equations and graphs help us solve problems? How do we model relations in real-life?* 

In mathematics, we use **Linear Modeling** to describe relationships where one thing changes at a constant rate relative to another. For example, if you walk at a steady speed of 5 km/h, your distance increases by exactly 5 km every hour. 

---

## 2. Distance-Time Graphs
A common application of linear modeling is the **Distance-Time Graph**. 
* **The x-axis** represents time. 
* **The y-axis** represents distance. 
* **The Gradient (Slope):** In these graphs, the gradient represents the **speed**.  A steeper line means a faster constant speed. 



---

## 3. Modeling with Equations
We can represent these relationships using algebraic equations where variables represent changing quantities. 

**Scenario:** A taxi charges a $0$ initial fee (starting at the origin) and then $\$2$ per kilometer. 
* **Equation:** $y = 2x$ (where $y$ is cost and $x$ is distance). 
* **Initial Condition:** Since the fee starts at $0$, the graph passes through $(0,0)$. 

| Distance ($x$) | $0$ km | $1$ km | $2$ km | $3$ km |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cost ($y$)** | $\$0$ | $\$2$ | $\$4$ | $\$6$ |

## 4. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Interpreting the Gradient
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    On a distance-time graph, Person A has a line with a gradient of $5$, and Person B has a line with a gradient of $8$. Who is moving faster? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    **Person B** is moving faster because the gradient represents speed, and a higher gradient indicates a faster rate. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Building the Model
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A phone plan costs $\$0.50$ per minute of calling time. 
    a) Write an equation to represent the cost ($y$) for $x$ minutes. 
    b) What is the cost for $40$ minutes?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **$y = 0.5x$** 
    b) $0.5 \times 40 = \mathbf{\$20}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Analyzing the Graph
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If a distance-time graph shows a perfectly horizontal line (gradient of $0$), what does this tell you about the object's movement? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The object is **stationary** (not moving). The distance is not changing as time passes. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
