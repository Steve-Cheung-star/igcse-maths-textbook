## 1. Inquiry: Is Probability Predictable?
**Inquiry Questions:** *Is probability predictable?  Can we make better decisions by understanding probability? *

Probability is the study of chance. We use a **Probability Scale** to represent the likelihood of an event occurring, ranging from **0 (Impossible)** to **1 (Certain)**.



### Essential Vocabulary
* **Event:** An outcome or set of outcomes (e.g., rolling a 6).
* **Fair:** An experiment where all outcomes have an equal chance of happening.
* **Random:** When each outcome is equally likely and cannot be predicted with certainty.

---

## 2. Calculating Theoretical Probability
Theoretical probability is what we *expect* to happen based on math. It is expressed as a ratio of favorable outcomes to the total number of possible outcomes.

**The Formula:**
$$P(\text{event}) = \frac{\text{Number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{Total number of possible outcomes}}$$ 

**Example: Rolling a Die**
What is the probability of rolling an odd number (1, 3, or 5) on a six-sided die?
* **Favorable outcomes:** 3 (the numbers 1, 3, 5).
* **Total outcomes:** 6 (the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6).
* **Calculation:** $P(\text{odd}) = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$.

---

## 3. Real-World Applications
Probabilities aren't just for dice; they appear in many forms:
* **Fractions:** $\frac{1}{4}$ chance of rain.
* **Decimals:** $0.25$ batting average in baseball.
* **Percentages:** $25\%$ chance of winning a lottery.

### Q2: Calculating Outcomes
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A bag contains 4 red marbles, 3 blue marbles, and 3 green marbles. If you pick one at random, what is the probability it is **blue**? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Total marbles: $4 + 3 + 3 = 10$.
    2. Favorable (blue): 3.
    3. **Result:** $P(\text{blue}) = \frac{3}{10}$ or **0.3** or **30%**. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Predicting Frequency
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If the probability of a spinner landing on "Win" is $\frac{1}{5}$, how many times would you expect to win if you spin it 50 times? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Multiply probability by number of trials: $\frac{1}{5} \times 50$.
    2. **Result:** You would expect to win **10 times**. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
