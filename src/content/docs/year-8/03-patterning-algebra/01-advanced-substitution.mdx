## 1. Inquiry: The Universal Language
**Inquiry Questions:** *Why do we use letters to represent numbers? How does the value of an entire expression change when the variable is a fraction or a negative number?*

In algebra, a **variable** is a placeholder. Think of it like a "storage box" where you can place any number. In Year 8, we move beyond simple whole numbers and start "plugging in" decimals and fractions.

---

## 2. The Power of Brackets
The most important habit to develop in Year 8 is using **brackets** whenever you substitute a value. This prevents "sign errors," especially when dealing with negative numbers or exponents.



### Example 1: Substituting Decimals
Evaluate $5x - 2.4$ when $x = 1.5$:
1. **Substitute with brackets:** $5(1.5) - 2.4$
2. **Multiply:** $7.5 - 2.4$
3. **Solve:** $5.1$

### Example 2: Substituting Fractions
Evaluate $8y + 1$ when $y = \frac{3}{4}$:
1. **Substitute with brackets:** $8(\frac{3}{4}) + 1$
2. **Multiply:** (Remember, $8 \times \frac{3}{4}$ is the same as $8 \div 4 \times 3$)
   * $2 \times 3 = 6$
3. **Solve:** $6 + 1 = 7$

---

## 3. Substitution with Squares and Negatives
When a variable is squared, the brackets become essential. 

**Scenario:** If $n = -4$, evaluate $n^2 + 5$.
* **Correct:** $(-4)^2 + 5 = 16 + 5 = \mathbf{21}$
* **Common Error:** $-4^2 + 5 = -16 + 5 = -11$ (Without brackets, the calculator only squares the 4, not the negative sign!)

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Decimal Substitution
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Evaluate $0.2a + 5$ for the following values of $a$:
    
    a) $a = 10$
    
    b) $a = 0.5$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $0.2(10) + 5 = 2 + 5 = \mathbf{7}$
    
    b) $0.2(0.5) + 5 = 0.1 + 5 = \mathbf{5.1}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Fraction Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If $x = \frac{2}{3}$, evaluate the expression $9x - 4$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. $9(\frac{2}{3}) - 4$
    2. $6 - 4$ (because $9 \div 3 = 3$, and $3 \times 2 = 6$)
    3. **Result:** $\mathbf{2}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
