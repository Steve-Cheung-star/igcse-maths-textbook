## 1. Inquiry: Breaking the Barrier
**Inquiry Questions:** *Why must we multiply the term outside the bracket by every term inside? How does a negative sign outside a bracket change the entire equation?*

Sometimes, a variable is "locked" inside a set of brackets, like in the equation $3(x + 4) = 15$. To solve for $x$, we first need to remove those brackets using the **Distributive Law**.



---

## 2. The Distributive Law
When you see a number directly outside a bracket, it means **multiplication**. You must multiply that number by every single term inside.

**Example: Expanding and Solving**
Solve $2(x - 5) = 14$:
1. **Expand:** Multiply $2$ by $x$ and $2$ by $-5$.
   * $2x - 10 = 14$
2. **Isolate:** Add $10$ to both sides.
   * $2x = 24$
3. **Solve:** Divide by $2$.
   * **Result:** $x = 12$

---

## 3. Handling Negatives
The most common mistake in Year 8 is forgetting that a negative outside the bracket flips the signs inside.

**Example:** $-3(x - 4)$
* $-3 \times x = -3x$
* $-3 \times -4 = +12$ (Negative $\times$ Negative = Positive)
* **Expanded form:** $-3x + 12$

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve for $y$: $3(y + 2) = 21$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Expand: $3y + 6 = 21$
    2. Subtract 6: $3y = 15$
    3. Divide by 3: **$y = 5$**
  </TabItem>

    1. Expand: $-2x + 6 = 10$
    2. Subtract 6: $-2x = 4$
    3. Divide by -2: **$x = -2$**
