## 1. Inquiry: Balancing the Scales
**Inquiry Questions:** *If $2x + 5 = x + 10$, how can we find the value of $x$? Why is it usually easier to move the smaller variable first?*

In previous lessons, the letter $x$ was always on one side. Now, we see variables on both sides. To solve these, we must treat the equals sign like a **balance scale**. Whatever we do to one side to "get rid" of a variable, we must do to the other.



---

## 2. The "Smaller Variable" Strategy
To keep our numbers positive and easy to manage, we follow these steps:

1.  **Identify** which side has fewer variables (the "smaller" $x$).
2.  **Eliminate** that variable from its side by adding or subtracting it.
3.  **Balance** the equation by doing the exact same thing to the other side.
4.  **Solve** the remaining two-step equation.

**Example: Solving $5x + 3 = 2x + 12$**
1.  **Compare:** $2x$ is smaller than $5x$.
2.  **Eliminate $2x$:** Subtract $2x$ from both sides.
    * $(5x - 2x) + 3 = (2x - 2x) + 12$
    * $3x + 3 = 12$
3.  **Isolate:** Subtract 3 from both sides.
    * $3x = 9$
4.  **Solve:** Divide by 3.
    * **Result:** $x = 3$

---

## 3. Equations with Brackets AND Both Sides
Sometimes, you have to combine your skills from Lesson 3.2 and 3.3.
* **Step 1:** Expand any brackets.
* **Step 2:** Collect the variables on one side.
* **Step 3:** Solve for $x$.

**Example:** $2(x + 4) = x + 10$
* **Expand:** $2x + 8 = x + 10$
* **Subtract $x$:** $x + 8 = 10$
* **Subtract 8:** **$x = 2$**

### Q2: Negatives on Both Sides
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve for $y$: $2y + 8 = 5y - 7$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Subtract $2y$ (it's smaller): $8 = 3y - 7$
    2. Add 7: $15 = 3y$
    3. Divide by 3: **$y = 5$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: The Multi-Step Challenge
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve: $3(x - 2) = x + 4$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Expand: $3x - 6 = x + 4$
    2. Subtract $x$: $2x - 6 = 4$
    3. Add 6: $2x = 10$
    4. **$x = 5$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
