## 1. Inquiry: The Science of Scaling
**Inquiry Questions:** *Why is a scale drawing or a model useful? Why are all circles similar? How do designers use similarity to create logos or architectural models?*

An **Enlargement** is a transformation that changes the size of a shape but preserves its proportions and angles. If two shapes have the same angles and their side lengths are in the same ratio, we call them **Mathematically Similar**.

---

## 2. Scale Factors
The **Scale Factor ($k$)** tells us how many times bigger or smaller the new image is compared to the original object.

* **If $k > 1$:** The shape gets larger (Enlargement).
* **If $0 < k < 1$:** The shape gets smaller (Reduction).
* **If $k = 1$:** The shape stays the same size.



**To find the Scale Factor:**
$$\text{Scale Factor} = \frac{\text{Image length}}{\text{Object length}}$$

---

## 3. Investigating Similarity
Two shapes are similar if:
1.  All corresponding **angles** are equal.
2.  All corresponding **sides** are in the same ratio.

**Special Case: Circles**
Every circle in the universe is similar to every other circle. This is because they are all defined by the same ratio ($\pi$), and their "shape" never changes, only their size (radius).

## 4. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Calculating Scale Factor
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A photograph is $10\text{cm}$ wide. It is enlarged so that the new width is $25\text{cm}$. What is the scale factor?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $\text{Scale Factor} = 25 \div 10 = \mathbf{2.5}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Finding Missing Sides
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Triangle A has a base of $4\text{cm}$. Triangle B is similar to A and has a scale factor of $3$. What is the base of Triangle B?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $4\text{cm} \times 3 = \mathbf{12\text{cm}}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Similarity Logic
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If a square with sides of $5\text{cm}$ is enlarged by a scale factor of $2$, what happens to its internal angles?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The angles **stay the same** ($90^\circ$). Enlargements change the lengths of sides, but the angles must remain equal for the shapes to be similar.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Extension Idea (Topic 7)
**Negative Scale Factors:** In advanced geometry, a scale factor of $-1$ doesn't just change the size; it performs a rotation/reflection that flips the shape through the center of enlargement!
