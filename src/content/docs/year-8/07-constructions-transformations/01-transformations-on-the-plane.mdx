## 1. Inquiry: Why is Geometry Beautiful?
**Inquiry Questions:** *Why is geometry beautiful? How do designers use mathematics to construct, design, and create innovative ideas?* 

Transformations are operations that move or change a geometric figure (the **object**) to produce a new figure (the **image**). These movements are the foundation of computer animation, video game design, and architecture.

---

## 2. Three Types of Movement
In Year 8, we focus on three ways to move a shape without changing its size or shape (congruent transformations):

### Translation (The Slide)
A translation moves every point of a figure the same distance in the same direction.
* **Specified by:** A distance and a direction.
* **On a graph:** "Move 3 units right and 2 units down."

### Reflection (The Flip)
A reflection creates a mirror image of a shape across a line.
* **Specified by:** A mirror line, such as the x-axis, the y-axis, or lines like $y = x$.



### Rotation (The Turn)
A rotation turns a figure about a fixed point called the center of rotation.
* **Specified by:** A center point and an angle of rotation (usually $90^\circ$, $180^\circ$, or $270^\circ$ anticlockwise).
* **Year 8 Skill:** You will investigate rotations where the center is **outside** the shape.



---

## 3. Transformations on the Cartesian Plane
We use coordinates to track how a point $(x, y)$ changes after a transformation:
* **Reflection in x-axis:** $(x, y) \rightarrow (x, -y)$ 
* **Reflection in y-axis:** $(x, y) \rightarrow (-x, y)$ 
* **Rotation of $180^\circ$ about origin:** $(x, y) \rightarrow (-x, -y)$

## 4. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Identifying Movements
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Identify which transformation is occurring in these real-world examples:
    a) An elevator moving from floor 1 to floor 5.
    b) Looking at your image in a still lake.
    c) The hands of a clock moving.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **Translation** (sliding up).
    b) **Reflection** (mirror image).
    c) **Rotation** (turning around a center).
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Reflecting Points
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If the point $A(3, 5)$ is reflected in the **x-axis**, what are the coordinates of its image, $A'$? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    When reflecting in the x-axis, the x-value stays the same and the y-value becomes negative.
    **Result:** $A'(3, -5)$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Describing Rotations
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A shape is turned halfway around a center point. What is the angle of rotation in degrees? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    A full turn is $360^\circ$, so a half-turn is **$180^\circ$**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
