## 2. Multiplying and Dividing Decimals
To multiply or divide decimals, we often use powers of 10 to simplify the problem before calculating. 

### Multiplication
When multiplying decimals, ignore the decimal points first, multiply the integers, and then place the decimal point so the answer has the same total number of decimal places as the original numbers combined. 
* **Example:** $0.3 \times 0.04$
* $3 \times 4 = 12$
* There are 3 total decimal places (one in 0.3, two in 0.04).
* **Result:** $0.012$

### Division by Powers of 10
Moving the decimal point is an efficient way to divide without a calculator: 
* **Divide by 10:** Move point 1 place left.
* **Divide by 100:** Move point 2 places left.
* **Divide by 1000:** Move point 3 places left.

## 3. Rounding and Significant Figures
In Topic 4 of Year 7, you learned about decimal places. Now, we introduce **Significant Figures (sig figs)**. 

**Rules for Significant Figures:**
1. All non-zero digits are significant.
2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant.
3. Leading zeros (at the start) are **not** significant.
4. Trailing zeros in a decimal are significant.

**Example:** Round $0.004562$ to 2 significant figures.
1. The first significant figure is 4.
2. The second is 5.
3. Look at the next digit (6); since it is 5 or above, round up.
4. **Result:** $0.0046$
