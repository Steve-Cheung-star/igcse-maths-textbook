## 2. Percentage Increase and Decrease
To find a new value after a percentage change, you can use the **Multiplier Method**.

* **Percentage Increase:** Add the percentage to 100% and convert to a decimal.
    * *Example:* Increase $60 by 15%.
    * Multiplier: $100\% + 15\% = 115\% = 1.15$
    * Calculation: $60 \times 1.15 = \$69$
* **Percentage Decrease:** Subtract the percentage from 100% and convert to a decimal.
    * *Example:* A $200 phone is 20% off.
    * Multiplier: $100\% - 20\% = 80\% = 0.8$
    * Calculation: $200 \times 0.8 = \$160$

## 3. Real-Life Contexts: Interest and Tax
Your outline highlights "real-life contexts" like discount, sales, tax, and simple interest.

**Simple Interest Formula:** $I = P \times r \times t$
* **P** = Principal (starting amount)
* **r** = Interest rate (as a decimal)
* **t** = Time (usually in years)

**Example:** Calculate the simple interest on $500 at 4% per year for 3 years.
1.  $P = 500, r = 0.04, t = 3$
2.  $500 \times 0.04 \times 3 = \$60$ interest.

### Q2: Solving Percent Problems
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A bag contains 20 marbles. 5 are blue. What percent of the marbles are **not** blue? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1.  Marbles that are not blue: $20 - 5 = 15$
    2.  Fraction: $\frac{15}{20}$
    3.  Convert to percentage: $\frac{15}{20} \times 100 = \mathbf{75\%}$ 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
