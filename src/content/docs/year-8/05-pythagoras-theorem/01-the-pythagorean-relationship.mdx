## 1. Inquiry: The Secret of Right Triangles
**Inquiry Questions:** *How do number and geometry connect? Where do we see right triangles in our daily lives?* 

Thousands of years ago, mathematicians discovered a consistent pattern in any triangle containing a $90^\circ$ (right) angle. They found that the squares of the two shorter sides always add up exactly to the square of the longest side.

---

## 2. Anatomy of a Right Triangle
Before using the formula, you must identify the most important side:
* **The Hypotenuse ($c$):** The longest side of a right-angled triangle. It is always located directly opposite the right angle.
* **The Legs ($a$ and $b$):** The two shorter sides that meet to form the right angle.



---

## 3. The Theorem: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$
The algebraic representation of this geometric relationship is:
$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$ 

To find a missing side, we use **squares** and **square roots**:
1. **Square** the lengths of the known sides.
2. **Add** them together (if finding the hypotenuse).
3. **Square root** the result to find the actual length.

**Example: Finding the Hypotenuse**
If a triangle has legs of $3\text{ cm}$ and $4\text{ cm}$, what is the hypotenuse?
* $3^2 + 4^2 = c^2$
* $9 + 16 = 25$
* $c = \sqrt{25} = \mathbf{5\text{ cm}}$
