## 1. Inquiry: The Shortcut
**Inquiry Questions:** *If you know the diagonal distance across a field, how can you find its width? How does Pythagoras help us find the height of a ladder reaching a window?* 

While the theorem is $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$, we don't always need to find the longest side ($c$). Sometimes we know the hypotenuse and need to find one of the shorter legs ($a$ or $b$). This requires a bit of algebraic rearrangement.

---

## 2. Finding a Shorter Side
If you are looking for a leg rather than the hypotenuse, you **subtract** the squares instead of adding them:
* $a^2 = c^2 - b^2$
* $b^2 = c^2 - a^2$

**Step-by-Step Strategy:**
1. **Identify the Hypotenuse:** Locate the side opposite the right angle ($c$).
2. **Substitute:** Plug the values into the rearranged formula.
3. **Subtract and Root:** Subtract the smaller square from the larger one, then take the square root.



**Example:** A 5m ladder is leaning against a wall. The base of the ladder is 3m from the wall. How high up the wall does the ladder reach?
* Here, the ladder is the hypotenuse ($c = 5$) and the distance from the wall is a leg ($a = 3$).
* $b^2 = 5^2 - 3^2$
* $b^2 = 25 - 9 = 16$
* $b = \sqrt{16} = \mathbf{4m}$

---

## 3. Pythagoras in Other Shapes
Many shapes contain "hidden" right triangles.
* **Rectangles:** The diagonal of a rectangle creates two right triangles.
* **Isosceles Triangles:** Drawing a line from the top point to the base creates two right triangles.

### Q2: Finding the Height
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A right-angled triangle has a hypotenuse of 13cm and a base of 12cm. Find its height.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Finding a leg: $13^2 - 12^2 = h^2$
    2. $169 - 144 = 25$
    3. $\sqrt{25} = \mathbf{5cm}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Real-World Diagonal
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    You want to take a shortcut across a rectangular park that is 60m long and 80m wide. How much shorter is the diagonal path compared to walking along the two sides?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Diagonal: $\sqrt{60^2 + 80^2} = \sqrt{3600 + 6400} = \sqrt{10000} = 100m$.
    2. Walking the sides: $60 + 80 = 140m$.
    3. Difference: $140 - 100 = \mathbf{40m}$ shorter.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
