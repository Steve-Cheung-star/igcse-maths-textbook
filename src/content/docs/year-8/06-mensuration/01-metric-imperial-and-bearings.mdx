## 1. Inquiry: Measuring Our World
**Inquiry Questions:** *How do we measure our world?  How do architects use mathematics to construct, design, measure and create innovative ideas? *

Measurement isn't just about rulers. It’s about choosing the right tool for the job. Whether you are navigating a ship using **bearings** or converting a recipe from **imperial** (ounces) to **metric** (grams), accuracy is key.

---

## 2. Bearings and Direction
A **bearing** is a precise way to give directions using degrees.
* **Measured from North:** Always start at $000^\circ$ (North).
* **Clockwise:** Always measure the angle in a clockwise direction.
* **Three Digits:** Bearings are always written with three digits (e.g., $045^\circ$ instead of $45^\circ$).



---

## 3. Metric vs. Imperial
Most of the world uses the **Metric system** (base 10), but you will still encounter **Imperial units** in places like the USA or in specific industries like aviation or construction.

**Key Metric Conversions:**
* **Length:** $10\text{ mm} = 1\text{ cm}$; $100\text{ cm} = 1\text{ m}$; $1000\text{ m} = 1\text{ km}$.
* **Mass:** $1000\text{ g} = 1\text{ kg}$.
* **Capacity:** $1000\text{ mL} = 1\text{ L}$.

**Common Imperial Units to Recognize:**
* Inches, Feet, Yards, and Miles (Length).
* Ounces, Pounds, and Tons (Mass).

### Q1: The Three-Figure Rule
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A hiker is traveling directly South-East. What is their bearing in three-figure notation?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. North is $0^\circ$, East is $90^\circ$, South is $180^\circ$.
    2. South-East is exactly halfway between $90^\circ$ and $180^\circ$.
    3. **Result:** $135^\circ$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Metric Jumping
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Convert the following:
    a) $2.5\text{ kg}$ to grams.
    b) $450\text{ mL}$ to liters.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $2.5 \times 1000 = \mathbf{2500\text{ g}}$.
    b) $450 \div 1000 = \mathbf{0.45\text{ L}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
