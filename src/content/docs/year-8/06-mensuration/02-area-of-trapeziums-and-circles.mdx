## 1. Inquiry: Deconstructing Shapes
**Inquiry Questions:** *How can we break a complex shape into simpler ones to find its area? Why does the area of a circle involve the square of its radius?*

Architects and designers rarely work with perfect squares. Often, they must calculate the area of slanted shapes (trapeziums) or curved spaces (circles). By understanding the "why" behind the formulas, you can solve measurement problems in any real-world design.

---

## 2. Area of a Trapezium
A trapezium is a quadrilateral with at least one pair of parallel sides. We call these parallel sides $a$ and $b$.



**The Formula:**
Through investigation, we find that a trapezium's area is the average of the parallel sides multiplied by the perpendicular height:
$$Area = \frac{(a + b) \times h}{2}$$

**Example:** A trapezium has parallel sides of $6\text{ cm}$ and $10\text{ cm}$, and a height of $5\text{ cm}$.
1.  Sum the parallel sides: $6 + 10 = 16$.
2.  Multiply by height: $16 \times 5 = 80$.
3.  Divide by 2: $\mathbf{40\text{ cm}^2}$.

---

## 3. Area of a Circle
Unlike the circumference (the distance around), the **Area** is the total surface covered by the circle.



**The Formula:**
The relationship discovered by mathematicians involves the square of the radius multiplied by $\pi$:
$$Area = \pi \times r^2$$

**Example:** Find the area of a circle with a radius of $3\text{ cm}$ (use $\pi \approx 3.14$).
1.  Square the radius: $3^2 = 9$.
2.  Multiply by $\pi$: $9 \times 3.14$.
3.  **Result:** $\mathbf{28.26\text{ cm}^2}$.

## 4. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: The Trapezium Garden
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A flower bed is in the shape of a trapezium with parallel sides of $4\text{ m}$ and $8\text{ m}$. The distance between them (height) is $3\text{ m}$. Calculate the area.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. $(4 + 8) = 12$
    2. $12 \times 3 = 36$
    3. $36 \div 2 = \mathbf{18\text{ m}^2}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Circular Calculation
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Calculate the area of a circle with a **diameter** of $10\text{ cm}$. (Remember to find the radius first! )
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Radius = $10 \div 2 = 5\text{ cm}$
    2. $Area = \pi \times 5^2$
    3. $Area = 25 \times \pi \approx \mathbf{78.54\text{ cm}^2}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Composite Decomposing
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A shape is made by attaching a semi-circle to the side of a rectangle. How would you calculate its total area?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    You **decompose** the shape: calculate the area of the rectangle, calculate the area of a full circle and divide by 2, then add the two results together.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
