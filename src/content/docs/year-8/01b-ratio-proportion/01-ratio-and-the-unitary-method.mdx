## 1. Inquiry: Comparing Quantities
**Inquiry Questions:** *How can we use proportional reasoning to solve problems? How does the unitary method simplify complex calculations?*

A ratio is a way of comparing two or more quantities. In Year 8, we focus on **Proportionality**—the idea that if one quantity changes, the other must change at the same rate to keep the relationship the same.



---

## 2. The Unitary Method
The **Unitary Method** is a technique where you find the value of **one unit** first, then multiply to find the required amount. This is incredibly useful for recipes, currency exchange, and best-buy problems.

**The Steps:**
1.  **Find the unit value:** Divide the total amount by the number of units you have.
2.  **Calculate the target:** Multiply that unit value by the number of units you want to find.

**Example:**
If 5 kg of apples cost $12.50, how much do 8 kg cost?
1.  Find the cost of 1 kg: $12.50 \div 5 = \$2.50$.
2.  Find the cost of 8 kg: $\$2.50 \times 8 = \$20.00$.

---

## 3. Sharing in a Ratio (Advanced)
In Year 8, you may be asked to find a total amount or a specific part when only one part of the ratio is known.

**Example:**
Alice and Bob share money in the ratio $3 : 5$. If Alice receives $21, how much does Bob receive?
1.  Alice's share is 3 parts.
2.  Find the value of 1 part: $21 \div 3 = \$7$.
3.  Bob's share is 5 parts: $5 \times 7 = \$35$.

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: The Unitary Method
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A car travels 240 km on 20 liters of fuel. How far can it travel on 35 liters?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1.  Find km per 1 liter: $240 \div 20 = 12 \text{ km/L}$.
    2.  Find km for 35 liters: $12 \times 35 = \mathbf{420 \text{ km}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Ratio Parts
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    The ratio of boys to girls in a club is $4 : 7$. If there are 28 girls, how many boys are there?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1.  The 7 parts for girls = 28.
    2.  1 part = $28 \div 7 = 4$.
    3.  Boys (4 parts) = $4 \times 4 = \mathbf{16 \text{ boys}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Currency Exchange
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    If $1 USD = 0.92 EUR, how many Euros will you get for $500 USD?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $500 \times 0.92 = \mathbf{460 \text{ EUR}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
