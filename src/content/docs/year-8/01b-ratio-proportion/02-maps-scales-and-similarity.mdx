## 1. Inquiry: Scaling the World
**Inquiry Questions:** *How can we represent large distances on a small piece of paper? What does it mean for two shapes to be "similar" but not "identical"?*

Scale is a specialized ratio that compares the size of a drawing or map to the actual size of the object in real life. 

### Types of Scale
1.  **Word Scale:** "1 cm represents 10 km."
2.  **Ratio Scale:** $1 : 100,000$ (This means 1 unit on the map is 100,000 units in real life).

---

## 2. Working with Map Scales
When using a ratio scale like $1 : 50,000$, both sides must be in the same units.

**Example:**
A map has a scale of $1 : 20,000$. If two towns are 5 cm apart on the map, what is the actual distance in kilometers?
1.  **Multiply by scale:** $5 \text{ cm} \times 20,000 = 100,000 \text{ cm}$.
2.  **Convert to meters:** $100,000 \div 100 = 1,000 \text{ m}$.
3.  **Convert to km:** $1,000 \div 1,000 = \mathbf{1 \text{ km}}$.

---

## 3. Similarity in Geometry
In Year 8, we define **Similar Shapes** as figures that have the same shape but different sizes.
* **Corresponding angles** are equal.
* **Corresponding sides** are in the same ratio (proportional).



**Finding a Missing Side:**
If Triangle A has a base of 4 cm and Triangle B (similar) has a base of 12 cm, the **Scale Factor** is $12 \div 4 = 3$. 
To find any other side in Triangle B, multiply the matching side in Triangle A by 3.

## 4. Practice & Homework

### Q1: Map Calculations
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A map uses the scale $1 : 250,000$. 
    
    a) If a road is 4 cm on the map, how long is it in real life (in km)?
    
    b) If a river is 25 km long, how long will it be on the map (in cm)?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $4 \times 250,000 = 1,000,000 \text{ cm} = \mathbf{10 \text{ km}}$.
    
    b) $25 \text{ km} = 2,500,000 \text{ cm}$. $2,500,000 \div 250,000 = \mathbf{10 \text{ cm}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Scale Factors
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Rectangle A is $2 \text{ cm} \times 5 \text{ cm}$. Rectangle B is similar and its shortest side is 6 cm. What is the length of its longer side?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Find scale factor: $6 \div 2 = 3$.
    2. Multiply longer side: $5 \times 3 = \mathbf{15 \text{ cm}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
