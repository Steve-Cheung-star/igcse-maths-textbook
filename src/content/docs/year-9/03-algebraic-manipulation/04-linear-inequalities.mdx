## 1. Inquiry: Ranges of Values
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we represent all the numbers that make a statement true, rather than just one?* In the real world, many things aren't "equal." For example, a bridge might have a weight limit ($w \le 5000\text{kg}$) or a speed limit ($s \le 100\text{km/h}$). We use **Inequalities** to model these situations. 

---

## 2. Inequality Symbols
* $>$ : Greater than
* $<$ : Less than
* $\ge$ : Greater than or equal to
* $\le$ : Less than or equal to

---

## 3. Solving Inequalities
The process for solving inequalities is almost identical to solving equations—whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other. 

**Example:** Solve $3x - 5 > 10$
1. Add $5$ to both sides: $3x > 15$
2. Divide by $3$: **$x > 5$**

**The Golden Rule:** If you multiply or divide an inequality by a **negative number**, you must **flip the inequality sign**.
* *Example:* $-2x < 10 \rightarrow$ divide by $-2 \rightarrow \mathbf{x > -5}$.

---

## 4. Representing on a Number Line
We represent the "solution set" on a number line using circles and arrows. 



* **Open Circle ($\circ$):** Used for $<$ and $>$ (the number itself is **not** included).
* **Closed Circle ($\bullet$):** Used for $\le$ and $\ge$ (the number itself **is** included).

### Q1: Two-Step Inequality
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve the inequality and describe the circle type on a number line: $4y + 2 \le 14$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Subtract $2$: $4y \le 12$
    2. Divide by $4$: **$y \le 3$**
    3. On a number line, this would be a **closed circle** at $3$ with an arrow pointing to the **left**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: The Negative Flip
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve $-3x + 1 < 10$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Subtract $1$: $-3x < 9$
    2. Divide by $-3$: **Flip the sign!**
    3. Result: **$x > -3$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Word Problem
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A taxi charges 5 HKD plus 2 HKD per kilometer. If you have a maximum of 25 HKD, write and solve an inequality for the distance $d$ you can travel.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Inequality: $5 + 2d \le 25$
    2. Subtract $5$: $2d \le 20$
    3. Divide by $2$: **$d \le 10$**
    4. You can travel a maximum of $10$ kilometers.
  </TabItem>
