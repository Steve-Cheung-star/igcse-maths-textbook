## 1. Inquiry: The Area Model
**Inquiry Question:** *How does the area of a rectangle help us understand multiplying two algebraic expressions?*

Algebraic manipulation is like a language. **Expanding** is the process of "opening" brackets to remove them, while **Factoring** (or factorising) is the process of putting brackets back in to simplify the expression.

---

## 2. Expanding Binomials
When we multiply two brackets together, such as $(x + 2)(x + 3)$, we must ensure every term in the first bracket is multiplied by every term in the second.

We often use the **FOIL** method:
* **F**irst: Multiply the first terms ($x \times x = x^2$)
* **O**utside: Multiply the outer terms ($x \times 3 = 3x$)
* **I**nside: Multiply the inner terms ($2 \times x = 2x$)
* **L**ast: Multiply the last terms ($2 \times 3 = 6$)



**Combining like terms:**
$x^2 + 3x + 2x + 6 = \mathbf{x^2 + 5x + 6}$

---

## 3. Factoring Algebraic Expressions
Factoring is the reverse of expanding. We look for the **Highest Common Factor (HCF)** of all terms and move it outside the bracket.

**Example:** Factor $6x^2 + 12x$
1. The HCF of $6$ and $12$ is **$6$**.
2. The HCF of $x^2$ and $x$ is **$x$**.
3. Move $6x$ outside: $6x(\dots + \dots)$
4. **Result:** $6x(x + 2)$.

---

## 4. Algebraic Fractions
Just like normal fractions, algebraic fractions can be simplified by dividing the numerator and denominator by a common factor.

**Example:** Simplify $\frac{10a^2b}{5ab}$
* $10 \div 5 = 2$
* $a^2 \div a = a$
* $b \div b = 1$
* **Result:** $\mathbf{2a}$

    **Result:** $x^2 + 3x - 10$
  </TabItem>

### Q2: Factoring
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Factorize completely: $15y^3 - 10y^2$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    HCF of $15$ and $10$ is $5$.
    HCF of $y^3$ and $y^2$ is $y^2$.
    **Result:** $5y^2(3y - 2)$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Simplifying Fractions
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Simplify: $\frac{x^2 + 5x}{x}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Factor the top first: $\frac{x(x + 5)}{x}$
    Cancel the $x$ from top and bottom.
    **Result:** $x + 5$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
