## 1. Inquiry: The Balance of Equality
**Inquiry Question:** *What is the best process to solve equations?* 

Solving an equation is like maintaining a balanced scale. Whatever operation you perform on one side, you must perform on the other to maintain equality.  In Year 9, we move beyond one-step problems to equations that require multiple steps of simplification before the variable can be isolated.

---

## 2. Variables on Both Sides
When the unknown variable appears on both sides of the equals sign, your first goal is to "collect" them on one side. 

**Strategy:** Move the smaller variable term to the side of the larger one to avoid dealing with negative coefficients.

**Example:** Solve $5x + 3 = 2x + 12$
1. Subtract $2x$ from both sides: $3x + 3 = 12$ 
2. Subtract $3$ from both sides: $3x = 9$ 
3. Divide by $3$: **$x = 3$** 

---

## 3. Equations with Brackets
If an equation contains brackets, you must **expand** them first using the distributive law before you start moving terms. 

**Example:** Solve $3(x - 4) = 15$
1. Expand the bracket: $3x - 12 = 15$ 
2. Add $12$ to both sides: $3x = 27$ 
3. Divide by $3$: **$x = 9$** 

---

## 4. Rearranging Formulas
Sometimes we don't want to find a numerical answer, but rather change the "subject" of a formula. 

* **Subject:** The variable that stands alone (usually on the left).
* **Inverse Operations:** Use the opposite operation to move other variables away. 
    * *Example:* If $V = IR$, to make $I$ the subject, divide both sides by $R$ to get $I = \frac{V}{R}$.

### Q2: Brackets First
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve for $a$: $2(a + 5) = 4a - 2$ 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Expand: $2a + 10 = 4a - 2$
    2. Subtract $2a$: $10 = 2a - 2$
    3. Add $2$: $12 = 2a$
    4. Divide by $2$: **$a = 6$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Changing the Subject
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Make $x$ the subject of the formula: $y = mx + c$ 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Subtract $c$: $y - c = mx$
    2. Divide by $m$: **$x = \frac{y - c}{m}$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
