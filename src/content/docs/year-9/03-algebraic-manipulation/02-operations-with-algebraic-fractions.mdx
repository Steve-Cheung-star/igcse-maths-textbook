## 1. Inquiry: Consistency in Rules
**Inquiry Question:** *Do the rules for numerical fractions still apply when we use letters instead of numbers?*

The fundamental rules of fractions never change. To add or subtract, you need a **common denominator**. To multiply, you go straight across. To divide, you **multiply by the reciprocal** (flip the second fraction).

---

## 2. Addition and Subtraction
To add or subtract algebraic fractions, you must find a common denominator. Usually, the easiest common denominator is the product of the two existing denominators.

**Example:** $\frac{x}{3} + \frac{x}{4}$
1. The common denominator is $3 \times 4 = 12$.
2. Multiply the first fraction by $\frac{4}{4}$ and the second by $\frac{3}{3}$:
   $$\frac{4x}{12} + \frac{3x}{12}$$
3. **Result:** $\frac{7x}{12}$



---

## 3. Multiplication and Division
Multiplication is straightforward: multiply the numerators and multiply the denominators.

**Example:** $\frac{2a}{5} \times \frac{3}{a}$
1. Multiply: $\frac{6a}{5a}$
2. Simplify by cancelling the $a$: **$\frac{6}{5}$**

For **Division**, remember the "Keep, Change, Flip" rule.
**Example:** $\frac{x}{2} \div \frac{x}{4}$
1. Flip the second fraction: $\frac{x}{2} \times \frac{4}{x}$
2. Multiply: $\frac{4x}{2x}$
3. Simplify: **$2$**

    Simplify: $\frac{2}{x} + \frac{3}{y}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The common denominator is $xy$.
    $\frac{2y}{xy} + \frac{3x}{xy}$
    **Result:** $\frac{2y + 3x}{xy}$
  </TabItem>

### Q2: Multiplying Fractions
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Simplify: $\frac{5ab}{3} \times \frac{6}{a}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $\frac{30ab}{3a}$
    Divide $30$ by $3$ and cancel the $a$.
    **Result:** $10b$
  </TabItem>

### Q3: Complex Division
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Simplify: $\frac{w^2}{5} \div \frac{w}{10}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $\frac{w^2}{5} \times \frac{10}{w} = \frac{10w^2}{5w}$
    **Result:** $2w$
