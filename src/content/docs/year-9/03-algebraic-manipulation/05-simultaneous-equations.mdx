## 1. Inquiry: The Intersection of Two Truths
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we find a single solution that satisfies two different conditions at the same time?*

A **System of Equations** (or Simultaneous Equations) is a set of two or more equations with the same variables. To "solve" the system, you must find the specific values for $x$ and $y$ that make **both** equations true simultaneously.

---

## 2. The Method of Substitution
The outline specifically focuses on the **Substitution Method**. This involves "plugging" one equation into the other to reduce the problem to a single variable.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1.  **Isolate:** Pick the simplest equation and solve for one variable (e.g., $y = \dots$).
2.  **Substitute:** Replace that variable in the *other* equation with the new expression.
3.  **Solve:** Solve the resulting one-variable equation.
4.  **Find the Second Variable:** Plug your answer back into the first equation.

**Example:**
Eq 1: $y = 2x$
Eq 2: $x + y = 12$

* Substitute Eq 1 into Eq 2: $x + (2x) = 12$
* Simplify: $3x = 12 \rightarrow \mathbf{x = 4}$
* Find $y$: $y = 2(4) \rightarrow \mathbf{y = 8}$
* **Solution:** $(4, 8)$

---

## 3. Modeling Real-Life Situations
Algebraic methods are often more efficient than trial and error for solving word problems.

**Scenario:** A farm has chickens ($c$) and cows ($w$). There are 10 heads and 32 legs.
* **Equation 1 (Heads):** $c + w = 10$
* **Equation 2 (Legs):** $2c + 4w = 32$

By solving these simultaneously, we can determine exactly how many of each animal there are without guessing.

### Q1: Basic Substitution
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve the system:
    1) $y = x + 3$
    2) $2x + y = 15$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Substitute $(x + 3)$ for $y$ in Eq 2: $2x + (x + 3) = 15$
    2. $3x + 3 = 15$
    3. $3x = 12 \rightarrow \mathbf{x = 4}$
    4. $y = 4 + 3 \rightarrow \mathbf{y = 7}$
    **Solution: (4, 7)**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Rearrange then Substitute
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Solve the system:
    1) $x - y = 2$
    2) $3x + 2y = 16$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Rearrange Eq 1: $x = y + 2$
    2. Substitute into Eq 2: $3(y + 2) + 2y = 16$
    3. $3y + 6 + 2y = 16$
    4. $5y = 10 \rightarrow \mathbf{y = 2}$
    5. $x = 2 + 2 \rightarrow \mathbf{x = 4}$
    **Solution: (4, 2)**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
