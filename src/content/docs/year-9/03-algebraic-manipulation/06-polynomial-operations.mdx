## 1. Inquiry: Simplifying Complexity
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we make a complex problem simpler?* 

A **polynomial** is an expression consisting of variables and coefficients. In Year 9, we deal with "higher-degree" polynomials, which means variables might have exponents (like $x^2$) or we might have multiple variables in a single term (like $xy^2$).

---

## 2. Adding and Subtracting Polynomials
The golden rule here is to **only combine like terms**. Terms are "like" only if they have the exact same variables raised to the exact same powers.

**Example:** $(3x^2y + 2xy^2) + (4x^2y - 6xy^2)$ 
1. Identify the $x^2y$ terms: $3x^2y + 4x^2y = 7x^2y$.
2. Identify the $xy^2$ terms: $2xy^2 - 6xy^2 = -4xy^2$.
3. **Result:** $7x^2y - 4xy^2$.

---

## 3. Multiplying Monomials by Polynomials
When multiplying a single term (monomial) by a bracketed expression (polynomial), use the distributive law to multiply the monomial by **every** term inside.

**Example:** $2x^2(3x^2 - 2x + 1)$ 
1. $2x^2 \times 3x^2 = 6x^4$ (Remember: add the indices!)
2. $2x^2 \times -2x = -4x^3$
3. $2x^2 \times 1 = 2x^2$
4. **Result:** $6x^4 - 4x^3 + 2x^2$.



---

## 4. Expanding Second-Degree Expressions
You must be able to expand binomials where the terms themselves are more complex, such as $(2x - y)(x + 3y)$.

**FOIL Method:** 
* **First:** $2x \times x = 2x^2$
* **Outside:** $2x \times 3y = 6xy$
* **Inside:** $-y \times x = -xy$
* **Last:** $-y \times 3y = -3y^2$
* **Simplify:** $2x^2 + 5xy - 3y^2$.

---

## 5. Rearranging Complex Formulas
In Year 8, you moved one or two terms. In Year 9, you must rearrange formulas where the subject appears more than once or is under a square root.

**Example:** Make $x$ the subject of $A = \pi x^2$. 
1. Divide by $\pi$: $\frac{A}{\pi} = x^2$.
2. Take the square root: **$x = \sqrt{\frac{A}{\pi}}$**.
