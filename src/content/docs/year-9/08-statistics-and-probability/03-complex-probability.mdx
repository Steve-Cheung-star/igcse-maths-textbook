## 1. Inquiry: The Logic of Chance
**Inquiry Question:** *When should we add probabilities, and when should we multiply them?*

To solve problems with two or more events (like picking two marbles from a bag), we use specific mathematical "Logic Gates."

---

## 2. The Multiplicative Rule (Independent Events)
If you want Event A **AND** Event B to happen, you **multiply** the probabilities.
* *Example:* Flipping a head ($1/2$) AND rolling a six ($1/6$).
* $P(A \text{ and } B) = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{6} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{12}}$.

---

## 3. The Additive Rule (Mutually Exclusive)
If you want Event A **OR** Event B to happen, you **add** the probabilities.
* *Example:* Rolling a 1 ($1/6$) OR rolling a 2 ($1/6$) on the same die.
* $P(A \text{ or } B) = \frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \mathbf{\frac{2}{6} = \frac{1}{3}}$.

### Q1: Combined Events
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A bag has 4 red and 6 green balls. You pick one, replace it, and pick again. What is the probability of getting Red then Green?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $P(R) = 4/10$, $P(G) = 6/10$
    $P(R \text{ then } G) = \frac{4}{10} \times \frac{6}{10} = \frac{24}{100} = \mathbf{24\%}$.
  </TabItem>
