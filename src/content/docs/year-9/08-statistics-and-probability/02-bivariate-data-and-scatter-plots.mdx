## 1. Inquiry: Correlation vs. Causation
**Inquiry Question:** *If two things happen at the same time, does it mean one caused the other? How can graphs be used to trick us?*

When we compare two different attributes of the same thing (like a person's height vs. their weight), we are looking at **Bivariate Data**.

---

## 2. Correlation and Outliers
* **Positive Correlation:** Both go up together.
* **Negative Correlation:** One goes up, the other goes down.
* **Outlier (Anomaly):** A data point that sits far away from the general trend. In Year 9, we must identify these and decide if they should be included in our predictions.



---

## 3. Misleading Graphs
The outline requires you to spot "bad" statistics. Common tricks include:
* **Truncated Axes:** The y-axis doesn't start at zero, making small changes look huge.
* **Biased Sampling:** Surveying only people who you know will give the answer you want.
* **Cherry-Picking:** Only showing the time period that supports your argument.
