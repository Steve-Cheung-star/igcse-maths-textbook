## 1. Inquiry: Tracking the "Running Total"
**Inquiry Question:** *How can we visualize the total number of people below a certain score or height? How is a histogram different from a standard bar chart?*

In previous years, you looked at frequencies. Now, we look at **Cumulative Frequency**, which adds up the frequencies as you go along to show a running total.

---

## 2. Cumulative Frequency Tables and Curves
To draw a cumulative frequency curve (an "Ogive"):
1.  **Create the Table:** Add a column where you add the current frequency to the sum of all previous frequencies.
2.  **Plotting:** Always plot the cumulative frequency on the **y-axis** against the **upper bound** of the class interval on the **x-axis**.
3.  **The Curve:** Connect the points with a smooth, S-shaped curve starting from the bottom-left.



---

## 3. Histograms for Grouped Data
Unlike a bar chart, a **Histogram** is used for continuous data where the bars touch. 
* **The Rule:** The **area** of the bar represents the frequency. 
* **Frequency Density:** If class widths are unequal, you must calculate Frequency Density ($FD = \text{Frequency} \div \text{Class Width}$) to determine the height of the bars.



---

## 4. Time Series (Line Graphs)
A **Time Series** graph is used to display how data changes over a specific period. 
* **X-axis:** Always represents Time (days, months, years).
* **Y-axis:** Represents the variable being measured.
* **Analysis:** We look for **trends** (general direction) and **seasonality** (patterns that repeat).

### Q1: Calculating Cumulative Frequency
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Given these frequencies: (0-10: 5), (10-20: 8), (20-30: 2). What are the cumulative frequencies?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    - Less than 10: **5**
    - Less than 20: 5 + 8 = **13**
    - Less than 30: 13 + 2 = **15**
  </TabItem>
