## 1. Inquiry: Beyond the Average
**Inquiry Question:** *If two classes have the same average test score, does it mean they performed the same? How do we measure the "gap" between the top and bottom students?*

The **Mean** only tells you the center. To understand the whole story, we need to look at the **Spread** (how far apart the data points are).

---

## 2. The Five-Number Summary and Quartiles
For large data sets, we divide the data into four equal parts (quartiles):
* **Lower Quartile (Q1):** The value at the 25% mark.
* **Median (Q2):** The middle value or 50% mark.
* **Upper Quartile (Q3):** The value at the 75% mark.
* **Interquartile Range (IQR):** $Q3 - Q1$. This measures the spread of the middle 50% of the data and is less affected by extreme outliers than the total range.



---

## 3. Calculating the Mean for Grouped Data
When data is grouped into intervals (e.g., $10 < x \le 20$), we don't know the exact values. To estimate the mean, we use the **midpoint** of each interval:
1.  **Find Midpoints ($x$):** The center of each range.
2.  **Multiply ($f \times x$):** Multiply the frequency of each group by its midpoint.
3.  **Sum and Divide:** $\frac{\text{Total } (f \times x)}{\text{Total Frequency}}$.

---

## 4. The Effect of Outliers
An **outlier** is a value significantly different from the rest. 
* **The Mean:** Highly sensitive. One very high score will "drag" the mean up significantly.
* **The Median:** Very resistant. Changing an extreme value usually has little to no effect on the middle position.

## 5. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Calculating IQR
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    In a data set, the Lower Quartile ($Q1$) is 15 and the Upper Quartile ($Q3$) is 42. Calculate the Interquartile Range.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $IQR = Q3 - Q1$
    $IQR = 42 - 15 = \mathbf{27}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Outlier Analysis
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A small company has 5 employees earning 30,000 HKD and a CEO earning 500,000 HKD. Which measure of average (Mean or Median) would be more "misleading" for a new job seeker?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The **Mean**. The CEO's high salary (outlier) will make the "average" salary look much higher than what most employees actually earn.
  </TabItem>
