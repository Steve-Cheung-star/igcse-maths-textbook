## 1. Inquiry: Truth and Uncertainty
**Inquiry Question:** *Can we make better decisions by understanding probability? How can mathematical information be interpreted graphically to support conclusions?*

Mathematics isn't just about calculation; it's about interpretation. This final lesson focuses on your ability to use the "vocabulary of probability" to interpret results involving uncertainty.

---

## 2. Systematic Outcomes and Complementary Events
To find the probability of complex events, you must list all outcomes in a **systematic way** (using tables or tree diagrams).
* **Mutually Exclusive Events:** Outcomes that cannot happen at the same time. You must know that the sum of all mutually exclusive probabilities always equals **1**.
* **Complementary Events:** The probability of an event *not* occurring. $P(\text{Not A}) = 1 - P(A)$.



---

## 3. Comparing Experimental vs. Theoretical Probability
The outline requires you to compare **experimental data** (what actually happened in a trial) with **theoretical probabilities** (what the math says should happen).
* **Inference:** If you flip a coin 100 times and get 90 heads, your experimental probability ($0.9$) is far from the theoretical ($0.5$). This suggests the coin might be biased.
* **Law of Large Numbers:** As you increase the number of trials, the experimental probability usually gets closer to the theoretical probability.

---

## 4. Identifying Misleading Graphs
A key objective is to identify through investigation graphs that present data in misleading ways.
* **Truncated Vertical Axis:** Line graphs that exaggerate a range by starting the vertical axis at a point greater than zero.
* **Incorrect Scaling:** Using 3D objects to represent 1D changes, which makes increases look much larger than they are.

### Q1: The Sum of Probabilities
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A spinner has Red, Blue, and Green sections. $P(\text{Red}) = 0.3$ and $P(\text{Blue}) = 0.5$. What is $P(\text{Green})$? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The sum must be 1.
    $1 - (0.3 + 0.5) = \mathbf{0.2}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
