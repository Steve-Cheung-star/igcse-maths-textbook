## 1. Inquiry: Beyond the Straight Line
**Inquiry Question:** *What happens when the difference between terms isn't constant? How can we describe growth that "speeds up"?*

Not every pattern in nature follows a straight line. The way bacteria grows or the way a falling object speeds up requires **Non-Linear Sequences**.

---

## 2. Geometric Sequences
In an arithmetic sequence, we add a constant. In a **Geometric Sequence**, we multiply by a constant called the **Common Ratio ($r$)**.

**Example:** $3, 6, 12, 24, 48, \dots$
* Here, we are multiplying by $2$ every time ($r = 2$).
* These sequences grow **exponentially**. They start slow but become massive very quickly.



---

## 3. Special Number Sequences
You should be able to recognize and generate terms for these classic mathematical patterns:

* **Square Numbers ($n^2$):** $1, 4, 9, 16, 25, \dots$
* **Cube Numbers ($n^3$):** $1, 8, 27, 64, 125, \dots$
* **Triangle Numbers:** $1, 3, 6, 10, 15, \dots$ (formed by adding consecutive integers: $1, 1+2, 1+2+3 \dots$)



---

## 4. Quadratic Sequences (The Second Difference)
If the "difference" between numbers changes, but the **difference of the differences** is constant, the sequence is **Quadratic** (it involves $n^2$).

**Example:** $2, 6, 12, 20, 30$
1. First differences: $4, 6, 8, 10$ (Not constant)
2. Second differences: $2, 2, 2$ (**Constant!**)
* Because the second difference is constant, we know the formula contains an $n^2$ term.

## 5. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Identifying the Type
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Identify if the following sequences are Arithmetic, Geometric, or neither:
    a) $100, 50, 25, 12.5 \dots$
    b) $1, 3, 9, 27 \dots$
    c) $2, 5, 8, 11 \dots$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) **Geometric** (Ratio is $0.5$ or $\div 2$)
    b) **Geometric** (Ratio is $3$)
    c) **Arithmetic** (Common difference is $+3$)
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Triangle Numbers
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    What is the $6^{th}$ triangle number?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Sum of first 6 integers: $1+2+3+4+5+6 = \mathbf{21}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Finding the Ratio
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A geometric sequence starts $5, 20, 80 \dots$ Find the $5^{th}$ term.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Ratio is $\times 4$.
    2. $4^{th}$ term: $80 \times 4 = 320$.
    3. $5^{th}$ term: $320 \times 4 = \mathbf{1280}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
