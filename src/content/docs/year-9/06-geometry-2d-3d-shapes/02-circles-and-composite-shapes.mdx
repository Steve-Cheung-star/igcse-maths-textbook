## 1. Inquiry: The Geometry of the Circle
**Inquiry Question:** *How is the diameter of a circle related to its perimeter?*

The circle is a unique shape where every point on the boundary is the same distance from the center. This constant relationship is defined by the mathematical constant $\pi$ (Pi).

---

## 2. Circumference and Area of a Circle
You need to master these two primary formulas: 
* **Circumference ($C$):** $C = 2\pi r$ or $C = \pi d$ (The distance around the edge). 
* **Area ($A$):** $A = \pi r^2$ (The space inside the circle). 



---

## 3. Composite 2-D Shapes
Many real-world objects aren't simple rectangles. We calculate the area of **composite shapes** by decomposing them into simpler shapes like rectangles, triangles, and parallelograms. 

**Strategy:**
1.  Divide the shape into "known" parts. 
2.  Calculate the area of each part individually. 
3.  Add (or subtract) the areas to find the total.

### Q2: Composite Area
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A shape consists of a rectangle ($4\text{m} \times 10\text{m}$) with a triangle (base $4\text{m}$, height $3\text{m}$) on top. Find the total area.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Area of rectangle: $4 \times 10 = 40\text{m}^2$
    2. Area of triangle: $\frac{1}{2} \times 4 \times 3 = 6\text{m}^2$
    **Total Area:** $40 + 6 = \mathbf{46\text{m}^2}$
  </TabItem>
