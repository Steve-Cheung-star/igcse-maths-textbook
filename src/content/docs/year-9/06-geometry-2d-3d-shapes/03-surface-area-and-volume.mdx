## 1. Inquiry: Filling and Covering
**Inquiry Question:** *How do we measure the capacity of a container versus the material needed to build it?*

In 3-D geometry, we differentiate between the "inside" and the "outside" of objects. Think of a swimming pool: 
* **Volume ($V$):** The amount of water needed to fill the pool. It is the measure of the space *inside* a 3-D object (measured in cubic units like $\text{cm}^3$, $\text{m}^3$, or liters).
* **Surface Area ($SA$):** The amount of tile needed to line the bottom and sides of the pool. It is the total area of all the *outside* faces of an object (measured in square units like $\text{cm}^2$ or $\text{m}^2$).

## 3. Surface Area of Prisms
To find the surface area, we calculate the area of every flat 2-D face and add them together. 

* **Rectangular Prism:** Has 6 rectangular faces (Top/Bottom, Front/Back, Left/Right). 
    $$SA = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh$$
* **Cylinder:** Includes the two circular ends and a "curved surface". Imagine peeling the label off a soup can—it forms a rectangle! The length of this rectangle is the circumference of the circle ($2\pi r$).

**Cylinder Surface Area Formula:**
$$SA = \text{Top} + \text{Bottom} + \text{Curved Side}$$
$$SA = \pi r^2 + \pi r^2 + (2\pi r \times h)$$
$$SA = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh$$

## 5. Practice & Homework (35 Minutes)

### Q1: Volume of a Cylinder
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A soda can has a radius of $3\text{cm}$ and a height of $12\text{cm}$. Find its volume (use $\pi \approx 3.14$).
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Base Area ($A$) = $\pi \times r^2 = 3.14 \times 3^2 = 3.14 \times 9 = 28.26\text{cm}^2$.
    2. Volume ($V$) = Base Area $\times$ height = $28.26 \times 12$.
    **Result:** $339.12\text{cm}^3$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Surface Area of a Cube
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Find the surface area of a cube with side length $4\text{cm}$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Area of one square face: $4 \times 4 = 16\text{cm}^2$.
    2. A cube has 6 identical faces.
    3. $16 \times 6 = \mathbf{96\text{cm}^2}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Volume of a Triangular Prism
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A prism has a triangular base with area $15\text{cm}^2$ and a length of $10\text{cm}$. What is its volume?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The volume of any prism is the area of its base times its length/height.
    $V = \text{Base Area} \times \text{Length}$
    $V = 15 \times 10 = \mathbf{150\text{cm}^3}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q5: Applied Volume (Swimming Pool)
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A cylindrical swimming pool has a diameter of $10\text{m}$ and is filled with water to a depth of $2\text{m}$. How many cubic meters of water are in the pool? (Use $\pi \approx 3.14$)
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. First, find the radius. Radius = Diameter $\div 2 = 10 \div 2 = 5\text{m}$.
    2. $V = \pi r^2 h$
    3. $V = 3.14 \times (5^2) \times 2$
    4. $V = 3.14 \times 25 \times 2 = 3.14 \times 50$
    **Result:** $\mathbf{157\text{m}^3}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q6: Working Backwards
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A cylinder has a volume of $314\text{cm}^3$ and a radius of $5\text{cm}$. What is its height? (Use $\pi \approx 3.14$)
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Set up the volume formula and solve for the unknown ($h$):
    1. $V = \pi r^2 h$
    2. $314 = 3.14 \times (5^2) \times h$
    3. $314 = 3.14 \times 25 \times h$
    4. $314 = 78.5 \times h$
    5. $h = 314 \div 78.5$
    **Result:** The height is $\mathbf{4\text{cm}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q8: Surface Area of a Triangular Prism
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A tent is shaped like a triangular prism. The front opening is a right triangle with a base of $3\text{m}$, a height of $4\text{m}$, and a hypotenuse (slanted side) of $5\text{m}$. The tent is $10\text{m}$ long. Calculate the total surface area of the tent, including the floor.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Find the area of all 5 faces (2 triangles, 3 rectangles):
    1. **Front & Back Triangles:** $2 \times (\frac{1}{2} \times 3 \times 4) = 2 \times 6 = 12\text{m}^2$.
    2. **Floor (Rectangle):** Base $\times$ Length = $3 \times 10 = 30\text{m}^2$.
    3. **Vertical Wall (Rectangle):** Height $\times$ Length = $4 \times 10 = 40\text{m}^2$.
    4. **Slanted Roof (Rectangle):** Hypotenuse $\times$ Length = $5 \times 10 = 50\text{m}^2$.
    5. **Total SA:** $12 + 30 + 40 + 50 = \mathbf{132\text{m}^2}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
