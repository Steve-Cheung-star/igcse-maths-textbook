## 1. Inquiry: Filling and Covering
**Inquiry Question:** *How do we measure the capacity of a container versus the material needed to build it?*

In 3-D geometry, we distinguish between:
* **Volume ($V$):** The amount of space *inside* a 3-D object (measured in cubic units like $\text{cm}^3$).
* **Surface Area ($SA$):** The total area of all the *outside* faces of an object (measured in square units like $\text{cm}^2$).

---

## 2. Volume of Prisms and Cylinders
A **prism** is a 3-D shape with a constant cross-section. The volume of *any* prism is calculated by finding the area of the base ($A$) and multiplying it by the height ($h$).

$$V = \text{Area of base} \times \text{height}$$



* **Rectangular Prism:** $V = l \times w \times h$
* **Cylinder:** $V = \pi r^2 h$ (since the base is a circle)

---

## 3. Surface Area of Prisms
To find the surface area, we calculate the area of every flat face and add them together. For a cylinder, this includes the two circular ends and the "curved surface" which, when unrolled, is a rectangle.

**Cylinder Surface Area Formula:**
$$SA = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh$$



---

## 4. Nets and 3-D Visualization
A **net** is a 2-D pattern that can be folded to make a 3-D solid. Visualizing nets helps in calculating surface area because it ensures you don't miss any hidden faces (like the bottom or back of a shape).

## 5. Practice & Homework (25 Minutes)

### Q1: Volume of a Cylinder
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A soda can has a radius of $3\text{cm}$ and a height of $12\text{cm}$. Find its volume (use $\pi \approx 3.14$).
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Base Area ($A$) = $\pi \times 3^2 = 3.14 \times 9 = 28.26\text{cm}^2$.
    2. Volume ($V$) = $28.26 \times 12$.
    **Result:** $339.12\text{cm}^3$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Surface Area of a Cube
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Find the surface area of a cube with side length $4\text{cm}$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Area of one face: $4 \times 4 = 16\text{cm}^2$.
    2. A cube has 6 identical faces.
    3. $16 \times 6 = \mathbf{96\text{cm}^2}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Volume of a Triangular Prism
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A prism has a triangular base with area $15\text{cm}^2$ and a length of $10\text{cm}$. What is its volume?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $V = \text{Base Area} \times \text{Length}$
    $V = 15 \times 10 = \mathbf{150\text{cm}^3}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
