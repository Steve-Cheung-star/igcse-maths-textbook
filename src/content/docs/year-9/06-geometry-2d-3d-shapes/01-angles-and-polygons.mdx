## 1. Inquiry: The Language of Shape
**Inquiry Questions:** *What is a measurement? What can be measured?* 

Geometry is the study of how shapes fit together. By understanding the relationships between angles, we can solve complex architectural and engineering problems.

---

## 2. Angle Relationships
You must be able to solve angle-relationship problems involving: 
* **Intersecting Lines:** Finding vertically opposite angles (which are equal) or supplementary angles (which sum to $180^\circ$). 
* **Parallel Lines and Transversals:** Identifying **alternate angles** (Z-angles), **corresponding angles** (F-angles), and **co-interior angles** (C-angles). 
* **Triangles:** Finding interior angles (sum to $180^\circ$) or complementary angles (sum to $90^\circ$). 



---

## 3. Interior and Exterior Angles of Polygons
Through investigation, you will discover the rules for any polygon (a flat shape with straight sides): 
* **Interior Angles:** The sum of interior angles follows the formula $(n - 2) \times 180^\circ$, where $n$ is the number of sides. 
* **Exterior Angles:** For any convex polygon, the exterior angles always sum to $360^\circ$.

    Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. If one angle is $70^\circ$, find the size of its alternate interior angle.

    **Result:** $70^\circ$

### Q2: Polygon Angles
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    What is the sum of the interior angles of a pentagon (5 sides)?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    Sum $= (n - 2) \times 180^\circ$
    Sum $= (5 - 2) \times 180^\circ = 3 \times 180^\circ$
    **Result:** $540^\circ$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
