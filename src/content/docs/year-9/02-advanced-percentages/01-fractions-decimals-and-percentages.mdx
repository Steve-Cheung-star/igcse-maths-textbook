## 1. Inquiry: Financial Decisions
**Inquiry Questions:** *How do we use percentages to make financial decisions? How can we best plan for the future?* Percentages are essentially a way to compare different quantities by expressing them as "parts per 100". To make accurate financial choices, we must be able to switch fluently between fractions, decimals, and percentages. 

---

## 2. Recurring Decimals and Exact Fractions
Not all divisions result in a "clean" decimal. Some fractions result in **recurring decimals**, where a digit or a pattern of digits repeats infinitely. 

* **Terminating Decimals:** Decimals that end (e.g., $\frac{1}{4} = 0.25$). 
* **Recurring Decimals:** Decimals that repeat (e.g., $\frac{1}{3} = 0.333...$ or $0.\dot{3}$). 

Recognizing that a recurring decimal is an **exact fraction** is vital for precision. For example, using $0.33$ in a calculation is less accurate than using $\frac{1}{3}$. 

---

## 3. Translation and Mental Math
In Year 9, you are expected to translate between these forms without always relying on a calculator. 

| Fraction | Decimal | Percentage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $\frac{1}{8}$ | $0.125$ | $12.5\%$  |
| $\frac{4}{3}$ | $1.333...$ | $133.3\%$  |
| $\frac{1}{5}$ | $0.2$ | $20\%$  |

**Key Skills:**
* **Percentages over 100:** Understanding that $115\%$ represents the whole plus an extra $15\%$. 
* **Reverse Percentages:** Finding the original value after a percentage change has occurred.

## 4. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Equivalent Forms
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Convert the following into their equivalent forms without a calculator:
    a) $12.5\%$ as a fraction. 
    b) $\frac{5}{6}$ as a recurring decimal. 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    a) $12.5\% = \frac{12.5}{100} = \frac{125}{1000} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{8}}$. 
    b) $5 \div 6 = \mathbf{0.8\dot{3}}$. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Percentage of Quantity
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Find $115\%$ of 200 HKD. 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $100\%$ is 200 HKD.
    $10\%$ is 20 HKD.
    $5\%$ is 10 HKD.
    Total: $200 + 20 + 10 = \mathbf{230}$ **HKD**. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q3: Reverse Percentages
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A jacket costs 115 HKD after a $15\%$ tax (VAT) was added. What was the original price? 
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The price represents $115\%$. 
    $115\% = 115$ HKD.
    $1\% = 1$ HKD.
    $100\% = \mathbf{100}$ **HKD**. 
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
