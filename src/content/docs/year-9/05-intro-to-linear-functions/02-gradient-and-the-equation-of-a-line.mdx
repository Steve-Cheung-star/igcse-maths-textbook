## 1. Inquiry: Measuring Steepness
**Inquiry Question:** *How do we quantify how "steep" a line is, and what does that number represent in the real world?* 

The **gradient** (or slope) measures the rate of change between two variables. In a graph, it tells us how much the line goes up or down for every unit we move to the right.

---

## 2. Calculating Gradient ($m$)
To find the gradient, we use the ratio of the "rise" (vertical change) over the "run" (horizontal change).

**The Gradient Formula:**
If you have two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, the formula is:
$$m = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$$ 



**Types of Slopes:**
* **Positive:** Line goes "uphill" from left to right.
* **Negative:** Line goes "downhill" from left to right.
* **Zero:** A horizontal line.
* **Undefined:** A vertical line.

---

## 3. The Equation of a Line: $y = mx + b$
Every straight line can be written in a standard form that reveals its two most important characteristics:

$$y = mx + b$$

* **$m$ (Gradient):** The slope of the line.
* **$b$ (y-intercept):** The point where the line crosses the y-axis (where $x=0$).

**Example:** For the equation $y = 3x + 5$:
* The gradient is **$3$** (it goes up 3 units for every 1 across).
* The y-intercept is **$5$** (it starts at $(0, 5)$ on the graph).

---

## 4. Modeling Realistic Situations
In real-world problems, $m$ represents the **unit rate** and $b$ represents the **initial cost** or starting value.

**Example Scenario:**
A shipping package costs $50\text{g}$ for the box plus $6\text{g}$ per flyer.
* **Equation:** $M = 6d + 50$ 
* **Meaning:** The gradient ($6$) is the mass per flyer. The y-intercept ($50$) is the mass of the empty packaging.

  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    Find the slope of the line passing through points $(2, 4)$ and $(5, 10)$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    $m = \frac{10 - 4}{5 - 2} = \frac{6}{3}$
    **Result:** $m = 2$
