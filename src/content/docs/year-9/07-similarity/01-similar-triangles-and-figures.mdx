## 1. Inquiry: Shadows and Scales
**Inquiry Questions:** *How can we measure the height of a tree using only its shadow? How do architects use scale models to build skyscrapers?*

Two shapes are **similar** if:
1.  Their corresponding **angles are equal**.
2.  Their corresponding **sides are in the same ratio** (proportional).

---

## 2. Scale Factors ($k$)
The **scale factor** is the number you multiply the side lengths of one shape by to get the side lengths of the other.

$$k = \frac{\text{New Length}}{\text{Original Length}}$$

* If $k > 1$, the shape is an **enlargement**.
* If $0 < k < 1$, the shape is a **reduction**.



---

## 3. Proving Similarity in Triangles
To prove two triangles are similar, you don't need to check every side and angle. You can use these shortcuts:
* **AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle):** All three angles are the same.
* **SSS (Side-Side-Side):** All three sides are in the same proportion.
* **SAS (Side-Angle-Side):** Two sides are in proportion and the included angle is equal.

---

## 4. Solving for Missing Lengths
Once you know two shapes are similar, you can find missing lengths by setting up a proportion or using the scale factor.

**Example:** Triangle A has sides of $3, 4, 5$. Triangle B is similar and its shortest side is $9$. Find the other sides.
1.  Find the scale factor: $k = 9 \div 3 = 3$.
2.  Multiply other sides by $3$:
    * $4 \times 3 = 12$
    * $5 \times 3 = 15$
3.  **Result:** The sides of Triangle B are $9, 12, 15$.

## 5. Practice & Homework (20 Minutes)

### Q1: Calculating Scale Factor
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A photograph measuring $10\text{cm}$ by $15\text{cm}$ is enlarged so that the shorter side is $30\text{cm}$. What is the new length of the longer side?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    1. Scale factor $k = 30 \div 10 = 3$.
    2. Longer side $= 15 \times 3 = \mathbf{45\text{cm}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Q2: Similar Triangles
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">
    A $2\text{m}$ tall stick casts a $3\text{m}$ shadow. At the same time, a flagpole casts a $12\text{m}$ shadow. How tall is the flagpole?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">
    The triangles formed by the objects and their shadows are similar.
    1. Ratio $= 12 \div 3 = 4$.
    2. Height $= 2 \times 4 = \mathbf{8\text{m}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
