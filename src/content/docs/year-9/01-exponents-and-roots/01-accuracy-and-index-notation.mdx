## 1. Inquiry: Patterns in Powers
**Inquiry Question:** *How do powers and roots relate to each other? How can number patterns help us learn more about numbers?* 

Mathematics is the study of patterns. When we multiply a number by itself repeatedly, we create a pattern called an **exponent** (or power).  Understanding these patterns allows us to describe everything from the growth of bacteria to the vast distances between stars using **Standard Index Form**. 

---

## 2. Accuracy: Rounding and Significant Figures
Before calculating complex powers, we must ensure our results are reported with the correct degree of accuracy. 

* **Decimal Places (d.p.):** Rounding based on the number of digits after the decimal point. 
* **Significant Figures (s.f.):** Rounding based on the "importance" of digits, starting from the first non-zero digit. 
    * *Example:* $0.004567$ to $2$ s.f. is $\mathbf{0.0046}$.

---

## 3. Index Notation
Instead of writing $10 \times 10 \times 10$, we use **Index Notation**: $10^3$. 

* **The Base:** The number being multiplied ($10$). 
* **The Index/Exponent:** How many times the base appears ($3$). 
* **Exponential Notation:** Expressing repeated multiplication using these indices (e.g., $5 \times 5 \times 5 \times 5 = 5^4$). 



### Calculator Skills
You must be able to use your calculator efficiently for: 
1.  **Squares ($x^2$):** Multiplying a number by itself. 
2.  **Cubes ($x^3$):** Multiplying a number by itself three times. 
3.  **Square Roots ($\sqrt{x}$):** Finding the positive value that, when squared, gives the original number.
