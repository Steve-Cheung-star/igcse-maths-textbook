## 1. Inquiry: Scaling the Universe
**Inquiry Questions:** *How do we represent the distance to the sun or the width of a human hair without losing track of the zeros?* 

**Standard Index Form** (also known as Scientific Notation) is a way of writing numbers as a value between 1 and 10 multiplied by a power of 10.

$$A \times 10^n$$

* **$A$** must be a number where $1 \le A < 10$.
* **$n$** is an integer (positive for large numbers, negative for small numbers).

---

## 2. Converting Notation
You need to be able to move fluently between ordinary and standard form representation.

### Large Numbers (Positive Indices)
To convert $45,000$:
1.  Place the decimal so the number is between 1 and 10: **$4.5$**.
2.  Count how many places the decimal moved: **4 places**.
3.  **Result:** $4.5 \times 10^4$.

### Small Numbers (Negative Indices)
To convert $0.0001234$:
1.  Place the decimal: **$1.234$**.
2.  Count the jumps to the right: **4 places**.
3.  **Result:** $1.234 \times 10^{-4}$.



---

## 3. Estimating and Calculators
Standard form allows us to make **sensible estimates** for multiplication and division by simply adding or subtracting the powers of 10.

**Calculator Display:**
Most modern calculators will display standard form using a small "E" or "$\times 10^x$". You must know how to input these using the function keys efficiently.

> **Extension Idea:** When multiplying in standard form, multiply the front numbers and add the powers: 
> $(2.4 \times 10^7) \times (5 \times 10^3) = 12 \times 10^{10} = \mathbf{1.2 \times 10^{11}}$.
