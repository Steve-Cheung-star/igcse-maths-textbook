The **gradient** (often represented by the letter $m$) measures the steepness and direction of a straight line. 

Before calculating any numbers, you should always look at the graph to determine what *kind* of gradient you are dealing with. We read graphs from left to right, just like reading a book.

## 1. Finding the Gradient from a Graph (Rise over Run)

If you are given a graph of a straight line, you can find its gradient by drawing a right-angled triangle between two points on the line. We use the phrase **"Rise over Run"**:

$$
m = \frac{\text{Vertical Change (Rise)}}{\text{Horizontal Change (Run)}}
$$

**How to calculate visually:**
Pick two points where the line crosses grid corners perfectly ($A$ and $B$).
1. Count the spaces horizontally for **Run** (move right $\rightarrow$ positive). $\text{Run} = 8$.
2. Count the spaces vertically for **Rise** (move up $\rightarrow$ positive). $\text{Rise} = 4$.
3. Calculate the gradient: $m = \frac{4}{8} = 0.5$.

## 2. Calculating the Gradient from Two Coordinates

Often, you won't have a graph. You will just be given the coordinates of two points, such as $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$. We calculate the "Rise over Run" using algebra.

**The Gradient Formula:**
$$
m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}
$$

<Aside type="caution" title="Crucial Gradient Rules">
* **Simplifying:** A gradient is just a fraction. Always simplify it! If you calculate $\frac{4}{2}$, write your final answer as $2$. If you get $\frac{3}{6}$, simplify it to $\frac{1}{2}$.
* **The Floating Negative:** If your gradient is negative, it doesn't matter where the minus sign lives: $-\frac{3}{4} = \frac{-3}{4} = \frac{3}{-4}$. Usually, we place it neatly in front of the fraction.
* **Working Backwards:** The formula works perfectly backwards too: $m = \frac{y_1 - y_2}{x_1 - x_2}$. Just **never mix the order**. If you start with point 1 on top, you MUST start with point 1 on the bottom!
* **Subtracting Negatives:** Be incredibly careful when subtracting a negative coordinate. Remember that minus a minus becomes a plus: $5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8$.
</Aside>

**Calculation steps:**
Let $(x_1, y_1) = (-3, 4)$ and $(x_2, y_2) = (2, -1)$.
$$
\begin{aligned}
m &= \frac{-1 - 4}{2 - (-3)} \\
m &= \frac{-5}{2 + 3} \\
m &= \frac{-5}{5} \\
\mathbf{m} &\mathbf{= -1}
\end{aligned}
$$

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Visually Interpreting a Graph">
    A straight line graph is shown. State its gradient.
    [Graph showing point A(0, 1) and B(3, 10)]
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    Pick two points that cross grid corners perfectly.
    Point $A(0, 1)$ is the y-intercept. Let's pick another clear corner, $B(3, 10)$.
    
    1. Count **Run** from $A$ to $B$ (move right 3): $\text{Run} = 3$.
    2. Count **Rise** from $A$ to $B$ (move up from 1 to 10): $\text{Rise} = 9$.
    
    $$m = \frac{\text{Rise}}{\text{Run}} = \frac{9}{3} = 3$$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
