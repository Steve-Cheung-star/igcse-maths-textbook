Understanding how to calculate the perimeter and area of 2D shapes is a core skill in geometry. In the IGCSE Extended exam, you will not be given the formulas for rectangles, parallelograms, or trapeziums—you must memorise them! 

<Aside type="note" title="Perimeter vs. Area">
* **Perimeter:** The total distance *around the outside* of a 2D shape. Imagine walking along the boundary. It is measured in 1D units (e.g., $\text{cm}$, $\text{m}$).
* **Area:** The amount of flat space *inside* the shape. Imagine painting the surface. It is measured in 2D squared units (e.g., $\text{cm}^2$, $\text{m}^2$).
</Aside>

<SteveTip title="The Perpendicular Height Rule">
For areas of triangles, parallelograms, and trapeziums, you must **always** use the perpendicular height (the height that meets the base at exactly $90^\circ$). Examiners will frequently give you the "slant height" as a trap. Do not use the slanted edge for area!
</SteveTip>

## 1. Rectangles and Triangles

These are your foundational shapes. All other area formulas are effectively derived from the rectangle.

### The Rectangle
* **Perimeter:** Add all four sides together: $2 \times \text{base} + 2 \times \text{height}$
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{height}
  $$

### The Triangle
Every triangle is exactly half of a rectangle (or half of a parallelogram).
* **Perimeter:** Simply add the lengths of the three outer sides.
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}
  $$

## 2. Parallelograms

A parallelogram is like a rectangle that has been pushed over. If you chop off the triangular end of a parallelogram and move it to the other side, it forms a perfect rectangle. 

Because of this, the area formula is exactly the same as a rectangle, provided you use the **perpendicular height**.

* **Perimeter:** Add the four outer sides ($2 \times \text{base} + 2 \times \text{slant edge}$).
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}
  $$

## 3. Trapeziums

A trapezium is a quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides. 
To find the area, we take the **average of the two parallel sides** and multiply it by the **perpendicular height**.

* **Perimeter:** Add the four outer sides together.
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (a + b) \times h
  $$
  *(where $a$ and $b$ are the parallel sides, and $h$ is the perpendicular height).*

## Practice Questions

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: The Parallelogram Trap (Core)">
    A parallelogram has a base of $12\text{ cm}$. Its slanted edge measures $7\text{ cm}$, and its perpendicular height is $5\text{ cm}$.
    1. Calculate the perimeter of the parallelogram.
    2. Calculate the area of the parallelogram.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    <Steps>
    1. **Perimeter:** The perimeter requires the lengths of the outer boundaries. A parallelogram has two pairs of equal opposite sides (two bases of 12, two slanted edges of 7).
       $$
       \text{Perimeter} = 12 + 12 + 7 + 7 = 38\text{ cm}
       $$
    2. **Area:** The area requires the base and the *perpendicular* height. We ignore the slanted edge of 7cm for this calculation!
       $$
       \text{Area} = 12 \times 5 = 60\text{ cm}^2
       $$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Trapezium Area (Core)">
    The parallel sides of a trapezium are $8\text{ m}$ and $14\text{ m}$. The perpendicular distance between them is $6\text{ m}$. 
    Calculate the area of the trapezium.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    <Steps>
    1. Identify the values: $a = 8$, $b = 14$, $h = 6$.
    2. Write down the trapezium area formula:
       $$
       \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (a + b) \times h
       $$
    3. Substitute the values into the formula:
       $$
       \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (8 + 14) \times 6
       $$
    4. Calculate the brackets first (BIDMAS):
       $$
       \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (22) \times 6
       $$
    5. Complete the multiplication:
       $$
       \text{Area} = 11 \times 6 = 66\text{ m}^2
       $$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Working Backwards (Extended)">
    The area of a triangle is $45\text{ cm}^2$. Its perpendicular height is $9\text{ cm}$. 
    Calculate the length of its base.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    <Steps>
    1. Write down the formula for the area of a triangle:
       $$
       \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}
       $$
    2. Substitute the known values into the equation:
       $$
       45 = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times 9
       $$
    3. Multiply both sides by 2 to remove the fraction:
       $$
       90 = \text{base} \times 9
       $$
    4. Divide by 9 to isolate the base:
       $$
       \text{base} = 90 \div 9 = 10\text{ cm}
       $$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

    The diagram shows the side profile of a shed consisting of a rectangle and a triangle on top. 
    The total width of the shed is $12\text{ m}$. The height of the rectangular section is $8\text{ m}$, and the perpendicular height of the triangular roof section is $4\text{ m}$.
    Calculate the total area of the profile.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    <Steps>
    1. **Split the shape:** The shape is composed of a rectangle at the bottom and a triangle at the top. We calculate their areas separately and add them together.
    2. **Area of the Rectangle:**
       $$
       \text{Area}_{\text{rect}} = \text{base} \times \text{height}
       $$
       $$
       \text{Area}_{\text{rect}} = 12 \times 8 = 96\text{ m}^2
       $$
    3. **Area of the Triangle:** The base of the triangle is the same as the width of the rectangle ($12\text{ m}$).
       $$
       \text{Area}_{\text{tri}} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}
       $$
       $$
       \text{Area}_{\text{tri}} = \frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 4 = 24\text{ m}^2
       $$
    4. **Total Area:**
       $$
       \text{Total Area} = 96 + 24 = 120\text{ m}^2
       $$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
