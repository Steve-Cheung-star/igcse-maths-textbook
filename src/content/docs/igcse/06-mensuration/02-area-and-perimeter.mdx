Understanding how to calculate the perimeter and area of 2D shapes is a core skill in geometry. In the IGCSE Extended exam, you will not be given the formulas for rectangles, parallelograms, or trapeziums—you must memorise them! 

<Aside type="note" title="Perimeter vs. Area">
* **Perimeter:** The total distance *around the outside* of a 2D shape. Think of **Perimeter** as the edge of the storm circle closing in on you in a battle royale. It is measured in 1D units (e.g., $\text{cm}$, $\text{m}$).
* **Area:** The amount of flat space *inside* the shape. The **Area** is the actual safe, playable space you have left to run around, loot, and build in. It is measured in 2D squared units (e.g., $\text{cm}^2$, $\text{m}^2$).
</Aside>

<SteveTip title="The Perpendicular Height Rule">
For areas of triangles, parallelograms, and trapeziums, you must **always** use the perpendicular height (the height that meets the base at exactly $90^\circ$). Examiners will frequently give you the "slant height" as a trap. Falling for the slant-height trap is like expanding your domain without a cursed technique—a complete waste of energy (and marks). Do not use the slanted edge for area!
</SteveTip>

## 1. Rectangles and Triangles

These are your foundational shapes. All other area formulas are effectively derived from the rectangle.

### The Rectangle
* **Perimeter:** Add all four sides together: $2 \times \text{base} + 2 \times \text{height}$
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{height}
  $$

### The Triangle
Every triangle is exactly half of a rectangle (or half of a parallelogram).
* **Perimeter:** Simply add the lengths of the three outer sides.
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}
  $$

## 2. Parallelograms

A parallelogram is like a rectangle that has been pushed over. If you chop off the triangular end of a parallelogram and move it to the other side, it forms a perfect rectangle. It’s perfectly balanced, as all things should be. You just snap the left triangle out of existence and materialize it on the right.

Because of this, the area formula is exactly the same as a rectangle, provided you use the **perpendicular height**.

* **Perimeter:** Add the four outer sides ($2 \times \text{base} + 2 \times \text{slant edge}$).
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}
  $$

## 3. Trapeziums

A trapezium is a quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides. 
To find the area, we take the **average of the two parallel sides** and multiply it by the **perpendicular height**.

* **Perimeter:** Add the four outer sides together.
* **Area:**
  $$
  \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (\text{top} + \text{bottom}) \times h
  $$
  *(where $h$ is the perpendicular height).*

    a) Calculate the area of a rectangle that has a length of $18\text{ cm}$ and a width of $7\text{ cm}$.

    b) Calculate the perimeter of this same rectangle.

    c) A triangle has a base of $14\text{ m}$ and a perpendicular height of $9\text{ m}$. Calculate its area.

    d) **(Pythagoras)** Calculate the exact length of the diagonal of the rectangle from part (a).

  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    **a)**

    <Steps>
      1. Use the formula: $\text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{height}$.
      2. $\text{Area} = 18 \times 7$.
      3. **Area = $126\text{ cm}^2$**.
    </Steps>

    **b)**

    <Steps>
      1. Use the formula: $\text{Perimeter} = 2 \times \text{length} + 2 \times \text{width}$.
      2. $\text{Perimeter} = (2 \times 18) + (2 \times 7) = 36 + 14$.
      3. **Perimeter = $50\text{ cm}$**.
    </Steps>

    **c)**

    <Steps>
      1. Use the formula: $\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}$.
      2. $\text{Area} = 0.5 \times 14 \times 9$.
      3. $\text{Area} = 7 \times 9$.
      4. **Area = $63\text{ m}^2$**.
    </Steps>

    **d)**

    <Steps>
      1. The diagonal splits the rectangle into two right-angled triangles with sides $18$ and $7$.
      2. Pythagoras: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.
      3. $18^2 + 7^2 = c^2 \implies 324 + 49 = c^2$.
      4. $c^2 = 373$.
      5. **Diagonal = $\sqrt{373}\text{ cm}$** (or approx $19.31\text{ cm}$).
    </Steps>

  </TabItem>

    A parallelogram has a base of $15\text{ cm}$. The slanted edge is $10\text{ cm}$ and the internal perpendicular height is $8\text{ cm}$.
    
    a) Calculate the total perimeter of the parallelogram.

    b) Calculate the area of the parallelogram.

    c) If the area is kept exactly the same, but the base is extended to $20\text{ cm}$, what must the new perpendicular height be?

    d) **(Trigonometry)** Calculate the internal base angle formed between the $15\text{ cm}$ base and the $10\text{ cm}$ slanted edge.

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    **a)**

    <Steps>
      1. Perimeter is the total distance around the outside. We use the base and the slanted edges.
      2. $\text{Perimeter} = 15 + 15 + 10 + 10$.
      3. **Perimeter = $50\text{ cm}$**.
    </Steps>

    **b)**

    <Steps>
      1. The area of a parallelogram uses the base and the *perpendicular* height. Ignore the slanted edge of $10\text{ cm}$.
      2. $\text{Area} = 15 \times 8$.
      3. **Area = $120\text{ cm}^2$**.
    </Steps>

    **c)**

    <Steps>
      1. We know the area must remain $120\text{ cm}^2$ and the new base is $20\text{ cm}$.
      2. Set up the equation: $120 = 20 \times \text{height}$.
      3. Divide both sides by $20$: $\text{height} = 120 \div 20$.
      4. **New height = $6\text{ cm}$**.
    </Steps>

    **d)**

    <Steps>
      1. The perpendicular height forms a right-angled triangle with the slanted edge as the hypotenuse.
      2. The opposite side is $8\text{ cm}$ and the hypotenuse is $10\text{ cm}$.
      3. Use Sine (SOH): $\sin(\theta) = \frac{8}{10} = 0.8$.
      4. $\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.8)$.
      5. **Base angle = $53.1^\circ$**.
    </Steps>

  </TabItem>

      4. $\theta = \tan^{-1}(2.333)$.

    A triangle is plotted on a coordinate grid with vertices at $A(2, 2)$, $B(10, 2)$, and $C(6, 8)$. Each discrete integer unit on the axes represents exactly $1\text{ cm}$.

    b) Determine the exact perpendicular height of the triangle.

    c) Calculate the total area of triangle $ABC$.

  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 7">
    **a)**

    <Steps>
      1. The base $AB$ is perfectly horizontal, lying on the line $y = 2$.
      2. We simply subtract the $x$-coordinates: $10 - 2 = 8$.
      3. **Base length = $8\text{ cm}$**.
    </Steps>

    **b)**

    <Steps>
      1. The perpendicular height is measured straight down from the peak $C(6, 8)$ to the base line at $y = 2$.
      2. Subtract the $y$-coordinates: $8 - 2 = 6$.
      3. **Height = $6\text{ cm}$**.
    </Steps>

    **c)**

    <Steps>
      1. Now use the standard formula for the area of a triangle.
      2. $\text{Area} = 0.5 \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$.
      3. $\text{Area} = 0.5 \times 8 \times 6 = 24$.
      4. **Area = $24\text{ cm}^2$**.
    </Steps>
