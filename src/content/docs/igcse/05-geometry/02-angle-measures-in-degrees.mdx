Geometry often requires precision and a clear understanding of direction. Whether you are constructing shapes or navigating ships across the ocean, angles and bearings are the mathematical tools you need to describe orientation accurately.

## 1. Measuring and Drawing Lines and Angles

Before moving to complex navigation problems, you must be completely confident in using your geometrical instruments: the ruler and the protractor.

<SteveTip title="Straight Edges Must Be Ruled">
In the exam, a ruler **must** be used for all straight edges. Never attempt to draw a line freehand, even if it is a sketch or a short connecting line. Examiners will check for straightness, and you will lose marks for sloppy drawings.
</SteveTip>

<Aside type="caution" title="The Protractor Trap">
Protractors have two sets of numbers (an inner scale and an outer scale). A common misconception is reading from the wrong scale. Always start at the $0^\circ$ mark on the line you are measuring from, and count upwards. If you are measuring an acute angle, ensure your final reading is less than $90^\circ$.
</Aside>

## 2. Understanding Bearings

Bearings are a real-world application of angles used in navigation by pilots, sailors, and hikers to describe a continuous direction.

<Aside type="tip" title="The Three Rules of Bearings">
To correctly measure or write a bearing, you must follow these three strict rules:
1. Always measure from the **North** line.
2. Always measure **clockwise**.
3. Always write your answer using **three figures** (e.g., write $045^\circ$, not $45^\circ$).
</Aside>

<Aside type="note" title="Anatomy of the Compass">
You must understand the standard compass points and their corresponding bearings:
* **North (N):** $000^\circ$ (or $360^\circ$)
* **East (E):** $090^\circ$
* **South (S):** $180^\circ$
* **West (W):** $270^\circ$
</Aside>

If a question states "point $D$ is due east of point $C$", it means the bearing of $D$ from $C$ is exactly $090^\circ$.

### Reverse Bearings (Back Bearings)

Often, an exam question will give you the bearing of one point from another and ask you to find the reverse direction.

**Example:** Find the bearing of $A$ from $B$ if the bearing of $B$ from $A$ is $025^\circ$.

<SteveTip title="The Reverse Bearing Rule">
Because North lines are always parallel, we can use the "allied angles" (co-interior angles) rule which add up to $180^\circ$. 
* If the given bearing is **less than $180^\circ$**, simply add $180^\circ$.
* If the given bearing is **more than $180^\circ$**, simply subtract $180^\circ$.
</SteveTip>

In our example, $025^\circ$ is less than $180^\circ$, so:
$$
\text{Bearing of } A \text{ from } B = 025^\circ + 180^\circ = 205^\circ
$$

      2. Since $030^\circ < 180^\circ$, add $180^\circ$.

  <TabItem label="đź“ť Question 5: Algebraic Bearings">
    The bearing of a ship $S$ from a port $P$ is represented by the expression $(3x + 10)^\circ$. 
    The reverse bearing of the port $P$ from the ship $S$ is given by the expression $(8x - 20)^\circ$.
    Assume the initial bearing of $S$ from $P$ is less than $180^\circ$.
    
    a) Write down an algebraic equation connecting these two bearings.

    b) Solve your equation to find the value of $x$.

    c) Calculate the exact three-figure bearing of the ship $S$ from the port $P$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="âś… Solution 5">
    **a)**
    <Steps>
      1. The rule for reverse bearings when the initial bearing is less than $180^\circ$ is: $\text{Reverse Bearing} = \text{Initial Bearing} + 180^\circ$.
      2. Substitute the algebraic expressions: $(8x - 20) = (3x + 10) + 180$.
      3. **Answer:** $8x - 20 = 3x + 190$
    </Steps>

    **b)**
    <Steps>
      1. Start with the simplified equation: $8x - 20 = 3x + 190$.
      2. Subtract $3x$ from both sides: $5x - 20 = 190$.
      3. Add $20$ to both sides: $5x = 210$.
      4. Divide by $5$: $x = 42$.
      5. **Answer:** $x = 42$
    </Steps>

    **c)**
    <Steps>
      1. Substitute $x = 42$ back into the expression for the initial bearing $(3x + 10)^\circ$.
      2. $3(42) + 10 = 126 + 10 = 136^\circ$.
      3. Check using the three-figure format.
      4. **Answer:** $136^\circ$
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

      4. From North ($000^\circ$) to the waterfall is another $40^\circ$.
      5. Total angle $= 30^\circ + 40^\circ = 70^\circ$.
