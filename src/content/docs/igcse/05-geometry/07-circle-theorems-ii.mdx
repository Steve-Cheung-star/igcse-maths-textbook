## 1. Equal Chords are Equidistant from the Centre

A chord is a straight line drawn between any two points on the circumference. Because a circle is perfectly round, chords that are exactly the same length will always be the exact same distance from the centre.



<Aside type="tip" title="Theorem 1: Equal Chords">
  **Equal chords are equidistant from the centre**.
  Conversely, if two chords are the same distance from the centre, they must be equal in length.
</Aside>

<Aside type="tip" title="Theorem 2: Perpendicular Bisector">
  **The perpendicular bisector of a chord passes through the centre**.
  * **Rule A:** A line from the centre perpendicular to a chord bisects the chord.
  * **Rule B:** A line from the centre that bisects a chord is perpendicular to the chord.
</Aside>

<Aside type="caution" title="The Pythagoras Connection">
  This theorem is almost *always* paired with **Pythagoras' Theorem** in exams. The radius, half the chord, and the perpendicular distance to the centre form a perfect right-angled triangle.
</Aside>

## 3. Tangents from an External Point

If you pick a random point anywhere outside a circle and draw straight lines to the circle, exactly two lines will act as tangents (touching the edge perfectly). Because of the circle's symmetry, these two lines will be a mirror image of each other.

<Aside type="tip" title="Theorem 3: Tangents from a Point">
  **Tangents from an external point are equal in length**.
</Aside>

<Aside type="note" title="The Tangent Kite">
  When you draw two tangents from an external point and connect their touching points to the centre with radii, you create a **kite**.
  * The tangents are equal.
  * The radii are equal.
  * The angle between the tangent and radius is $90^\circ$.
  * A line drawn from the centre to the external point slices the kite into **two identical right-angled triangles**.
</Aside>

**Example Task:** A chord of length $30\text{cm}$ is drawn in a circle with centre $O$. The perpendicular distance from $O$ to the chord is $8\text{cm}$. Calculate the radius of the circle, $r$.

**Solution:**
<Steps>
  1. **Apply the theorem:** The perpendicular line from the centre bisects the chord. 
     Half of the chord $= 30\text{cm} \div 2 = 15\text{cm}$.
     *Reason:* The perpendicular bisector of a chord passes through the centre.
  2. **Set up Pythagoras:** A right-angled triangle is formed by the radius (hypotenuse), the $8\text{cm}$ distance, and the $15\text{cm}$ half-chord.
     $$
     r^2 = 8^2 + 15^2
     $$
  3. **Solve:**
     $$
     r^2 = 64 + 225
     $$
     $$
     r^2 = 289
     $$
     $$
     r = \sqrt{289} = 17\text{cm}
     $$
</Steps>

    b) Calculate the perpendicular distance from the centre $O$ to chord $AB$.

    d) Calculate the total perpendicular distance exactly between the two parallel chords $AB$ and $CD$.

  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">

      2. Form a right-angled triangle with radius $13\text{cm}$.

      4. **Distance to $CD = 12\text{cm}$.**
