A **rate** is a way of comparing two different quantities (like distance and time, or money and hours). In mathematics, the word "per" almost always means **divide**.

---

## 1. Common Measures of Rate

A rate tells you how much of one thing happens for every one unit of another. The units themselves usually tell you the formula you need to use.

| Rate Type | Common Units | Calculation Logic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hourly Pay** | GBP/hr | Total Pay ÷ Hours worked |
| **Exchange Rate** | USD/EUR | Currency A ÷ Currency B |
| **Fuel Consumption** | km/L | Distance ÷ Fuel used |
| **Flow Rate** | L/sec | Volume ÷ Time |
| **Density** | g/cm³ | Mass ÷ Volume |

<Aside type="note" title="Reading the Units">
The forward slash (/) in units like **m/s** is literally a division sign. It tells you to take the **metres** and divide them by the **seconds**. If you forget a formula in the exam, just look at the units in the question!
</Aside>

## 2. Speed, Distance, and Time

Speed is the rate at which distance changes over time. You must be able to rearrange the relationship between these three variables.

### The Core Formulae
* $$ \text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} $$
* $$ \text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} $$
* $$ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} $$

### The m/s to km/h Shortcut
In the exam, you often need to convert between these two. Instead of doing three separate conversions (metres to km, seconds to mins, mins to hours), use the **3.6 Rule**:

* **To go from m/s to km/h:** Multiply by **3.6**.
* **To go from km/h to m/s:** Divide by **3.6**.

> **Why 3.6?** There are **3600** seconds in an hour and **1000** metres in a km. $3600 \div 1000 = 3.6$.

### Entering the Formula (Minutes to Decimal)
If the question gives you **2 hours 15 mins**, you must convert to a decimal before multiplying or dividing:
15 ÷ 60 = 0.25 $\implies$ **2.25 hours**

### Exiting the Formula (Decimal to Minutes)
If your calculation gives you a time like **4.3 hours**, this is **NOT** 4 hours and 3 minutes.
1. **The "Whole" is hours:** **4 hours**.
2. **The decimal is minutes:** Multiply the decimal part by 60.
3. 0.3 × 60 = **18 minutes**.
**Answer:** **4 hours and 18 minutes**.

<SteveTip title="The 0.45 Illusion">
Students often write **1.45** for **1 hour 45 mins**. This is a guaranteed way to lose the method mark. Always ask: "Is 45 minutes nearly an hour?" Yes. So the decimal must be **0.75**. If you find yourself writing the minutes exactly as the decimal, stop and divide by 60!
</SteveTip>

## 4. Average Speed

Average speed is **not** just the mean of two speeds. It must be calculated using the total distance and total time for the entire journey.

$$ \text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} $$

**Example from Syllabus:** A cyclist travels 45 km in 3 hours 45 minutes. What is their average speed?
1. Convert the time to a decimal: 3 hours + (45 ÷ 60) hours = **3.75 hours**.
2. Apply the formula: 45 ÷ 3.75 = **12 km/h**.

## 5. Speed Equations & Algebra

### Linear Speed Equations
Sometimes you are told a journey takes **20 minutes** longer if the speed is reduced by **10 km/h**. You set this up by making the **Time** the subject:
$$ \text{Time}_1 - \text{Time}_2 = \frac{20}{60} $$

### Quadratic Speed Equations (Sneak Peek)
In harder Paper 4 questions, you might see something like: *"A car travels **100 km**. If the speed increases by $x$, the time taken decreases by **1 hour**."*

$$ \frac{100}{v} - \frac{100}{v+x} = 1 $$

Multiplying this out leads to a **Quadratic Equation**.
<Aside type="note" title="Coming Soon">
We will cover how to solve these equations in **Chapter 2: Algebra**. For now, focus on being able to write the initial fraction correctly!
</Aside>

<SteveTip title="The Difference Equation">
When setting up time difference equations, always do:
**[Slow Time] - [Fast Time] = [Time Difference]**
Since the slower speed takes *more* time, it must come first to get a positive result.
</SteveTip>

## 6. Practice Problems

### Part A: Speed and Time Conversions

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Speed & Time">
    * **a)** A wind turbine spins such that the tip of the blade moves at **15 m/s**. Calculate this speed in **km/h**.
    * **b)** A journey of **15 km** takes **20 minutes**. Find the average speed in **km/h**.
    * **c)** A train travels **154 km** at an average speed of **70 km/h**. Calculate the time taken in **hours and minutes**.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    * **a)** Use the 3.6 shortcut: 15 × 3.6 = **54 km/h**.
    * **b)** Convert minutes to decimal: 20 ÷ 60 = 1/3 hours. Speed: 15 ÷ (1/3) = **45 km/h**.
    * **c)** Time: 154 ÷ 70 = 2.2 hours. Convert decimal: 0.2 × 60 = 12 minutes. **Answer: 2 hours 12 minutes**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part B: Average Speed & Multi-Stage Journeys

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Multi-Part Journeys">
    * **a)** A car travels **80 km** at **40 km/h**, stops for a **30-minute** break, and then travels **120 km** at **60 km/h**. Calculate the average speed for the entire journey.
    * **b)** A runner completes a **5 km** race. They run the first **3 km** at **12 km/h** and the remaining **2 km** at **10 km/h**. What is their average speed for the whole race?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    * **a)** Total Distance: 80 + 120 = 200 km. <br/>
       Time 1: 80 ÷ 40 = 2 hours. Stop Time: 0.5 hours. Time 2: 120 ÷ 60 = 2 hours. <br/>
       Total Time = 2 + 0.5 + 2 = 4.5 hours. <br/>
       Average Speed = 200 ÷ 4.5 = **44.4 km/h** (to 1 d.p.).
    * **b)** Total Distance: 5 km. <br/>
       Time 1: 3 ÷ 12 = 0.25 hours. Time 2: 2 ÷ 10 = 0.2 hours. <br/>
       Total Time = 0.25 + 0.2 = 0.45 hours. <br/>
       Average Speed = 5 ÷ 0.45 = **11.1 km/h** (to 1 d.p.).
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part C: Real-World Rates (Pay, Fuel, Flow)

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Everyday Rates">
    * **a)** An apprentice earns **£8.50** per hour. They work **38 hours** a week. Calculate their annual pay (assume 52 weeks in a year).
    * **b)** A car travels **630 km** and uses **45 litres** of fuel. Calculate its fuel consumption in **km/L**.
    * **c)** A tap fills a **500 ml** jug in **4 seconds**. How long will it take to fill a **12 litre** bucket? Give your answer in minutes and seconds.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    * **a)** Weekly pay: 8.50 × 38 = £323. Annual pay: 323 × 52 = **£16,796**.
    * **b)** Fuel consumption: Distance ÷ Fuel = 630 ÷ 45 = **14 km/L**.
    * **c)** Flow rate: 500 ÷ 4 = 125 ml/sec. Volume: 12 Litres = 12,000 ml. Time: 12,000 ÷ 125 = 96 seconds. **Answer: 1 minute 36 seconds**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part D: Exchange Rates & Density

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Currencies and Mass">
    * **a)** A tourist changes **£450** into Euros (€) when the exchange rate is **£1 = €1.15**. How many Euros do they receive?
    * **b)** Later, they change **€85** back to Pounds when the rate is **£1 = €1.18**. How many Pounds do they get?
    * **c)** A metal block has a mass of **420 g** and a volume of **60 cm³**. Calculate its density.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    * **a)** £ to € (multiplying by the rate): 450 × 1.15 = **€517.50**.
    * **b)** € to £ (dividing by the rate): 85 ÷ 1.18 = **£72.03** (to 2 d.p.).
    * **c)** Density = Mass ÷ Volume = 420 ÷ 60 = **7 g/cm³**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part E: Algebra & Speed Equations

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: Speed Equations">
    * **a)** A car travels **200 km** at an average speed of **$x$ km/h**. If the speed was **$(x+10)$ km/h**, the journey would take 1 hour less. Show that $x^2 + 10x - 2000 = 0$.
    * **b)** A cyclist rides **30 km** at speed $v$. If they rode at $(v-5)$ km/h, it would take 30 minutes longer. Write down an equation in terms of $v$.
    * **c)** A runner covers **12 km** at speed $s$. If they speed up by 2 km/h, they shave 15 minutes off their time. Set up the fractional equation.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    * **a)** $\text{Time}_1 = \frac{200}{x}$, $\text{Time}_2 = \frac{200}{x+10}$.<br/>
       Equation: $\frac{200}{x} - \frac{200}{x+10} = 1$.<br/>
       Multiply by $x(x+10)$: $200(x+10) - 200x = x^2 + 10x \implies$ **$x^2 + 10x - 2000 = 0$**.
    * **b)** Slow Time - Fast Time = Diff. (Note: 30 mins = 0.5 hours).<br/>
       **$\frac{30}{v-5} - \frac{30}{v} = 0.5$**
    * **c)** Slow Time - Fast Time = Diff. (Note: 15 mins = 0.25 or 1/4 hours).<br/>
       **$\frac{12}{s} - \frac{12}{s+2} = 0.25$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
