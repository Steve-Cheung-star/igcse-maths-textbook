Ratio and proportion are all about the **relationship** between quantities. Whether you are scaling up a recipe for a party, converting currencies, or calculating distances on a map, the core skill is understanding how "parts" relate to the "whole."

---

## 1. Simplifying Ratios

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind. Just like a fraction, a ratio is most useful when given in its **simplest form**. To simplify, divide all parts of the ratio by their **Highest Common Factor (HCF)**.

* **Example:** Simplify the ratio 20 : 30 : 40.
    1.  Find the largest number that divides into 20, 30, and 40. That number is 10.
    2.  Divide each part: 20 ÷ 10 = 2, 30 ÷ 10 = 3, and 40 ÷ 10 = 4.
    **Answer:** 2 : 3 : 4

<Aside type="caution" title="The Units Trap">
Before you can simplify a ratio, the units **must** be the same. You cannot simplify 50p : £2 until they are both in the same unit.
* **Incorrect:** 50 : 2 = 25 : 1
* **Correct:** 50p : 200p = 1 : 4
</Aside>

    * a : b = 2 : 3

### How to Convert
* **Ratio to Fraction:** The denominator is the **sum** of the ratio parts.
    * If the ratio of red to blue is 3 : 5, then the fraction of red is 3/8.
* **Fraction to Ratio:** The numerator is the first part, and the (denominator - numerator) is the second part.
    * If 2/7 of a cake is eaten, the ratio of **Eaten : Left** is 2 : (7 - 2) = 2 : 5.

<SteveTip title="The 'Whole' Mistake">
Students often see 2/5 and write the ratio 2 : 5. **This is wrong!**
2/5 means 2 parts out of a **total** of 5. That leaves 3 parts over.
The correct ratio is 2 : 3. Always check: do your ratio parts add up to the fraction's denominator?
</SteveTip>

### The "Share" Method
**Example: Divide £200 in the ratio 7 : 3.**

1.  **Add the parts:** Find the total number of "shares."
    7 + 3 = 10 shares
2.  **Divide the total:** Find out how much **one share** is worth.
    £200 ÷ 10 = £20 per share
3.  **Multiply back:** Multiply each part of the ratio by the value of one share.
    7 × £20 = £140 and 3 × £20 = £60
**Answer:** £140 and £60.

## 5. Proportional Reasoning in Context

### A. Adapting Recipes (The Unitary Method)
Find the amount needed for **one** unit first, then scale up.
* **Example:** A recipe for 4 people uses 300g of flour. For 7 people:
    1.  300g ÷ 4 = 75g (for 1 person).
    2.  75g × 7 = 525g (for 7 people).

### B. Map Scales (1 : n)
1 unit on the map represents n units in real life. Keep units identical on both sides during the first calculation!
* **Example:** Scale 1 : 50,000. Map distance 4cm.
    1.  4cm × 50,000 = 200,000cm.
    2.  200,000 ÷ 100 = 2,000m = 2km.

### C. Best Value
Compare the cost of the **same amount** (e.g., price per 100g or per 1kg).
* **Pack A:** 500g for £2.50 $\rightarrow$ £0.50 per 100g
* **Pack B:** 700g for £3.36 $\rightarrow$ £0.48 per 100g (Winner!)

### Part A: Conversions & Continuous Ratios

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Simplifying & Units">
    Simplify the following ratios into their simplest form:
    * **a)** $12 : 28 : 40$
    * **b)** $1.5\text{kg} : 500\text{g}$
    * **c)** $45\text{ minutes} : 2\text{ hours}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    * **a)** HCF is 4. Divide all parts by 4: **$3 : 7 : 10$**
    * **b)** Convert to grams: $1500\text{g} : 500\text{g}$. Divide by 500: **$3 : 1$**
    * **c)** Convert to minutes: $45 : 120$. Divide by 15: **$3 : 8$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: The Alphabetical Bridge">
    Find the combined ratio $a : b : c$ for the following:
    * **a)** $a : b = 2 : 3$ and $b : c = 4 : 5$
    * **b)** $a : c = 1 : 3$ and $b : c = 2 : 5$
    * **c)** $a : b = 10 : 3$ and $a : c = 4 : 7$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    * **a)** $b$ is the bridge. LCM of 3 and 4 is 12. Row 1 (×4), Row 2 (×3). **$8 : 12 : 15$**
    * **b)** $c$ is the bridge. LCM of 3 and 5 is 15. Row 1 (×5), Row 2 (×3). **$5 : 6 : 15$**
    * **c)** $a$ is the bridge. LCM of 10 and 4 is 20. Row 1 (×2), Row 2 (×5). **$20 : 6 : 35$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: The Fraction-Ratio Link">
    * **a)** In a bag of sweets, $\frac{3}{10}$ are lemon. What is the ratio of **Lemon : Other**?
    * **b)** The ratio of blue to red pens is $4 : 5$. What **fraction** of the pens are red?
    * **c)** $\frac{2}{9}$ of a group are adults. What is the ratio of **Adults : Children**?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    * **a)** Total is 10 parts. Lemon is 3, so Others = $10 - 3 = 7$. **$3 : 7$**
    * **b)** Total parts = $4 + 5 = 9$. Red is 5 parts. **$5/9$**
    * **c)** Total is 9 parts. Adults is 2, so Children = $9 - 2 = 7$. **$2 : 7$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part B: Dividing & The "Given One Part" Trick

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Sharing a Total">
    Share £450 in the ratio 2 : 3 : 4. Find the difference between the largest and smallest shares.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    1. Total parts: 2 + 3 + 4 = 9.
    2. One part: £450 ÷ 9 = £50.
    3. Smallest: 2 × 50 = £100. Largest: 4 × 50 = £200.
    4. Difference: 200 - 100 = 100.
    **Answer:** £100
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: The 'Given One Part' Trap">
    The ratio of red marbles to blue marbles in a bag is 4 : 7. If there are 28 red marbles, how many marbles are there in **total**?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    1. We know the 4 "red" parts are equal to 28 marbles.
    2. Find the value of 1 part: 28 ÷ 4 = 7 marbles.
    3. Find the total number of parts: 4 + 7 = 11 parts.
    4. Multiply by 1 part: 11 × 7 = 77.
    **Answer:** 77 marbles
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part C: Proportional Reasoning in Context

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 6: Map Scale Inverse">
    A map scale is 1 : 200,000. A forest is represented by a line 4.5cm long on the map. Find the actual length of the forest in kilometres.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 6">
    1. 4.5cm × 200,000 = 900,000cm.
    2. Convert to m: 900,000 ÷ 100 = 9,000m.
    3. Convert to km: 9,000 ÷ 1000 = 9km.
    **Answer:** 9km
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 7: Adapting Recipes">
    A recipe for 6 cupcakes requires 150g of sugar. How much sugar is needed to make 15 cupcakes?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 7">
    1. Use the Unitary Method. Find the sugar for 1 cupcake: 150g ÷ 6 = 25g.
    2. Multiply by the new amount: 25g × 15 = 375g.
    **Answer:** 375g
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 8: Best Value">
    Coffee is sold in two sizes:
    * **Regular:** 200g for £4.50
    * **Large:** 350g for £7.70
    Which size is the better value for money? You must show your working.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 8">
    1. Find the cost per 100g for both to compare fairly.
    2. **Regular:** £4.50 ÷ 2 = £2.25 per 100g.
    3. **Large:** £7.70 ÷ 3.5 = £2.20 per 100g.
    4. Since £2.20 is less than £2.25, the Large size is cheaper per gram.
    **Answer:** The Large size is better value.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
