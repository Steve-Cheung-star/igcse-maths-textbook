Ratio and proportion are all about the **relationship** between quantities. Whether you are scaling up a recipe for a party, converting currencies, or calculating distances on a map, the core skill is understanding how "parts" relate to the "whole."

---

## 1. Simplifying Ratios

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind. Just like a fraction, a ratio is most useful when given in its **simplest form**. To simplify, divide all parts of the ratio by their **Highest Common Factor (HCF)**.

* **Example:** Simplify the ratio $20 : 30 : 40$.
    1.  Find the largest number that divides into 20, 30, and 40. That number is 10.
    2.  Divide each part: $20 \div 10 = 2$, $30 \div 10 = 3$, and $40 \div 10 = 4$.
    **Answer:** $2 : 3 : 4$

<Aside type="caution" title="The Units Trap">
Before you can simplify a ratio, the units **must** be the same. You cannot simplify 50p : £2 until they are both in the same unit.
* **Incorrect:** $50 : 2 = 25 : 1$
* **Correct:** $50\text{p} : 200\text{p} = 1 : 4$
</Aside>

## 2. Continuous Ratios (The Bridge)

Sometimes you are given two separate ratios that share one common item, and you need to find the relationship between the other two. To do this, you must make the **common item** have the same number of parts in both ratios using the **LCM**.

**Example: Given $a : b = 2 : 3$ and $a : c = 5 : 4$, find the ratio $b : c$.**

1.  **Identify the Link:** The letter $a$ appears in both ratios.
2.  **Find the LCM:** In the first ratio, $a=2$. In the second, $a=5$. The LCM of 2 and 5 is **10**.
3.  **Scale the Ratios:**
    * Scale $a:b$ by $5$: $(2 \times 5) : (3 \times 5) \rightarrow \mathbf{10 : 15}$
    * Scale $a:c$ by $2$: $(5 \times 2) : (4 \times 2) \rightarrow \mathbf{10 : 8}$
4.  **Combine:** Now that $a$ is the same ($10$), we can see the full relationship is $b : a : c = 15 : 10 : 8$.
**Answer:** $b : c = 15 : 8$

## 3. The Golden Bridge: Ratios vs. Fractions

In structured questions, you will often be given a fraction and asked for a ratio, or vice versa. The secret is the **Total Number of Parts**.



### How to Convert
* **Ratio to Fraction:** The denominator is the **sum** of the ratio parts.
    * If the ratio of red to blue is $3 : 5$, then the fraction of red is $\frac{3}{3+5} = \frac{3}{8}$.
* **Fraction to Ratio:** The numerator is the first part, and the (denominator - numerator) is the second part.
    * If $\frac{2}{7}$ of a cake is eaten, the ratio of **Eaten : Left** is $2 : (7-2) = 2 : 5$.

<SteveTip title="The 'Whole' Mistake">
Students often see $\frac{2}{5}$ and write the ratio $2:5$. **This is wrong!**
$\frac{2}{5}$ means 2 parts out of a **total** of 5. That leaves 3 parts over.
The correct ratio is $2:3$. Always check: do your ratio parts add up to the fraction's denominator?
</SteveTip>

## 4. Dividing a Quantity in a Given Ratio

This involves sharing a total amount into unequal parts. Remember the three-step mantra: **Add, Divide, Multiply.**

### The "Share" Method
**Example: Divide £200 in the ratio $7 : 3$.**

1.  **Add the parts:** Find the total number of "shares."
    $7 + 3 = 10 \text{ shares}$
2.  **Divide the total:** Find out how much **one share** is worth.
    £$200 \div 10 =$ £20 per share
3.  **Multiply back:** Multiply each part of the ratio by the value of one share.
    $7 \times$ £$20 =$ £140 and $3 \times$ £$20 =$ £60
**Answer:** £140 and £60.

## 5. Proportional Reasoning in Context

### A. Adapting Recipes (The Unitary Method)
Find the amount needed for **one** unit first, then scale up.
* **Example:** A recipe for 4 people uses 300g of flour. For 7 people:
    1.  $300\text{g} \div 4 = 75\text{g}$ (for 1 person).
    2.  $75\text{g} \times 7 = 525\text{g}$ (for 7 people).

### B. Map Scales ($1 : n$)
1 unit on the map represents $n$ units in real life. Keep units identical on both sides during the first calculation!
* **Example:** Scale $1 : 50,000$. Map distance 4cm.
    1.  $4\text{ cm} \times 50,000 = 200,000\text{ cm}$.
    2.  $200,000 \div 100 = 2,000\text{ m} = 2\text{ km}$.

### C. Best Value
Compare the cost of the **same amount** (e.g., price per 100g or per 1kg).
* **Pack A:** 500g for £2.50 $\rightarrow$ £0.50 per 100g
* **Pack B:** 700g for £3.36 $\rightarrow$ £0.48 per 100g (Winner!)

## 6. Practice Problems

### Part A: Conversions & Continuous Ratios
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Continuous Ratios">
    Given that $x : y = 3 : 4$ and $y : z = 2 : 5$, find the ratio $x : y : z$ in its simplest form.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    1. The common letter is $y$.
    2. In the first ratio $y=4$. In the second $y=2$.
    3. The LCM of 4 and 2 is 4. 
    4. First ratio stays: $3 : 4$.
    5. Second ratio is multiplied by 2: $(2\times 2) : (5\times 2) = 4 : 10$.
    **Answer:** $3 : 4 : 10$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: The Fraction Bridge">
    In a school, $\frac{5}{8}$ of the students are girls. Find the ratio of **Girls : Boys**.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    1. Total parts = 8.
    2. Girls = 5 parts.
    3. Boys = $8 - 5 = 3$ parts.
    **Answer:** $5 : 3$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part B: Dividing & Scaling
<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Sharing a Total">
    Share £450 in the ratio $2 : 3 : 4$. Find the difference between the largest and smallest shares.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    1. Total parts: $2 + 3 + 4 = 9$.
    2. One part: £$450 \div 9 =$ £50.
    3. Smallest: $2 \times 50 =$ £100. Largest: $4 \times 50 =$ £200.
    4. Difference: $200 - 100 = 100$.
    **Answer:** £100
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Map Scale Inverse">
    A map scale is $1 : 200,000$. A forest is represented by a line 4.5cm long on the map. Find the actual length of the forest in kilometres.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    1. $4.5\text{ cm} \times 200,000 = 900,000\text{ cm}$.
    2. Convert to m: $9,000\text{ m}$.
    3. Convert to km: $9\text{ km}$.
    **Answer:** $9\text{ km}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
