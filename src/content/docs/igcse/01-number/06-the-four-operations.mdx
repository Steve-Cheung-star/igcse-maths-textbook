In IGCSE Maths, you are expected to fluently add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers, fractions, and decimals without always relying on a calculator. A tiny slip in the order of operations or a dropped negative sign can derail an entire multi-step algebra problem later on.

---

## 1. Order of Operations (BIDMAS)

When a calculation involves multiple operations, you cannot simply read it from left to right like a book. You must follow the strict mathematical hierarchy known as **BIDMAS** (or BEDMAS).

<Aside type="note" title="The BIDMAS Hierarchy">
1. **B**rackets: Calculate anything inside brackets first.
2. **I**ndices: Evaluate powers and roots. For Extended students, this includes resolving any **negative and fractional indices** immediately!
3. **D**ivision & **M**ultiplication: Do these from left to right. They have the *same* priority.
4. **A**ddition & **S**ubtraction: Do these from left to right. They have the *same* priority.
</Aside>

*Example:* Calculate $25 - 2 \times (10 - 6)$
1. Brackets first: $10 - 6 = 4$
2. The sum is now: $25 - 2 \times 4$
3. Multiplication next: $2 \times 4 = 8$
4. Subtraction last: $25 - 8 = 17$

## 2. Calculating with Directed (Negative) Numbers

Examiners frequently test negative numbers using practical, real-world situations like temperature changes or bank overdrafts.

### Adding and Subtracting
Think of adding and subtracting like moving up and down a thermometer.
* **Adding a negative:** The temperature drops. $5 + (-3) = 5 - 3 = 2$
* **Subtracting a positive:** The temperature drops. $5 - 3 = 2$
* **Subtracting a negative:** The temperature rises! $5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8$

<SteveTip title="The Double Negative Trap">
When two negative signs are right next to each other (with no number in between), they merge into a positive. 
$$12 - - 4 \rightarrow 12 + 4 = 16$$
However, if they are separated by a number, they do NOT make a positive! 
$$-5 - 3 = -8$$ (You are at -5 degrees, and the temperature drops by another 3 degrees).
</SteveTip>

### Multiplying and Dividing
The rules here are entirely different from addition and subtraction. Do not mix them up!
* **Same signs multiply/divide to a POSITIVE:** $(-4) \times (-5) = 20$ and $(-20) \div (-4) = 5$
* **Different signs multiply/divide to a NEGATIVE:** $(-20) \div 4 = -5$ and $3 \times (-6) = -18$

<Aside type="tip" title="The Long Multiplication Chain">
If you are multiplying a long chain of negative numbers, just count the negative signs!
* An **even** number of negatives gives a **positive** answer: $(-1) \times (-2) \times (-3) \times (-4) = 24$
* An **odd** number of negatives gives a **negative** answer: $(-2) \times (-2) \times (-2) = -8$
</Aside>

## 3. Calculating with Decimals

### Addition and Subtraction
**Rule:** Always line up the decimal points!

<SteveTip title="The Phantom Zeroes Trap">
If you try to calculate $15 - 4.23$ by just writing $15$ and putting $4.23$ underneath it, you will make a borrowing mistake. If you are subtracting a decimal from a whole number, you **must** add a decimal point and placeholder zeroes so you have columns to borrow from!
$$15.00 - 4.23 = 10.77$$
</SteveTip>

### Multiplication
**Rule:** Ignore the decimals, multiply the whole numbers, then put the decimal point back.
1. $0.3 \times 0.04$
2. Ignore decimals: $3 \times 4 = 12$
3. Count total decimal places in the question: 1 place in $0.3$ + 2 places in $0.04$ = 3 total places.
4. Answer must have 3 decimal places: $0.012$

### Division
**Rule:** You cannot easily divide by a decimal. Multiply both numbers by 10, 100, or 1000 until the number you are dividing by (the divisor) is a whole integer.
* $1.2 \div 0.04$
* Multiply both by 100 to make the divisor a whole number: $120 \div 4 = 30$

## 4. Calculating with Fractions

### Addition and Subtraction
You **must** find a common denominator before you can add or subtract.
$$ \frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{5} \rightarrow \frac{5}{15} + \frac{6}{15} = \frac{11}{15} $$

### Multiplication
Multiply the top by the top (numerator) and the bottom by the bottom (denominator). 
*If you are multiplying by a whole integer, write it over 1!* ($5 \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{5}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{10}{3}$).

<Aside type="tip" title="Cross-Cancelling">
To avoid dealing with massive numbers, simplify *before* you multiply by looking for common factors diagonally.
$$ \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{8} \rightarrow \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{6} $$
</Aside>

### Division
Use the "Keep, Change, Flip" (KCF) rule. Keep the first fraction, change $\div$ to $\times$, and flip the second fraction upside down.
$$ \frac{2}{7} \div \frac{3}{5} \rightarrow \frac{2}{7} \times \frac{5}{3} = \frac{10}{21} $$
*If dividing by a whole integer, flip it to a fraction with 1 on top.* ($\frac{3}{4} \div 2 \rightarrow \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{8}$).

<SteveTip title="The Mixed Number Disaster">
If you are multiplying or dividing **mixed numbers** (e.g., $2\frac{1}{2} \times 3\frac{1}{4}$), you **MUST** convert them into improper (top-heavy) fractions first! 

Many students try to multiply the whole numbers and fractions separately ($2 \times 3$ and $\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{4}$). This is completely wrong and will score zero marks. 

**Correct Method:** 1. Convert: $\frac{5}{2} \times \frac{13}{4}$
2. Multiply: $\frac{65}{8}$
3. Convert back: $8\frac{1}{8}$
</SteveTip>

## 5. Practice Problems

### Part A: Directed Numbers & Temperature

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Practical Temperature">
    The temperature in Moscow is $-12^\circ\text{C}$. The temperature in London is $15^\circ\text{C}$ warmer. 
    What is the temperature in London?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    "Warmer" means we add the difference to the starting temperature.
    $$-12 + 15 = 3$$
    **Answer:** $3^\circ\text{C}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Negative Arithmetic">
    Work out the value of $-6 - (-4) + (-3)$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    1. Resolve the double negative: $-6 + 4 + (-3)$
    2. Adding a negative is subtraction: $-6 + 4 - 3$
    3. Work left to right: $-2 - 3 = -5$
    **Answer:** $-5$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Chain Multiplication">
    Calculate $(-3) \times (-5) \times (-2)$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    1. Count the negatives: There are three (an odd number), so the answer will be negative.
    2. Multiply the integers: $3 \times 5 \times 2 = 30$.
    **Answer:** $-30$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part B: Decimals

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Whole Number Edge Case">
    Calculate $20 - 3.45$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    1. Line up the decimals by adding placeholder zeroes to the 20: 
       $$20.00 - 3.45$$
    2. Borrow and subtract column by column.
    **Answer:** $16.55$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: Decimal Multiplication">
    Calculate $0.07 \times 0.6$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    1. Ignore decimals and multiply integers: $7 \times 6 = 42$
    2. Count decimal places in the question: 2 places in $0.07$ + 1 place in $0.6$ = 3 places total.
    3. Make 42 have 3 decimal places: $0.042$
    **Answer:** $0.042$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 6: Decimal Division">
    Work out $0.56 \div 0.08$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 6">
    1. The divisor is $0.08$. Multiply it by $100$ to make it a whole number ($8$).
    2. You must also multiply the first number by $100$: $0.56 \times 100 = 56$.
    3. The new calculation is $56 \div 8$.
    **Answer:** $7$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part C: Fractions & Mixed Numbers

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 7: Adding Fractions">
    Calculate $\frac{3}{8} + \frac{1}{6}$. Give your answer in its simplest form.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 7">
    1. Find the Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) of 8 and 6, which is 24.
    2. Convert both fractions: $\frac{3 \times 3}{8 \times 3} = \frac{9}{24}$ and $\frac{1 \times 4}{6 \times 4} = \frac{4}{24}$
    3. Add numerators: $\frac{9 + 4}{24} = \frac{13}{24}$
    **Answer:** $\frac{13}{24}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 8: Fraction by an Integer">
    Calculate $\frac{5}{12} \times 4$. Give your answer in its simplest form.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 8">
    1. Put the integer over 1: $\frac{5}{12} \times \frac{4}{1}$
    2. Cross-cancel the 4 and the 12 (divide both by 4): $\frac{5}{3} \times \frac{1}{1}$
    3. Multiply across: $\frac{5}{3}$
    4. Convert to a mixed number: $1\frac{2}{3}$
    **Answer:** $1\frac{2}{3}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 9: Mixed Number Division">
    Calculate $2\frac{1}{4} \div \frac{3}{8}$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 9">
    1. Convert mixed to improper: $\frac{9}{4} \div \frac{3}{8}$
    2. Keep, Change, Flip (KCF): $\frac{9}{4} \times \frac{8}{3}$
    3. Cross cancel: (3 goes into 9 three times, 4 goes into 8 two times).
       $$\frac{3}{1} \times \frac{2}{1} = 6$$
    **Answer:** $6$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 10: Mixed Number Subtraction (Extended)">
    Calculate $3\frac{1}{5} - 1\frac{3}{4}$. Give your answer as a mixed number in its simplest form.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 10">
    1. Convert to improper fractions: 
       $3\frac{1}{5} = \frac{16}{5}$ and $1\frac{3}{4} = \frac{7}{4}$
    2. Find a common denominator (20):
       $\frac{16 \times 4}{5 \times 4} = \frac{64}{20}$ and $\frac{7 \times 5}{4 \times 5} = \frac{35}{20}$
    3. Subtract: $\frac{64}{20} - \frac{35}{20} = \frac{29}{20}$
    4. Convert back: $29 \div 20 = 1$ with a remainder of $9$.
    **Answer:** $1\frac{9}{20}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part D: Order of Operations (BIDMAS)

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 11: BIDMAS with Negatives">
    Calculate the value of $10 - (-2)^3 \div 4$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 11">
    1. **Indices:** $(-2)^3 = -8$
    2. The equation is now: $10 - (-8) \div 4$
    3. **Division:** $(-8) \div 4 = -2$
    4. The equation is now: $10 - (-2)$
    5. **Subtraction:** $10 + 2 = 12$
    **Answer:** $12$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 12: Advanced BIDMAS (Extended)">
    Calculate $16^{\frac{1}{2}} - (-3)^2 \times 2$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 12">
    1. **Indices first:** $16^{\frac{1}{2}}$ is the square root of 16, which is $4$.
       $(-3)^2$ is $-3 \times -3$, which is $9$.
    2. The equation is now: $4 - 9 \times 2$
    3. **Multiplication next:** $9 \times 2 = 18$
    4. The equation is now: $4 - 18$
    5. **Subtraction last:** $4 - 18 = -14$
    **Answer:** $-14$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
