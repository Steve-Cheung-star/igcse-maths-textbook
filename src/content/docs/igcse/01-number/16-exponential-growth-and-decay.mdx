In the real world, things rarely change by the same fixed amount every year. Instead, they change by a **percentage** of their current value. This creates **Exponential Growth** (speeding up) or **Exponential Decay** (slowing down).

---

## 1. The Exponential Formula

While you can calculate change year-by-year, it is much faster to use the general formula. You do not need to know the constant $e$ for IGCSE; we use the **multiplier method**.

$$Value = P \times (\text{multiplier})^n$$

* **$P$**: The initial amount (Principal).
* **Multiplier**: The percentage change expressed as a decimal ($1 + r$ for growth, $1 - r$ for decay).
* **$n$**: The number of time periods (usually years).

## 2. Exponential Growth (Population)

Growth occurs when the multiplier is greater than 1. This is commonly seen in population studies or bacterial growth.

**Example:** A town has a population of 25,000. The population grows at a rate of 3% per year. Calculate the population after 12 years.

1.  **Identify the multiplier:** $100\% + 3\% = 103\% \implies \mathbf{1.03}$
2.  **Set up the equation:** $25,000 \times (1.03)^{12}$
3.  **Calculate:** $25,000 \times 1.42576... = 35,644.02...$
4.  **Round to context:** You cannot have 0.02 of a person! 
    **Answer:** 35,644 people.

<SteveTip title="Linear vs Exponential">
If the question says "The population increases by 500 people every year," that is **Linear**. You just add.
If the question says "The population increases by 2% every year," that is **Exponential**. You must use powers!
</SteveTip>

---

## 3. Exponential Decay (Depreciation)

Decay occurs when the multiplier is less than 1. The most common exam example is **Depreciation**—the loss of value in an item like a car or a computer.

**Example:** A car is bought for £18,000. It depreciates at a rate of 15% per year. Find its value after 4 years.

1.  **Identify the multiplier:** $100\% - 15\% = 85\% \implies \mathbf{0.85}$
2.  **Set up the equation:** $18,000 \times (0.85)^4$
3.  **Calculate:** $18,000 \times 0.522 = 9396$
    **Answer:** £9,396

## 5. Standard Practice Problems

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Basic Growth">
    * **a)** The number of bacteria in a colony doubles every hour. If there are 500 bacteria initially, how many will there be after 8 hours?
    * **b)** A town's population of 12,000 grows at 4% per year. What is the population after 5 years? (Round to the nearest whole person).
    * **c)** A viral video has 2,000 views. Views increase by 15% every hour. How many views will it have after exactly 24 hours?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    * **a)** "Doubles" means an increase of 100%, so the multiplier is **2**. Calculation: $500 \times 2^8 = 128,000$. **Answer: 128,000 bacteria**
    * **b)** Multiplier: $1 + 0.04 = 1.04$. Calculation: $12,000 \times 1.04^5 = 14599.8...$ **Answer: 14,600 people**
    * **c)** Multiplier: $1 + 0.15 = 1.15$. Calculation: $2000 \times 1.15^{24} = 57245.5...$ **Answer: 57,246 views**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

    * **a)** A laptop costs £800. Its value depreciates by 25% each year. Calculate its exact value after 3 years.
    * **b)** A car bought for £24,000 loses 12% of its value annually. What is its value after 6 years? (To the nearest penny).

    * **c)** Multiplier: $1 - 0.05 = 0.95$. Calculation: $400 \times 0.95^{10} = 239.49...$ **Answer: 239.5 g**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Finding the Initial Value">
    * **a)** An investment grows by 5% per year. After 3 years it is worth £1,157.63. What was the initial amount invested?
    * **b)** A machine depreciates by 20% a year. After 2 years it is worth £3,200. What was its original price?
    * **c)** A forest shrinks by 2.5% per year. After 4 years, it covers 8,500 hectares. What was its original area? (Nearest whole number).
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    * **a)** Let initial = $P$. Equation: $P \times 1.05^3 = 1157.63$. Reverse it: $P = 1157.63 \div 1.05^3$. **Answer: £1,000**
    * **b)** Equation: $P \times 0.80^2 = 3200$. Reverse it: $P = 3200 \div 0.80^2$. **Answer: £5,000**
    * **c)** Equation: $P \times 0.975^4 = 8500$. Reverse it: $P = 8500 \div 0.975^4 = 9377.3...$ **Answer: 9,377 hectares**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Solving for 'n' (Trial and Improvement)">
    * **a)** A rare stamp is worth £500. Its value increases by 10% each year. Find the number of **full years** it takes for the value to exceed £800.
    * **b)** A car worth £15,000 depreciates 18% per year. How many full years until its value drops below £5,000?
    * **c)** A bank account offers 3.5% compound interest per year. How many full years will it take to double an initial investment of £1,000?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    * **a)** Equation: $500 \times 1.1^n > 800$. Try $n=4$: $500 \times 1.1^4 = 732.05$. Try $n=5$: $500 \times 1.1^5 = 805.26$. **Answer: 5 years**
    * **b)** Equation: $15,000 \times 0.82^n < 5000$. Try $n=5$: $\approx £5561$. Try $n=6$: $\approx £4560$. **Answer: 6 years**
    * **c)** Equation: $1000 \times 1.035^n > 2000$ (or just $1.035^n > 2$). Try $n=20$: $\approx 1.989$. Try $n=21$: $\approx 2.059$. **Answer: 21 years**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: Structured Comparing Models">
    * **a)** Town A has 50,000 people and grows by 2% per year. Find its population after 10 years.
    * **b)** Town B has 60,000 people and its population decreases by 1% per year. Find its population after 10 years.
    * **c)** By testing values of $n$, determine the exact year in which the population of Town A first exceeds Town B.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    * **a)** $50,000 \times 1.02^{10} = 60949.7...$ **Answer: 60,950 people**
    * **b)** $60,000 \times 0.99^{10} = 54264.2...$ **Answer: 54,264 people**
    * **c)** Test values where $50000 \times 1.02^n > 60000 \times 0.99^n$. 
       $n=6$: Town A = 56,308, Town B = 56,488. (A is still smaller).
       $n=7$: Town A = 57,434, Town B = 55,923. (A is now larger).
       **Answer: Year 7**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 6: Structured Multi-Stage Growth">
    * **a)** A car is bought for £20,000. It depreciates by 20% in the first year, and then by 10% in each subsequent year. What is its value after exactly 4 years?
    * **b)** A rare painting is bought for £5,000. Its value increases by 8% each year for the first 3 years. Then the market slows down and it increases by only 3% each year. Find its value after 6 years total.
    * **c)** A shopkeeper increases the price of an item by 10%, but then immediately applies a 10% discount on the new price. Show mathematically that the final price is a 1% overall decrease from the original.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 6">
    * **a)** You have 1 year at 20% decay, and 3 years at 10% decay. 
       Calculation: $20,000 \times 0.8 \times 0.9^3 = 11664$. **Answer: £11,664**
    * **b)** Stage 1 (3 yrs): $5000 \times 1.08^3$. Stage 2 (3 yrs): Multiply that result by $1.03^3$. 
       Calculation: $5000 \times 1.08^3 \times 1.03^3 = 6882.63...$ **Answer: £6,882.63**
    * **c)** Let the original price be $P$. 
       Increase by 10%: $P \times 1.1$. 
       Decrease by 10%: $(P \times 1.1) \times 0.9$. 
       Overall multiplier: $1.1 \times 0.9 = 0.99$. 
       A multiplier of 0.99 equals a 1% decrease. **Answer: Shown.**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
