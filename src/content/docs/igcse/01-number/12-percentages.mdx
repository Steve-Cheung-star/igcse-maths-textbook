Percentages are parts of 100. Whether you are calculating a discount at a Mong Kok sneaker store, a bank's interest rate, or a business profit, the goal is to understand how a portion relates to the whole.

---

## 1. Finding a Percentage of a Quantity

To find a percentage of an amount, convert the percentage to a decimal (the **multiplier**) and multiply.

* **Method:** Divide the percentage by 100, then multiply by the quantity.
* **Example:** Find 15% of 480 HKD (the cost of a family dim sum lunch).
    1. $15 \div 100 = 0.15$
    2. $0.15 \times 480 = 72$
    **Answer:** 72 HKD

## 2. Expressing One Quantity as a Percentage of Another

This is essentially "writing a test score." You create a fraction representing the part over the whole, and then turn it into a percentage.

* **Formula:** $\frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100$
* **Example:** A student scores 34 out of 40 on their HKDSE mock exam. What is their percentage?
    1. $\frac{34}{40} = 0.85$
    2. $0.85 \times 100 = 85$
    **Answer:** 85%

## 3. Percentage Increase and Decrease

You can do this in two steps (find the percentage, then add/subtract), but using a **multiplier** is much faster and absolutely essential for harder "repeated change" questions.

### The Multiplier Method
* **Increase by 20%:** The original is 100%. $100\% + 20\% = 120\%$. As a decimal: **1.20**
* **Decrease by 15%:** The original is 100%. $100\% - 15\% = 85\%$. As a decimal: **0.85**

**Example:** A sneaker in Mong Kok costs 800 HKD. It is reduced by 35% in a sale. Find the new price.
1. Multiplier: $100\% - 35\% = 65\% \rightarrow 0.65$.
2. Calculation: $800 \times 0.65 = 520$.
**Answer:** 520 HKD

* **Fast Calculation:** $5000 \times 1.10 \times 0.90 = 4950$.

<Aside type="caution" title="Pitfall: The '+10% then -10%' Trap">
A massive misconception is thinking that increasing by 10% and then decreasing by 10% brings you back to the original starting number. It does NOT! 
The second percentage change (the decrease) is calculated on the **new, larger amount**, so it takes a bigger bite out of the total.
</Aside>

<SteveTip title="Profit and Loss">
"Profit" is just a fancy word for **percentage increase**. "Loss" is a **percentage decrease**. 
If a shopkeeper in Sham Shui Po buys a smartphone case bulk lot for 200 HKD and sells it for 250 HKD:
1. Profit = 50 HKD.
2. Percentage Profit = $\frac{50}{200} \times 100 = 25\%$.
</SteveTip>

## 4. Simple and Compound Interest

This is a favorite Paper 4 topic. Note that **formulas are not provided** in the exam.

### Simple Interest
Interest is only calculated on the **original** amount. It stays exactly the same every single year. It is a linear sequence.
* **Formula:** $I = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$ (where $P$ = Principal/Original Amount, $R$ = Rate, $T$ = Time).

### Compound Interest
Interest is calculated on the **new total** each year (you earn interest on your interest). This creates exponential growth.
* **Formula:** $\text{Total Amount} = P \times (\text{Multiplier})^T$

**Example: 20,000 HKD is invested at 4% per year compound interest in Hang Seng Bank for 3 years.**
1. Multiplier for 4% increase: $1.04$
2. Calculation: $20,000 \times (1.04)^3 = 22,497.28$
**Answer:** 22,497.28 HKD

<Aside type="caution" title="Pitfall: Interest vs. Total Balance">
Read the question carefully! Does it ask for the **total amount** in the bank or just the **interest earned**? 
If it asks for interest only: **[Total Balance] - [Original Investment] = [Interest Earned]**.
</Aside>

## 5. Depreciation and Inflation

These use exactly the same mathematical structure as compound interest, but they represent real-world values changing over time.

* **Depreciation (Value Goes Down):** Used for cars, machinery, or technology. The multiplier is less than 1.
    * *Example:* A Tesla worth 250,000 HKD depreciates by 12% each year. Value after 5 years: $250,000 \times (0.88)^5$.
* **Inflation (Value Goes Up):** Used for the cost of living, groceries, or property prices. The multiplier is greater than 1.
    * *Example:* A pineapple bun at a local cha chaan teng costs 12 HKD. If annual inflation is 3%, what is the cost after 2 years? $12 \times (1.03)^2$.

## 6. Solving for Unknowns (Advanced Extended)

Sometimes the exam flips the compound interest question and asks you to find the **Rate ($R$)** or the **Time ($n$)**.

### Finding an Unknown Rate (The nth Root)
If an investment of 50,000 JPY grows to 60,000 JPY in 4 years, what is the annual compound interest rate?
1. Set up the equation: $50,000 \times x^4 = 60,000$
2. Isolate the multiplier ($x$): $x^4 = \frac{60,000}{50,000} = 1.2$
3. Use the nth root on your calculator: $x = \sqrt[4]{1.2} \approx 1.0466$
4. Convert multiplier back to a percentage: A multiplier of $1.0466$ means a $4.66\%$ increase.
**Answer:** 4.66%

### Finding Unknown Time (Logarithms or Graphs)
If an investment of 10,000 HKD at 5% needs to reach 15,000 HKD, how many years does it take?
1. Set up the equation: $10,000 \times (1.05)^n = 15,000$
2. Isolate the power: $1.05^n = 1.5$

<Aside type="note" title="Sneak Peek: Logs and Graphs">
To solve for an exponent like $n$, we will eventually use **Logarithms** (Chapter 3). For now, you can solve this by using the **Table** function on your calculator (inputting $y = 1.05^x$) or by sketching the graph and finding where it crosses the line $y = 1.5$.
</Aside>

## 7. Reverse Percentages (The "Original Value" Trap)

This is heavily tested in Paper 4. You are given the **final** value after a change has happened, and you are asked to find the **original** starting value.

**Example: A "Nintendo Switch 2" in Sham Shui Po is sold for 3,420 HKD after a 10% student discount. Find the original price.**

<Aside type="caution" title="Pitfall: The 10% Illusion">
Do not calculate 10% of 3420 HKD and add it on! The 10% discount was calculated based on the *original* (larger) price, not the sale price.
</Aside>

To go backward in time, we reverse the multiplication into a division.

Whenever you see phrases like "Original price," "Before the increase," "Cost price," or "Prior to the sale," you are dealing with a **Reverse Percentage**. Your first step should always be to set up a division:
**[Final Amount] ÷ [Multiplier] = [Original Amount]**
</SteveTip>

    * **c)** Discount amount: $400 - 340 = 60$. Percentage: $\frac{60}{400} \times 100 = \mathbf{15\%}$.
  </TabItem>

### Part D: Solving for Unknowns

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4">
    * **a)** An investment of 12,000 SGD is left in a compound interest account for 6 years. It grows to 15,000 SGD. Calculate the annual percentage interest rate to 3 significant figures.
    * **b)** A company car is bought for 240,000 HKD. After 3 years of depreciating at a constant annual rate, its value is 135,000 HKD. Calculate the exact annual rate of depreciation.
    * **c)** John invests 30,000 HKD in a savings account paying 4% compound interest per year. Using trial and improvement, find the minimum number of full years it takes for the total amount to exceed 40,000 HKD.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    * **a)** Equation: $12000 \times r^6 = 15000$. Divide: $r^6 = 1.25$. Root: $r = \sqrt[6]{1.25} \approx 1.03789$. This is a $\mathbf{3.79\%}$ increase.
    * **b)** Equation: $240000 \times r^3 = 135000$. Divide: $r^3 = 0.5625$. Root: $r = \sqrt[3]{0.5625} \approx 0.8255$. This multiplier represents a decrease of $100\% - 82.55\% = \mathbf{17.45\%}$.
    * **c)** Equation: $30000 \times (1.04)^n > 40000 \rightarrow 1.04^n > 1.333...$ 
       Test $n=7$: $1.04^7 \approx 1.316$.
       Test $n=8$: $1.04^8 \approx 1.368$. 
       **Answer:** $\mathbf{8\text{ years}}$.
  </TabItem>

### Part E: Reverse Percentages

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5">
    * **a)** After a 15% pay rise, an accountant in Central earns 48,300 HKD per month. Calculate their salary before the pay rise.
    * **b)** In 2005, a tech entrepreneur sold his Chelsea-moored houseboat (the one with the "Revolutionary" name) for 253,000 GBP. If this sale price represented a 15% profit on his original purchase, how much did the houseboat originally cost?
    * **c)** At a pop-up shop in Tsim Sha Tsui, a limited edition console is reduced by 25%. A buyer uses a coupon to get another 10% off the sale price and pays 2,700 HKD. What was the original price?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    * **a)** Multiplier for 15% increase: $1.15$. Reverse calculation: $48,300 \div 1.15 = \mathbf{42,000\text{ HKD}}$.
    * **b)** Multiplier for 15% profit (increase): $1.15$. Reverse calculation: $253,000 \div 1.15 = \mathbf{220,000\text{ GBP}}$.
    * **c)** The chained multipliers going forward were $0.70$ and $0.90$. 
       Working backwards step-by-step: 
       Price before coupon: $2700 \div 0.90 = 3000$. 
       Price before pop-up sale: $3000 \div 0.75 = \mathbf{4000\text{ HKD}}$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
