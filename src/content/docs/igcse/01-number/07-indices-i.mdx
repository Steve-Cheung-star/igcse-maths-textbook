<Aside type="tip" title="The Index Laws">
1. **Multiplication (Add the powers):** $$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$$
2. **Division (Subtract the powers):** $$a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}$$
3. **Power of a Power (Multiply the powers):** $$(a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}$$
</Aside>

## 2. Zero and Negative Indices

To access the top grades, you must know what to do when the power is not a simple positive integer.

### The Zero Power
Any non-zero number (or letter) raised to the power of 0 is exactly **1**.
* $5^0 = 1$
* $1982^0 = 1$

<SteveTip title="The Zero Power Trap">
Be very careful when letters and numbers are mixed! Examiners love to ask you to evaluate $3x^0$. 
Many students confidently write $1$. This is **wrong**! 
Only the $x$ is raised to the power of 0. So, it is actually $3 \times (x^0)$, which means $3 \times 1 = 3$.
</SteveTip>

### Negative Indices
A negative power means the **reciprocal** of the positive power. It "flips" the number underneath a 1. It does *not* make the number itself negative!
* $$a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$$
* *Example:* $7^{-2} = \frac{1}{7^2} = \frac{1}{49}$

<Aside type="note" title="Anatomy of a Complex Index">
$$ 
x^{\scriptstyle -\frac{\color{blue}{m}}{\color{green}{n}}} \implies \frac{1}{(\sqrt[\color{green}{n}]{x})^{\color{blue}{m}}} 
$$
</Aside>

* A power of $\frac{1}{2}$ is a **square root**: $16^{\scriptstyle \frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{16} = 4$
* A power of $\frac{1}{3}$ is a **cube root**: $8^{\scriptstyle \frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{8} = 2$
* A power of $\frac{1}{4}$ is a **fourth root**: $16^{\scriptstyle \frac{1}{4}} = \sqrt[4]{16} = 2$

**Rule:** "Root the bottom, Power the top." Always do the root first to make the number smaller and easier to manage!

## 4. Combining Rules (Extended)

The hardest questions will mix negative and fractional indices together. Just take it one step at a time!

**Example: Evaluate $8^{\scriptstyle -\frac{2}{3}}$**
1. **Deal with the negative:** Flip the number to make the power positive.
   $$\frac{1}{8^{\scriptstyle \frac{2}{3}}}$$
2. **Root the bottom (denominator):** The 3 means take the cube root of 8.
   $$\sqrt[3]{8} = 2$$
3. **Power the top (numerator):** The 2 means square the result.
   $$2^2 = 4$$
4. **Put it back under the 1:**
   **Answer:** $\frac{1}{4}$

## 5. Practice Problems

### Part A: The Three Golden Laws

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Multiplication Rule">
    Evaluate the following. Leave your answer as an integer.
    * **a)** $2^{-3} \times 2^4$
    * **b)** $5^6 \times 5^{-4}$
    * **c)** $3^{-2} \times 3^5$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    * **a)** Add the powers: $-3 + 4 = 1 \implies 2^1 = \mathbf{2}$
    * **b)** Add the powers: $6 + (-4) = 2 \implies 5^2 = \mathbf{25}$
    * **c)** Add the powers: $-2 + 5 = 3 \implies 3^3 = \mathbf{27}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Power of a Power">
    Simplify and evaluate the following:
    * **a)** $(2^3)^2$
    * **b)** $(10^2)^3$
    * **c)** $(3^{-1})^{-3}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    * **a)** Multiply the indices: $3 \times 2 = 6 \implies 2^6 = \mathbf{64}$
    * **b)** Multiply the indices: $2 \times 3 = 6 \implies 10^6 = \mathbf{1,000,000}$
    * **c)** Multiply the indices: $-1 \times -3 = 3 \implies 3^3 = \mathbf{27}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Division Rule">
    Evaluate the following. Give your answer as a fraction or integer.
    * **a)** $2^3 \div 2^4$
    * **b)** $5^2 \div 5^4$
    * **c)** $4^5 \div 4^2$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    * **a)** Subtract powers: $3 - 4 = -1 \implies 2^{-1} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{2}}$
    * **b)** Subtract powers: $2 - 4 = -2 \implies 5^{-2} = \frac{1}{5^2} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{25}}$
    * **c)** Subtract powers: $5 - 2 = 3 \implies 4^3 = \mathbf{64}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

### Part B: Negative and Fractional Indices

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Negative Indices">
    Find the exact value of:
    * **a)** $7^{-2}$
    * **b)** $4^{-3}$

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    * **a)** Flip and square: $\frac{1}{7^2} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{49}}$
    * **b)** Flip and cube: $\frac{1}{4^3} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{64}}$
    * **c)** Flip and apply power: $\frac{1}{2^5} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{32}}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: Simple Fractional Indices">
    Find the exact value of:
    * **a)** $64^{\scriptstyle \frac{1}{2}}$
    * **b)** $125^{\scriptstyle \frac{1}{3}}$
    * **c)** $32^{\scriptstyle \frac{1}{5}}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    * **a)** The power of $\frac{1}{2}$ is a square root: $\sqrt{64} = \mathbf{8}$
    * **b)** The power of $\frac{1}{3}$ is a cube root: $\sqrt[3]{125} = \mathbf{5}$
    * **c)** The power of $\frac{1}{5}$ is a fifth root: $\sqrt[5]{32} = \mathbf{2}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 6: Advanced Fractional Indices (Extended)">
    Find the exact value of:
    * **a)** $81^{\scriptstyle \frac{3}{4}}$
    * **b)** $27^{\scriptstyle \frac{2}{3}}$
    * **c)** $32^{\scriptstyle \frac{4}{5}}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 6">
    * **a)** Root bottom, power top: $(\sqrt[4]{81})^3 = 3^3 = \mathbf{27}$
    * **b)** Root bottom, power top: $(\sqrt[3]{27})^2 = 3^2 = \mathbf{9}$
    * **c)** Root bottom, power top: $(\sqrt[5]{32})^4 = 2^4 = \mathbf{16}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 7: Combined Negative and Fractional (Extended)">
    Evaluate the following:
    * **a)** $8^{\scriptstyle -\frac{2}{3}}$
    * **b)** $16^{\scriptstyle -\frac{3}{4}}$
    * **c)** $125^{\scriptstyle -\frac{2}{3}}$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 7">
    * **a)** Flip, then root bottom, power top: $\frac{1}{(\sqrt[3]{8})^2} = \frac{1}{2^2} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{4}}$
    * **b)** Flip, then root bottom, power top: $\frac{1}{(\sqrt[4]{16})^3} = \frac{1}{2^3} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{8}}$
    * **c)** Flip, then root bottom, power top: $\frac{1}{(\sqrt[3]{125})^2} = \frac{1}{5^2} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{25}}$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
