Time is one of the few areas in maths that doesn't operate on a base-10 or base-100 system, which makes it a frequent trap for students. You need to be confident converting between units, jumping across time zones, and reading real-world timetables.

---

## 1. Units of Time and Conversions

You must know the exact relationship between all standard units of time. For the purposes of your exams, leap years are ignored unless specifically mentioned.

* **1 minute** = 60 seconds
* **1 hour** = 60 minutes
* **1 day** = 24 hours
* **1 week** = 7 days
* **1 year** = 12 months
* **1 year** = 52 weeks
* **1 year** = 365 days

<Aside type="caution" title="Exam Tip: The Decimal Trap">
Remember from our calculator lesson: **time is not decimal!** A duration of `2.5 hours` is NOT 2 hours and 50 minutes. Because an hour is 60 minutes, `0.5` of an hour is half of 60, which is 30 minutes. Always multiply the decimal part of an hour by 60 to find the exact minutes!
</Aside>

---

## 2. The 12-Hour and 24-Hour Clock

Exam questions will frequently use the 24-hour clock, especially when dealing with timetables. 

* **12-Hour Clock:** Uses **a.m.** (morning) and **p.m.** (afternoon/evening).
* **24-Hour Clock:** Always uses **four digits** and counts continuously from midnight (`00 00`) to one minute to midnight (`23 59`).

### Conversion Rules:
* **a.m. times:** Keep the numbers the same, but ensure there are four digits. (e.g., 6.15 a.m. becomes `06 15`). *Exception: 12.xx a.m. (midnight) translates to `00 xx`.*
* **p.m. times:** Add 12 to the hours. (e.g., 3.15 p.m. becomes 3 + 12 = 15, so it is written as `15 15`). *Exception: 12.xx p.m. (midday) stays as `12 xx`.*

<SteveTip title="Cambridge Formatting Quirk">
You are probably used to seeing digital time written with a colon (like `15:15`). However, the Cambridge syllabus officially denotes 24-hour time with a space instead of a colon, such as **`15 15`** or **`03 15`**. Get used to reading and writing it this way for your exam!
</SteveTip>

## 3. Calculating Durations and Reading Timetables

When calculating how long a journey takes, the safest method is **counting up** (or using a "time line"). Do not try to subtract times using standard column subtraction, because time operates in 60s, not 100s!

**Example:** A bus leaves at `08 45` and arrives at `11 20`. How long is the journey?
1. Jump to the next full hour: `08 45` to `09 00` is **15 minutes**.
2. Jump the full hours: `09 00` to `11 00` is **2 hours**.
3. Jump the remaining minutes: `11 00` to `11 20` is **20 minutes**.
4. Total duration: 2 hours + 15 mins + 20 mins = **2 hours 35 minutes**.

## 4. Time Zones and Overlapping Days

Examiners love to give you a flight that leaves at one local time and arrives at another, often crossing midnight or incorporating half-hour time zones.
Note that IGCSE uses the **xx xx** convention for 24-hour time without colons (e.g., 08 00).
To solve these, always **bring the times to a neutral, shared time zone first.**

**Example: A flight leaves London (GMT) at 18 00 on Tuesday and arrives in Mumbai (GMT+5:30) at 08 00 on Wednesday. How long was the flight?**

### The Calculation Method

You cannot simply subtract 18 00 from 08 00 because they are in different time zones and on different days.
**Convert the arrival time back to the departure time zone first.**

1.  **Bring to same Time Zone:** When it is 08 00 (Wed) in Mumbai, what time is it in London?
Mumbai is 5 hours and 30 mins ahead, so subtract 5.5 hours from 08 00. This gives **02 30 (Wed)** in London.
2.  **Calculate Difference:** From 18 00 (Tue) to midnight is 6 hours.
From midnight to 02 30 (Wed) is 2.5 hours. 
3.  6 + 2.5 = 8.5 hours.
**Answer:** **8 hours 30 minutes**.

    A train departs at `14 35`. The journey takes 3 hours and 45 minutes. What time does the train arrive? Give your answer in the 24-hour clock.

    5. `18 00` + 20 mins = `18 20`.

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Time Zones">
    New York is 5 hours behind London. 
    A flight leaves New York at `21 30` local time and takes 7 hours and 10 minutes to reach London. 
    What is the local time in London when the flight arrives?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    **Step 1:** Calculate the arrival time in New York's local time.
    * Departure: `21 30`
    * Add 7 hours: `21 30` + 7 hours = `28 30`. 
    * Because `28 30` is past midnight (`24 00`), subtract 24. 28 - 24 = 4. So it arrives at `04 30` the next day (New York time).
    * Add the 10 minutes: `04 30` + 10 mins = `04 40` (New York time).

    **Step 2:** Apply the time difference.
    * New York is 5 hours *behind* London, which means London is 5 hours *ahead* of New York.
    * Add 5 hours to the New York arrival time: `04 40` + 5 hours = `09 40`.
    
    **Answer:** `09 40`
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Speed and Time Zones">
    An airplane flies from London to Tokyo, a distance of 9000 km. The average speed of the plane is 800 km/h. 
    Tokyo is 9 hours ahead of London. 
    If the flight leaves London at `14 30` local time on Tuesday, what is the local time and day in Tokyo when it arrives?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    **Step 1: Calculate the flight duration.**
    * Time = 9000 / 800 = 11.25 hours.
    * Convert 0.25 hours into minutes: 0.25 × 60 = 15 minutes.
    * Total flight time = 11 hours 15 minutes.

    **Step 2: Find the arrival time in London's local time.**
    * Departure: `14 30`
    * Add 11 hours: `14 30` + 11 hours = `25 30`.
    * Add 15 minutes: `25 30` + 15 mins = `25 45`.
    * Subtract 24 hours to step into the next day: 25 - 24 = 1. The plane lands at `01 45` on Wednesday (London time).

    **Step 3: Apply the time difference for Tokyo.**
    * Tokyo is 9 hours ahead, so we add 9 hours to the London arrival time.
    * `01 45` + 9 hours = `10 45`.
    
    **Answer:** `10 45` on Wednesday
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## 6. Advanced Practice: Time Zones & Speed/Distance

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: Offsets and Calculations">
    * **a)** A flight leaves City A (GMT) at **10 45** and lands in City B (GMT+3) at **19 15** local time. How long was the flight?
    * **b)** A plane leaves City C (GMT-4) at **21 15** and arrives in City D (GMT+1) at **11 45** the **next day**. Calculate the duration.
    * **c)** A flight takes 6 hours 45 minutes from City P (GMT+5:30) to City Q (GMT+1). If it leaves at **03 00**, what is the local arrival time in Q?
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    * **a)** Convert Arrival to GMT: 19 15 - 3 hours = 16 15. Duration from 10 45 to 16 15: 16 15 - 10 45 = **5 hours 30 minutes**.
    * **b)** Convert Arrival to GMT-4: 11 45 - 5 hours = 06 45 (next day). Duration from 21 15 to 06 45: 2.75 hours (to midnight) + 6.75 hours = 9.5 hours = **9 hours 30 minutes**.
    * **c)** Arrival in P's time: 03 00 + 6 hours 45 minutes = 09 45. Convert to Q's time (which is 4.5 hours behind P): 09 45 - 4 hours 30 minutes = **05 15**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 6: Crossing Midnight & The Date Line">
    * **a)** A train departs at **22 40**. The journey takes 8 hours 50 minutes. Find the arrival time (assuming no time zone changes).
    * **b)** A flight leaves Tokyo (GMT+9) at **23 30** on Monday. It flies for 10 hours to Los Angeles (GMT-8). Find the local arrival time and day in Los Angeles.
    * **c)** A plane leaves Perth (GMT+8) at **18 45** on Friday. It flies 14 hours 20 minutes to London (GMT). Find the local arrival time and day in London.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 6">
    * **a)** 22 40 + 8 hours = 06 40 (next day). Add 50 minutes: 06 40 + 50 minutes = **07 30**.
    * **b)** Arrival in Tokyo time: 23 30 + 10 hours = 09 30 Tuesday. LA is 17 hours behind Tokyo (9 - (-8) = 17). 09 30 Tuesday - 17 hours = **16 30 Monday**.
    * **c)** Arrival in Perth time: 18 45 + 14 hours 20 minutes = 09 05 Saturday. London is 8 hours behind Perth. 09 05 Saturday - 8 hours = **01 05 Saturday**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 7: Speed & Time Zones (Basic)">
    * **a)** A plane flies **6000 km** from City A (GMT+1) to City B (GMT+4) at an average speed of **800 km/h**. It departs City A at **09 30** local time. Calculate the flight duration.
    * **b)** Convert the departure time to City B's local time.
    * **c)** Find the local time of arrival at City B.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 7">
    * **a)** Duration = 6000 / 800 = 7.5 hours = **7 hours 30 minutes**.
    * **b)** City B is 3 hours ahead of City A. Departure in B's time: 09 30 + 3 hours = **12 30**.
    * **c)** Local arrival: 12 30 + 7 hours 30 minutes = **20 00**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 8: Speed & Time Zones (Intermediate)">
    * **a)** A flight leaves Paris (GMT+1) at **22 45** on Monday and arrives in Tokyo (GMT+9) at **18 15** on Tuesday. What is the arrival time converted to Paris time?
    * **b)** How long was the flight?
    * **c)** If the distance between the cities is **9200 km**, calculate the average speed of the flight in km/h.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 8">
    * **a)** Tokyo is 8 hours ahead of Paris. Arrival in Paris time: 18 15 Tuesday - 8 hours = **10 15 Tuesday**.
    * **b)** Duration from 22 45 Monday to 10 15 Tuesday: 1 hour 15 minutes (to midnight) + 10 hours 15 minutes = **11 hours 30 minutes**.
    * **c)** 11 hours 30 minutes = 11.5 hours. Speed = 9200 / 11.5 = **800 km/h**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 9: The Canadian Hockey Mishap">
    * **a)** Mr. Cheung is flying from Hong Kong (GMT+8) to Vancouver (GMT-8) for a hockey game starting at **19 00** on Friday. The distance is **10,200 km**, and the average flight speed is **850 km/h**. How long is the flight duration?
    * **b)** His flight leaves Hong Kong at **23 30** on Friday local time. What time does he land in Vancouver (local time)?
    * **c)** Does he make it to the puck drop on time?

  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 9">

    * **b)** Vancouver is 16 hours behind HK. Departure in Vancouver time: 23 30 - 16 hours = 07 30 Friday. Arrival: 07 30 + 12 hours = **19 30 Friday**.
    * **c)** The game starts at 19 00, but he lands at 19 30. **No, he misses the puck drop by 30 minutes and is stuck eating airport poutine!**
  </TabItem>
