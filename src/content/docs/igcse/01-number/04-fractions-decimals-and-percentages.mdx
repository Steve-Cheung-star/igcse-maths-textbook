Fractions, decimals, and percentages are just three different languages used to describe the exact same thing: **a part of a whole**. To succeed in IGCSE Maths, you must be able to translate between these three forms quickly and accurately.

---

## 1. The Language of Fractions

Before you calculate anything, you must know the correct terminology for the different types of fractions.

* **Numerator:** The top number (how many parts you have).
* **Denominator:** The bottom number (how many parts make up a whole).

### Types of Fractions

1. **Proper Fractions:** The numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g., $\frac{3}{4}$, $\frac{1}{2}$). Its value is always less than 1.
2. **Improper Fractions ("Top-Heavy"):** The numerator is larger than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $\frac{5}{4}$, $\frac{10}{3}$). Its value is 1 or greater.
3. **Mixed Numbers:** A whole number combined with a proper fraction (e.g., $1\frac{1}{4}$, $3\frac{1}{3}$).

### Simplest Form (Cancelling Down)
Whenever you write a fraction in an exam, it **must** be in its simplest form unless the question tells you otherwise. To simplify a fraction, divide both the numerator and the denominator by their Highest Common Factor (HCF).

* *Example:* Simplify $\frac{18}{24}$. 
  The HCF of 18 and 24 is 6. 
  Divide top and bottom by 6 $\rightarrow \frac{3}{4}$.

<SteveTip title="The 'Not Quite Done' Trap">
A very common way to lose the final mark on a 3-mark question is stopping at a fraction like $\frac{12}{16}$. Even if your math was perfect, if you don't cancel it down to $\frac{3}{4}$, you drop the final accuracy mark! If you aren't sure if you have the HCF, just keep dividing by 2 or 3 until you can't anymore.
</SteveTip>

## 2. Decimals and Percentages

### Decimals
Decimals use the base-10 place value system to show fractions of a whole. The first decimal place is tenths ($\frac{1}{10}$), the second is hundredths ($\frac{1}{100}$), and so on.

### Percentages
The word *percent* literally translates from Latin as **"out of 100"**. 
* 45% simply means 45 out of 100, or $\frac{45}{100}$.

<Aside type="tip" title="The Conversion Cheat Sheet">
* **Fraction to Decimal:** Divide the top by the bottom ($3 \div 4 = 0.75$).
* **Decimal to Percentage:** Multiply by 100 (move the decimal two jumps right).
* **Percentage to Decimal:** Divide by 100 (move the decimal two jumps left).
* **Percentage to Fraction:** Put the number over 100, then simplify!
</Aside>

### Converting Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions
You will frequently need to switch between these two forms, especially before multiplying or dividing fractions (which we cover in the next section).

**Mixed to Improper:**
<Steps>
1. Multiply the whole number by the denominator.
2. Add the numerator.
3. Put that result over the original denominator.
</Steps>
* *Example:* $2\frac{3}{5} \rightarrow (2 \times 5) + 3 = 13 \rightarrow \frac{13}{5}$

**Improper to Mixed:**
<Steps>
1. Divide the top by the bottom to get the whole number.
2. The remainder becomes the new numerator.
</Steps>
* *Example:* $\frac{17}{3} \rightarrow 17 \div 3 = 5$ with a remainder of $2 \rightarrow 5\frac{2}{3}$

## 4. Practice Problems

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Mixed & Improper">
    Convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions as required.
    
    **Core:**
    * **a)** Convert $3\frac{1}{4}$ to an improper fraction.
    * **b)** Convert $\frac{15}{4}$ to a mixed number.
    * **c)** Convert $5\frac{2}{3}$ to an improper fraction.
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** Convert $\frac{45}{7}$ to a mixed number.
    * **e)** Convert $12\frac{7}{8}$ to an improper fraction.
    * **f)** Convert $\frac{134}{11}$ to a mixed number.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    **Core:**
    * **a)** $(3 \times 4) + 1 = 13$ $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{13}{4}$**
    * **b)** $15 \div 4 = 3$ remainder $3$ $\rightarrow$ **$3\frac{3}{4}$**
    * **c)** $(5 \times 3) + 2 = 17$ $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{17}{3}$**
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** $45 \div 7 = 6$ remainder $3$ $\rightarrow$ **$6\frac{3}{7}$**
    * **e)** $(12 \times 8) + 7 = 103$ $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{103}{8}$**
    * **f)** $134 \div 11 = 12$ remainder $2$ $\rightarrow$ **$12\frac{2}{11}$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Percentages to Fractions & Decimals">
    Convert the following percentages. Ensure all fractions are given in their simplest form.
    
    **Core:**
    * **a)** Write 25% as a fraction.
    * **b)** Write 70% as a decimal.
    * **c)** Write 8% as a fraction.
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** Write 12.5% as a decimal.
    * **e)** Write 175% as a mixed number.
    * **f)** Write 0.4% as a fraction.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    **Core:**
    * **a)** $\frac{25}{100}$ (divide top & bottom by 25) $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{1}{4}$**
    * **b)** $70 \div 100$ (move decimal left twice) $\rightarrow$ **0.7**
    * **c)** $\frac{8}{100}$ (divide top & bottom by 4) $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{2}{25}$**
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** $12.5 \div 100$ $\rightarrow$ **0.125**
    * **e)** $\frac{175}{100} = 1\frac{75}{100}$ (simplify the fraction by dividing by 25) $\rightarrow$ **$1\frac{3}{4}$**
    * **f)** $\frac{0.4}{100}$ (multiply by 10 to clear decimal) $= \frac{4}{1000}$ (divide by 4) $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{1}{250}$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Decimals to Fractions & Percentages">
    Convert the following decimals. Ensure all fractions are given in their simplest form.
    
    **Core:**
    * **a)** Write 0.3 as a fraction.
    * **b)** Write 0.85 as a percentage.
    * **c)** Write 0.04 as a fraction.
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** Write 2.5 as a percentage.
    * **e)** Write 0.005 as a fraction.
    * **f)** Write 3.14 as a mixed number.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    **Core:**
    * **a)** The 3 is in the tenths column $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{3}{10}$**
    * **b)** $0.85 \times 100$ $\rightarrow$ **85%**
    * **c)** The 4 is in the hundredths column, so $\frac{4}{100}$ (divide by 4) $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{1}{25}$**
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** $2.5 \times 100$ $\rightarrow$ **250%**
    * **e)** The 5 is in the thousandths column, so $\frac{5}{1000}$ (divide by 5) $\rightarrow$ **$\frac{1}{200}$**
    * **f)** The whole number is 3. The decimal is $\frac{14}{100}$. Simplify to get $\frac{7}{50}$ $\rightarrow$ **$3\frac{7}{50}$**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Fractions to Decimals & Percentages">
    Convert the following fractions.
    
    **Core:**
    * **a)** Write $\frac{1}{2}$ as a percentage.
    * **b)** Write $\frac{3}{5}$ as a decimal.
    * **c)** Write $\frac{7}{20}$ as a percentage.
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** Write $\frac{5}{8}$ as a decimal.
    * **e)** Write $\frac{4}{3}$ as a percentage (to 1 decimal place).
    * **f)** Write $\frac{11}{40}$ as a percentage.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    **Core:**
    * **a)** $1 \div 2 = 0.5$, then multiply by 100 $\rightarrow$ **50%**
    * **b)** $3 \div 5$ $\rightarrow$ **0.6**
    * **c)** Make denominator 100 (multiply top and bottom by 5) $\rightarrow \frac{35}{100} \rightarrow$ **35%**
    
    **Extended:**
    * **d)** $5 \div 8$ (you may need to use short division here: $8 \text{ into } 5.000$) $\rightarrow$ **0.625**
    * **e)** $4 \div 3 = 1.333...$ Multiply by 100 $= 133.33...\%$ $\rightarrow$ **133.3%**
    * **f)** $11 \div 40 = 0.275$, then multiply by 100 $\rightarrow$ **27.5%**
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
