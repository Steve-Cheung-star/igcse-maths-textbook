Probability is the mathematical way of measuring how likely it is that an event will happen. Whether you are tossing a coin, rolling a dice, or predicting the weather, probability provides a numerical value to "chance."

<Aside type="note" title="The Probability Scale">
Probabilities always exist on a scale from **0 to 1**. 

* **0** means the event is absolutely **impossible**.
* **1** means the event is absolutely **certain**.
* **0.5** means there is an **even chance** (a 50/50 probability).
</Aside>

## 2. Probability Notation and The Complement Rule

To save time writing long sentences, mathematicians use specific notation. Let $A$ represent an event (like "rolling a 6").

* **$P(A)$** means "the probability of event $A$ occurring."
* **$P(A')$** means "the probability of event $A$ **NOT** occurring." The little dash is called the prime symbol, and this is known as the **complement** of $A$.

<Aside type="tip" title="The Complement Rule">
Because an event must either happen or not happen, the probabilities of these two outcomes must add up to 1 (or 100%).

$$
P(A) + P(A') = 1
$$

Therefore, you can always find the probability of an event *not* happening by subtracting the probability of it happening from 1:

$$
P(A') = 1 - P(A)
$$
</Aside>

**Example:** If the probability of it raining tomorrow is $P(\text{Rain}) = 0.8$, then the probability of it NOT raining is:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\text{Rain}') &= 1 - 0.8 \\
&= 0.2
\end{aligned}
$$

## 3. Calculating the Probability of a Single Event

To find the probability of a single event where all outcomes are equally likely, use this fundamental formula:

$$
P(\text{Event}) = \frac{\text{Number of successful outcomes}}{\text{Total number of possible outcomes}}
$$

Information for these calculations can be presented to you in plain text, in **frequency tables**, or in **Venn diagrams**.

### Using Venn Diagrams for Probability
When using a Venn diagram, the "total number of possible outcomes" is the sum of all the numbers (or frequencies) inside the entire Universal Set ($\mathscr{E}$ or $U$).

In the Venn diagram above, the numbers represent frequencies (how many items are in each section).

* Total items = $5 + 3 + 8 + 4 = 20$.
* Number of items in set $A$ = $5 + 3 = 8$.
* Therefore, $P(A) = \frac{8}{20} = \frac{2}{5}$.

<Aside type="caution" title="Venn Diagram Trap">
When a question asks for $P(A)$ from a Venn diagram, you must add up **all** the numbers inside the $A$ circle, including the intersection! Don't just take the number sitting alone in the outer crescent of $A$.
</Aside>

    The probability that a bus arrives late is $0.15$. Let $L$ be the event that the bus is late.
    
    a) Write down $P(L)$.

    b) Calculate $P(L')$, the probability that the bus is NOT late. Give your answer as a percentage.

    **a)**
    <Steps>
      1. $P(L) = 0.15$.
    </Steps>

    **b)**
    <Steps>
      1. Use the complement rule: $P(L') = 1 - P(L)$.
      2. $P(L') = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85$.
      3. To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100: $0.85 \times 100 = 85\%$.
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>

      3. Alternatively, add up all the tokens that are NOT green: $8 (\text{Red}) + 12 (\text{Blue}) + 4 (\text{Yellow}) = 24$.
      4. Therefore, $P(\text{Green}') = \frac{24}{30}$.
    </Steps>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 8: Missing Probabilities in a Table">
    A biased dice has four faces numbered 1, 2, 3, and 4. The probability of rolling each number is shown in the table below.

    | Outcome | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | Probability | $0.2$ | $x$ | $0.35$ | $0.15$ |

      2. Combine the known probabilities: $0.2 + 0.35 + 0.15 = 0.7$.

      4. Subtract $0.7$ from both sides: $x = 1 - 0.7 = 0.3$.

    <Steps>
      1. The even numbers on this dice are 2 and 4.
      2. $P(\text{Even}) = P(2) + P(4)$.
      3. $P(\text{Even}) = 0.3 + 0.15 = 0.45$.
    </Steps>
