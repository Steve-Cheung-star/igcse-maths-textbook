Probability is the mathematical way of measuring how likely it is that an event will happen. Whether you are tossing a coin, rolling a dice, or predicting the weather, probability provides a numerical value to "chance."

<Aside type="note" title="The Probability Scale">
Probabilities always exist on a scale from **0 to 1**. 
* **0** means the event is absolutely **impossible**.
* **1** means the event is absolutely **certain**.
* **0.5** means there is an **even chance** (a 50/50 probability).
</Aside>

<SteveTip title="Acceptable Formats">
Probabilities MUST be written as a **fraction**, **decimal**, or **percentage**. You will lose marks if you write a probability as a ratio (e.g., writing "1:4" instead of "1/4" or "0.25" is incorrect for probability!).
</SteveTip>

## 2. Probability Notation and The Complement Rule

To save time writing long sentences, mathematicians use specific notation. Let $A$ represent an event (like "rolling a 6").

* **$P(A)$** means "the probability of event $A$ occurring."
* **$P(A')$** means "the probability of event $A$ **NOT** occurring." The little dash is called the prime symbol, and this is known as the **complement** of $A$.

<Aside type="tip" title="The Complement Rule">
Because an event must either happen or not happen, the probabilities of these two outcomes must add up to 1 (or 100%).

$$
P(A) + P(A') = 1
$$

Therefore, you can always find the probability of an event *not* happening by subtracting the probability of it happening from 1:

$$
P(A') = 1 - P(A)
$$
</Aside>

**Example:** If the probability of it raining tomorrow is $P(\text{Rain}) = 0.8$, then the probability of it NOT raining is:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\text{Rain}') &= 1 - 0.8 \\
&= 0.2
\end{aligned}
$$

## 3. Calculating the Probability of a Single Event

To find the probability of a single event where all outcomes are equally likely, use this fundamental formula:

$$
P(\text{Event}) = \frac{\text{Number of successful outcomes}}{\text{Total number of possible outcomes}}
$$

Information for these calculations can be presented to you in plain text, in **frequency tables**, or in **Venn diagrams**.

### Using Venn Diagrams for Probability
When using a Venn diagram, the "total number of possible outcomes" is the sum of all the numbers (or frequencies) inside the entire Universal Set ($\mathscr{E}$ or $U$).

In the Venn diagram above, the numbers represent frequencies (how many items are in each section).
* Total items = $5 + 3 + 8 + 4 = 20$.
* Number of items in set $A$ = $5 + 3 = 8$.
* Therefore, $P(A) = \frac{8}{20} = \frac{2}{5}$.

<Aside type="caution" title="Venn Diagram Trap">
When a question asks for $P(A)$ from a Venn diagram, you must add up **all** the numbers inside the $A$ circle, including the intersection! Don't just take the number sitting alone in the outer crescent of $A$.
</Aside>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: The Complement Rule">
    The probability that a bus arrives late is $0.15$. 
    Let $L$ be the event that the bus is late.
    1. Write down $P(L)$.
    2. Calculate $P(L')$, the probability that the bus is NOT late. Give your answer as a percentage.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    <Steps>
      1. $P(L) = 0.15$.
      2. Use the complement rule: $P(L') = 1 - P(L)$.
      3. $P(L') = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85$.
      4. To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100: $0.85 \times 100 = 85\%$.
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Single Event from a Table">
    A bag contains 30 coloured tokens. The table below shows the frequencies of each colour.
    
    | Colour | Red | Blue | Green | Yellow |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | Frequency | 8 | 12 | 6 | 4 |

    A token is chosen at random. Find the probability that the token is:
    1. Blue.
    2. Not Green.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    <Steps>
      1. Verify the total number of outcomes: $8 + 12 + 6 + 4 = 30$.
      2. For Blue: There are 12 blue tokens. $P(\text{Blue}) = \frac{12}{30}$. (This simplifies to $\frac{2}{5}$, but $\frac{12}{30}$ is fully acceptable).
      3. For Not Green: First, find $P(\text{Green}) = \frac{6}{30}$.
      4. Use the complement rule: $P(\text{Green}') = 1 - \frac{6}{30} = \frac{24}{30}$.
      5. Alternatively, add up all the tokens that are NOT green: $8 (\text{Red}) + 12 (\text{Blue}) + 4 (\text{Yellow}) = 24$. Therefore, $P(\text{Green}') = \frac{24}{30}$.
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Probability from a Venn Diagram">
    In a class of 35 students, some study Art ($A$) and some study Music ($M$).
    * 12 students study both Art and Music.
    * 8 students study Art only.
    * 10 students study Music only.
    * 5 students study neither.

    A student is chosen at random from the whole class. Find:
    1. $P(A)$
    2. $P(M')$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    <Steps>
      1. The total number of students is 35. This is our denominator.
      2. To find $P(A)$, we must count everyone who studies Art. This includes the "Art only" students AND the "Both" students.
      3. Number of students in $A = 8 + 12 = 20$.
      4. $P(A) = \frac{20}{35}$ (simplifies to $\frac{4}{7}$).
      5. To find $P(M')$, we must count everyone who does NOT study Music. This includes the "Art only" students AND the "Neither" students.
      6. Number of students not studying Music $= 8 + 5 = 13$.
      7. $P(M') = \frac{13}{35}$.
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
