Percentages are simply fractions out of 100. In the IGCSE 0607 syllabus, you must be able to move between fractions, decimals, and percentages fluently to solve complex problems.

---

## 1. Percentage Change

This is a frequent calculation in both Paper 2 and Paper 4. You are comparing a **New Value** to an **Original Value**.

<Aside type="tip" title="The Golden Formula">

$$ \text{Percentage Change} = \frac{\text{New} - \text{Original}}{\text{Original}} \times 100 $$

</Aside>

### Example: Price Increase

A pair of shoes costs 80. The price increases to 100. Find the percentage increase.

<Steps>

1.  **Find the difference (the increase):**
    
    $100 - 80 = 20$

2.  **Divide by the Original Value (80):**
    
    $$ \frac{20}{80} = 0.25 $$

3.  **Multiply by 100 to get the percentage:**
    
    $0.25 \times 100 = 25\%$

</Steps>

**The final answer is a 25% increase.**

---

## 2. Reverse Percentages

You use this when you know the **result** of a change and need to find the **starting value**.

<Aside type="danger" title="Common Pitfall">

**Never** calculate the percentage of the final value and add or subtract it. You must use a **multiplier**.

</Aside>

### The Multiplier Method

* To **increase** by 15%, multiply by $1.15$
* To **decrease** by 15%, multiply by $0.85$

**Example:** A phone is sold for 510 after a 15% discount. Find the original price ($x$).

$$ x \times 0.85 = 510 $$

$$ x = \frac{510}{0.85} = 600 $$

The original price was **600**.

## 3. Practice Problems

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question">

    A car depreciates in value by 12% per year. If it is worth 17,600 now, what was it worth exactly one year ago?

  </TabItem>
  
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution">

    Since it is a 12% decrease, the multiplier is $0.88$.
    
    $$ \text{Original Value} = \frac{17,600}{0.88} $$
    
    **Answer:** The car was worth **20,000**.

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
