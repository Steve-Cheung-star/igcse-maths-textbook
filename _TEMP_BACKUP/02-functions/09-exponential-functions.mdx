## 1. Exponential Functions

An exponential function has a constant base raised to a variable power. The two standard forms you must know are $f(x) = a^x$ (where $a > 0, a \neq 1$) and the natural exponential $f(x) = e^x$. Euler's number, $e \approx 2.718$, is the standard base for continuous growth.

### Key Features of $f(x) = a^x$ and $f(x) = e^x$
* **Domain:** All real numbers, $x \in \mathbb{R}$.
* **Range:** Strictly positive values, $f(x) > 0$.
* **Horizontal Asymptote:** The $x$-axis ($y = 0$). The graph flattens out to the left (for $a > 1$) but never touches zero.
* **$y$-intercept:** Passes through $(0, 1)$ because any non-zero number to the power of $0$ is $1$.

---

## 2. Logarithmic Functions

The logarithmic function is the inverse of the exponential function. The standard forms are $f(x) = \log_a x$ and the natural logarithm $f(x) = \ln x$ (which means $\log_e x$).

### Key Features of $f(x) = \log_a x$ and $f(x) = \ln x$
* **Domain:** Strictly positive values, $x > 0$. You cannot take the log of zero or a negative number.
* **Range:** All real numbers, $y \in \mathbb{R}$.
* **Vertical Asymptote:** The $y$-axis ($x = 0$). The graph plunges downwards as $x$ approaches zero.
* **$x$-intercept:** Passes through $(1, 0)$ because $\log_a 1 = 0$.

Because they are inverses, reflecting the graph of $y = e^x$ across the line $y = x$ perfectly produces the graph of $y = \ln x$.

## 3. Relationships and Conversions

To solve equations, you must be able to convert between exponential and logarithmic forms fluidly.

**The Base Conversion Rule:**
Any exponential function can be rewritten using base $e$:
$$ a^x = e^{x \ln a} $$

**The Inverse Properties:**
Applying a log to its matching exponential base (or vice versa) cancels them out:
$$ \log_a(a^x) = x $$
$$ a^{\log_a x} = x $$
$$ \ln(e^x) = x $$
$$ e^{\ln x} = x $$

## Practice Questions

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Key Features and Inverses">
    Let $f(x) = e^{x-2} + 3$.
    
    a) Write down the equation of the horizontal asymptote of $f$.  
    b) Find the exact coordinates of the $y$-intercept.  
    c) Find an expression for $f^{-1}(x)$, and state its domain.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    **a)** The base function $e^x$ has an asymptote at $y = 0$. The "+3" at the end translates the entire graph vertically upwards by 3 units.
    The horizontal asymptote is **$y = 3$**.

    **b)** To find the $y$-intercept, set $x = 0$:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    y &= e^{0-2} + 3 \\
    y &= e^{-2} + 3
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    The exact coordinates are **$(0, e^{-2} + 3)$** or **$(0, \frac{1}{e^2} + 3)$**.

    **c)** To find the inverse, set $f(x) = y$, swap $x$ and $y$, and solve for $y$:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    x &= e^{y-2} + 3 \\
    x - 3 &= e^{y-2}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Take the natural logarithm ($\ln$) of both sides to remove the base $e$:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \ln(x - 3) &= y - 2 \\
    y &= \ln(x - 3) + 2
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    So, $f^{-1}(x) = \ln(x - 3) + 2$.
    The domain of the inverse is the range of the original function. Since $f(x) > 3$, the domain of $f^{-1}(x)$ is **$x > 3$**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Solving Equations Algebraically">
    Solve the following equations for $x$:
    
    a) $3^{2x} = 27^{x-1}$  
    b) $\ln(x+2) + \ln(x) = \ln(8)$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    **a)** Express both sides using the same base. We know that $27 = 3^3$.
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    3^{2x} &= (3^3)^{x-1} \\
    3^{2x} &= 3^{3x-3}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Since the bases are equal, we can equate the exponents:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    2x &= 3x - 3 \\
    -x &= -3 \\
    x &= 3
    \end{aligned}
    $$

    **b)** Use the laws of logarithms ($\ln a + \ln b = \ln(ab)$) to combine the left side:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \ln(x(x+2)) &= \ln(8) \\
    \ln(x^2 + 2x) &= \ln(8)
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Since we have a single natural log on both sides, their arguments must be equal (or raise $e$ to the power of both sides):
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    x^2 + 2x &= 8 \\
    x^2 + 2x - 8 &= 0 \\
    (x + 4)(x - 2) &= 0
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    So $x = -4$ or $x = 2$.
    **Check the domain:** You cannot take the logarithm of a negative number. If $x = -4$, then $\ln(x)$ becomes $\ln(-4)$, which is undefined. 
    Therefore, the only valid solution is **$x = 2$**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Financial Modelling (Continuous Growth)">
    An entrepreneur invests **5000 EUR** into an account that pays a nominal annual interest rate of $4.5\%$, compounded continuously. 
    The formula for continuous compounding is $V = Pe^{rt}$.
    
    a) Find the exact value of the investment after $5$ years. Give your answer to 2 decimal places.  
    b) Using algebraic methods, find the exact time it will take for the investment to double to **10000 EUR**.  
    c) Verify your answer to part (b) using your GDC.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    **a)** We have $P = 5000$, $r = 0.045$, and $t = 5$.
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    V &= 5000 e^{0.045 \times 5} \\
    V &= 5000 e^{0.225}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Using a calculator, $V = 6261.611...$
    The value is **6261.61 EUR**.

    **b)** We want the final value $V = 10000$.
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    10000 &= 5000 e^{0.045t}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Divide both sides by $5000$:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    2 &= e^{0.045t}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Take the natural logarithm of both sides to isolate the exponent:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \ln(2) &= \ln(e^{0.045t}) \\
    \ln(2) &= 0.045t \\
    t &= \dfrac{\ln(2)}{0.045}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    The exact time is $\mathbf{\frac{\ln(2)}{0.045}}$ **years** (approx $15.4$ years).

    **b)** To verify on the TI-Nspire:
    1. Open a **Calculator** page.
    2. Press **[Menu] > 3: Algebra > 1: Numerical Solve (nSolve)**.
    3. Type `nSolve(10000 = 5000 * e^(0.045 * t), t)`.
    4. The calculator will return $15.40327...$, confirming your exact algebraic answer.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Scientific Modelling (Radioactive Decay)">
    The mass, $M$, in grams, of a radioactive isotope remaining after $t$ days is modelled by the function:
    $$
    M(t) = 150 e^{-0.02t}
    $$
    
    a) Write down the initial mass of the isotope.  
    b) Find the mass remaining after 30 days.  
    c) The *half-life* is the time required for the mass to reduce to exactly half of its initial amount. Determine the half-life of this isotope.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    **a)** The initial mass occurs at $t = 0$.
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    M(0) &= 150 e^{-0.02(0)} \\
    M(0) &= 150 e^0 \\
    M(0) &= 150 \times 1
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    The initial mass is **$150$ grams**.

    **b)** Substitute $t = 30$ into the model:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    M(30) &= 150 e^{-0.02(30)} \\
    M(30) &= 150 e^{-0.6}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Using a calculator, $M(30) = 82.321...$
    The remaining mass is **$82.3$ grams** (to 3 s.f.).

    **c)** The initial mass is $150$, so half of that is $75$. We need to solve for $t$ when $M(t) = 75$.
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    75 &= 150 e^{-0.02t}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Divide by $150$:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    0.5 &= e^{-0.02t}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    Take the natural logarithm of both sides:
    $$
    \begin{aligned}
    \ln(0.5) &= -0.02t \\
    t &= \dfrac{\ln(0.5)}{-0.02}
    \end{aligned}
    $$
    $t = 34.657...$
    The half-life is approximately **$34.7$ days**.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
