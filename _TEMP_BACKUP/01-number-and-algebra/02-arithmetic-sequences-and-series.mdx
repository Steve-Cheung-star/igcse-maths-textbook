Mathematics is fundamentally the study of patterns. When a pattern consists of a list of numbers progressing by a constant addition or subtraction, we call it an **arithmetic sequence**. When we add those numbers together, it becomes an **arithmetic series**. 

In the AA SL course, you are expected to move beyond simply identifying the next number in a pattern. You will use rigorous algebra to find the $100^{\text{th}}$ term, sum the first $n$ terms using quadratic equations, decode $\Sigma$ (sigma) notation, and apply these concepts to model discrete real-world phenomena.

## 1. Arithmetic Sequences (The $n^{\text{th}}$ Term)

An arithmetic sequence is an ordered list of terms where the difference between any two consecutive terms is strictly constant. We call this the **common difference**, denoted by $d$. 

If the sequence is increasing, $d$ is positive. If the sequence is decreasing, $d$ is negative.

<FormulaBox title="1.2 The nth term of an arithmetic sequence">
  $$
  u_n=u_1+(n-1)d
  $$
</FormulaBox>

Where:
* $u_n$ is the value of the $n^{\text{th}}$ term.
* $u_1$ is the first term.
* $n$ is the position of the term ($1^{\text{st}}, 2^{\text{nd}}, 3^{\text{rd}}$, etc.).
* $d$ is the common difference, found by calculating $u_n - u_{n-1}$ (e.g., $u_2 - u_1$).

## 2. Arithmetic Series & Sigma Notation

An arithmetic **series** is formed when you add the terms of an arithmetic sequence together. The sum of the first $n$ terms is denoted by $S_n$.

<FormulaBox title="1.2 The sum of n terms of an arithmetic sequence">
  $$
  S_n=\frac{n}{2}(2u_1+(n-1)d) \quad ; \quad S_n=\frac{n}{2}(u_1+u_n)
  $$
</FormulaBox>

You must strategically choose which formula to use:
1. Use the first version when you know the number of terms ($n$), the first term ($u_1$), and the common difference ($d$).
2. Use the second version when you know the number of terms ($n$), the first term ($u_1$), and the **last term** ($u_n$).

### Decoding Sigma ($\Sigma$) Notation
Sigma notation is a concise way to represent a series. The Greek letter $\Sigma$ stands for "sum". 

$$
\sum_{r=1}^{n} (ar + b)
$$

* **Bottom ($r=1$):** The starting value of $r$ (the index). Substitute this into the expression to find $u_1$.
* **Top ($n$):** The final value of $r$. Substitute this into the expression to find the last term $u_n$.
* **Expression ($ar+b$):** The rule for the sequence. Because it is linear, it guarantees the sequence is arithmetic.

<Aside type="note" title="Key Formula to Memorise">
  $$
  \text{Number of terms in a sigma series} = (\text{Top number}) - (\text{Bottom number}) + 1
  $$
  *(Note: This is not provided in your exam formula booklet! If a series goes from $r=5$ to $r=12$, there are $12 - 5 + 1 = 8$ terms, not 7!)*
</Aside>

## Practice Questions

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 1: Finding a Specific Term">
    Consider the arithmetic sequence: $17, 13, 9, 5, \dots$
    
    a) Write down the value of the first term, $u_1$, and the common difference, $d$.
    
    b) Find the value of the $35^{\text{th}}$ term.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 1">
    **Part a:**
    The first term is clearly stated:
    $$
    u_1 = 17
    $$
    Calculate $d$ by subtracting the first term from the second:
    $$
    d = 13 - 17 = -4
    $$
    
    **Part b:**
    Use the $n^{\text{th}}$ term formula with $n = 35$:
    $$
    u_n = u_1 + (n-1)d
    $$
    $$
    u_{35} = 17 + (35-1)(-4)
    $$
    $$
    u_{35} = 17 + (34)(-4)
    $$
    $$
    u_{35} = 17 - 136 = -119
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 2: Finding the Sum">
    An arithmetic sequence has a first term of $12$ and a common difference of $6$. 
    Find the sum of the first $20$ terms.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 2">
    We are given $u_1 = 12$, $d = 6$, and $n = 20$. 
    Because we do not know the last term, we use the first sum formula:
    
    $$
    S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2u_1 + (n-1)d)
    $$
    $$
    S_{20} = \frac{20}{2}(2(12) + (20-1)(6))
    $$
    $$
    S_{20} = 10(24 + (19)(6))
    $$
    $$
    S_{20} = 10(24 + 114)
    $$
    $$
    S_{20} = 10(138) = 1380
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 3: Finding 'n' given the last term">
    Consider the finite arithmetic sequence: $-5, -1, 3, 7, \dots, 103$.
    
    a) Find the number of terms in this sequence.
    
    b) Calculate the sum of the sequence.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 3">
    **Part a:**
    We know $u_1 = -5$, $d = 4$, and the $n^{\text{th}}$ term is $u_n = 103$. We must solve for $n$:
    $$
    103 = -5 + (n-1)(4)
    $$
    $$
    108 = 4(n-1)
    $$
    $$
    27 = n - 1
    $$
    $$
    n = 28
    $$
    There are 28 terms in the sequence.

    **Part b:**
    Since we know the first and last terms, we can use the shorter sum formula:
    $$
    S_n = \frac{n}{2}(u_1 + u_n)
    $$
    $$
    S_{28} = \frac{28}{2}(-5 + 103)
    $$
    $$
    S_{28} = 14(98) = 1372
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

  <TabItem label="📝 Question 4: Sigma Notation">
    Evaluate the following expression:
    $$
    \sum_{r=1}^{15} (3r - 2)
    $$
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 4">
    First, establish the key parameters by substituting values for $r$.
    
    Find the first term ($u_1$) by letting $r = 1$:
    $$
    u_1 = 3(1) - 2 = 1
    $$
    
    Find the last term ($u_{15}$) by letting $r = 15$:
    $$
    u_{15} = 3(15) - 2 = 43
    $$
    
    The number of terms $n$ is $15 - 1 + 1 = 15$.
    
    Now, use the sum formula $S_n = \frac{n}{2}(u_1 + u_n)$:
    $$
    S_{15} = \frac{15}{2}(1 + 43)
    $$
    $$
    S_{15} = 7.5 \times 44 = 330
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 5: Simultaneous Equations (Paper 1 Classic)">
    In an arithmetic sequence, the $4^{\text{th}}$ term is $21$ and the $10^{\text{th}}$ term is $51$.
    
    a) Find the first term ($u_1$) and the common difference ($d$).
    
    b) Find the value of the $20^{\text{th}}$ term.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 5">
    **Part a:**
    Set up two equations using the $n^{\text{th}}$ term formula:
    $$
    u_4 = u_1 + 3d = 21 \quad \text{--- (Equation 1)}
    $$
    $$
    u_{10} = u_1 + 9d = 51 \quad \text{--- (Equation 2)}
    $$
    
    Subtract Equation 1 from Equation 2 to eliminate $u_1$:
    $$
    (u_1 + 9d) - (u_1 + 3d) = 51 - 21
    $$
    $$
    6d = 30
    $$
    $$
    d = 5
    $$
    
    Substitute $d = 5$ back into Equation 1:
    $$
    u_1 + 3(5) = 21
    $$
    $$
    u_1 + 15 = 21
    $$
    $$
    u_1 = 6
    $$
    
    **Part b:**
    Now find $u_{20}$:
    $$
    u_{20} = 6 + (20-1)(5)
    $$
    $$
    u_{20} = 6 + 19(5) = 6 + 95 = 101
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 6: Solving a Quadratic for 'n'">
    An arithmetic sequence is given by $8, 11, 14, 17, \dots$
    The sum of the first $n$ terms is $440$. Find the value of $n$.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 6">
    Identify the parameters: $u_1 = 8$, $d = 3$, and $S_n = 440$.
    Substitute these into the sum formula:
    $$
    440 = \frac{n}{2}(2(8) + (n-1)(3))
    $$
    
    Multiply both sides by 2 to clear the fraction:
    $$
    880 = n(16 + 3n - 3)
    $$
    $$
    880 = n(13 + 3n)
    $$
    
    Expand and rearrange into a standard quadratic equation ($ax^2 + bx + c = 0$):
    $$
    3n^2 + 13n - 880 = 0
    $$
    
    Solve using the quadratic formula or by factorising (or GDC if Paper 2). Factorising gives:
    $$
    (3n + 55)(n - 16) = 0
    $$
    
    So, $n = -\frac{55}{3}$ or $n = 16$.
    Since $n$ represents the number of terms, it must be a positive integer ($n \in \mathbb{Z}^+$). Therefore, we reject the negative fraction.
    
    Final Answer:
    $$
    n = 16
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 7: Contextual Application">
    A theatre has 25 rows of seats. There are $14$ seats in the first row, $16$ seats in the second row, $18$ seats in the third row, and so on, following an arithmetic sequence.
    
    a) Find the number of seats in the $25^{\text{th}}$ row.
    
    b) Calculate the total seating capacity of the theatre.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 7">
    **Part a:**
    This is an arithmetic sequence where $u_1 = 14$, $d = 2$, and we want to find $u_{25}$.
    $$
    u_{25} = 14 + (25-1)(2)
    $$
    $$
    u_{25} = 14 + 24(2)
    $$
    $$
    u_{25} = 14 + 48 = 62 \text{ seats}
    $$
    
    **Part b:**
    We need the sum of all 25 rows ($S_{25}$). We can use the formula with the first and last term:
    $$
    S_{25} = \frac{25}{2}(14 + 62)
    $$
    $$
    S_{25} = 12.5(76) = 950 \text{ total seats}
    $$
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="📝 Question 8: Algebraic Sequence (Show That)">
    The first three terms of an arithmetic sequence are $x$, $2x-5$, and $x+2$.
    
    a) Find the value of $x$.
    
    b) Hence, write down the first three terms of the sequence and state the common difference.
  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="✅ Solution 8">
    **Part a:**
    Because it is an arithmetic sequence, the difference between consecutive terms must be equal. Therefore:
    $$
    u_2 - u_1 = u_3 - u_2
    $$
    
    Substitute the algebraic expressions:
    $$
    (2x - 5) - x = (x + 2) - (2x - 5)
    $$
    
    Simplify both sides:
    $$
    x - 5 = -x + 7
    $$
    
    Solve for $x$:
    $$
    2x = 12
    $$
    $$
    x = 6
    $$
    
    **Part b:**
    Substitute $x = 6$ back into the expressions:
    * $u_1 = 6$
    * $u_2 = 2(6) - 5 = 12 - 5 = 7$
    * $u_3 = 6 + 2 = 8$
    
    The first three terms are $6, 7, 8$. The common difference is $d = 1$.
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
