1.5 Ordering and Inequalities

In IGCSE Maths, you will frequently be asked to order a random mixture of fractions, decimals, percentages, and even powers. To do this successfully, you need a reliable strategy and a solid understanding of mathematical symbols.

1. The Language of Inequalities

Section titled “1. The Language of Inequalities”

Before you can order numbers, you need to know how to compare them mathematically. You must be completely familiar with these six symbols:

  • === (Equal to): 4=824 = \frac{8}{2}4=28​
  • ≠\neq= (Not equal to): 5≠35 \neq 35=3
  • >>> (Greater than): 7>27 > 27>2
  • <<< (Less than): 0.5<10.5 < 10.5<1
  • ≥\geq≥ (Greater than or equal to): x≥10x \geq 10x≥10 (x is 10 or bigger)
  • ≤\leq≤ (Less than or equal to): y≤0y \leq 0y≤0 (y is 0 or negative)
Steve's Exam Wisdom

The Banned Crocodile

Please, I am begging you: forget the primary school “hungry crocodile” trick! Every time a student talks about a crocodile eating the bigger number, a secondary school maths teacher’s hair turns grey.

Look at the inequality symbols as literal arrowheads sitting on a number line:

  • <<< points to the left (the direction of the smaller, negative numbers).
  • >>> points to the right (the direction of the larger, positive numbers).

The pointy end always points exactly to where the smaller number lives on the number line. It is perfectly logical. No reptiles required!

2. Ordering Mixed Quantities

Section titled “2. Ordering Mixed Quantities”

Examiners love to test your conversion skills (from Section 1.4) at the exact same time they test your ordering skills. You might be asked to order a list like: 38,38%,0.3,13\frac{3}{8}, \quad 38\%, \quad 0.3, \quad \frac{1}{3}83​,38%,0.3,31​.

The Foolproof 3-Step Strategy:

Section titled “The Foolproof 3-Step Strategy:”
  1. Convert Everything to Decimals: Decimals are by far the easiest format to compare.
  2. Pad with Zeroes: Make sure every decimal has the same number of decimal places. This stops your brain from thinking 0.30.30.3 is smaller than 0.150.150.15 just because 333 is smaller than 151515.
  3. Sort and Revert: Order the numbers, but write your final answer using the original forms given in the question.
Steve's Exam Wisdom

The 'Original Form' Trap

This is where students lose the final mark! If the question asks you to order 12\frac{1}{2}21​ and 0.40.40.4, and you write "0.4,0.50.4, 0.50.4,0.5" on the answer line, you will lose a mark. You must write "0.4,120.4, \frac{1}{2}0.4,21​". The examiner wants to see the numbers exactly as they were printed in the question.

3. The Negative Number Trap

Section titled “3. The Negative Number Trap”

When dealing with negative numbers, your brain has to work in reverse. The “bigger” a negative number looks, the smaller its actual value is. This is because it is further to the left on the number line.

← Smaller −3−2−10123→ Larger \xleftarrow{\text{ Smaller }} \quad -3 \quad -2 \quad -1 \quad 0 \quad 1 \quad 2 \quad 3 \quad \xrightarrow{\text{ Larger }} Smaller ​−3−2−10123 Larger ​

Visualising an Example

Section titled “Visualising an Example”

Let’s place these four numbers on a number line to see how they relate:
−2.5…−0.5…1.5…−1.2-2.5 \quad \dots \quad -0.5 \quad \dots \quad 1.5 \quad \dots \quad -1.2−2.5…−0.5…1.5…−1.2

If we map their approximate positions:

←−3↑−2.5−2↑−1.2−1↑−0.501↑1.52→\xleftarrow{} \quad -3 \quad \underset{\mathbf{-2.5}}{\mathbf{\uparrow}} \quad -2 \quad \underset{\mathbf{-1.2}}{\mathbf{\uparrow}} \quad -1 \quad \underset{\mathbf{-0.5}}{\mathbf{\uparrow}} \quad 0 \quad 1 \quad \underset{\mathbf{1.5}}{\mathbf{\uparrow}} \quad 2 \quad \xrightarrow{}​−3−2.5↑​−2−1.2↑​−1−0.5↑​011.5↑​2​

By reading the number line directly from left to right, we automatically have our ascending order (smallest to largest): −2.5,−1.2,−0.5,1.5-2.5, \quad -1.2, \quad -0.5, \quad 1.5−2.5,−1.2,−0.5,1.5

And we can use our inequality arrows to state that: −2.5<−1.2<−0.5<1.5-2.5 < -1.2 < -0.5 < 1.5−2.5<−1.2<−0.5<1.5

4. Practice Problems

Section titled “4. Practice Problems”
  • 📝 Question 1: Using Symbols
  • ✅ Solution 1

Insert the correct symbol (<,>,<, >,<,>, or ===) between these pairs of numbers:

  1. −4…−7-4 \quad \dots \quad -7−4…−7
  2. 0.6…350.6 \quad \dots \quad \frac{3}{5}0.6…53​
  3. 23…322^3 \quad \dots \quad 3^223…32
  1. −4>−7-4 > -7−4>−7 (Negative 4 is further to the right on the number line than negative 7).
  2. 0.6=350.6 = \frac{3}{5}0.6=53​ (35\frac{3}{5}53​ converts exactly to 0.6).
  3. 23<322^3 < 3^223<32 (Because 8<98 < 98<9).
  • 📝 Question 2: Ordering Mixed Formats
  • ✅ Solution 2

Write the following quantities in ascending order (smallest to largest): 0.35,38,30%,130.35, \quad \frac{3}{8}, \quad 30\%, \quad \frac{1}{3}0.35,83​,30%,31​

Step 1: Convert all to decimals.

  • 0.35→0.350.35 \rightarrow 0.350.35→0.35
  • 38→3÷8=0.375\frac{3}{8} \rightarrow 3 \div 8 = 0.37583​→3÷8=0.375
  • 30%→0.330\% \rightarrow 0.330%→0.3
  • 13→0.333...\frac{1}{3} \rightarrow 0.333...31​→0.333...

Step 2: Pad with zeroes (to 3 decimal places).

  • 0.3500.3500.350
  • 0.3750.3750.375
  • 0.3000.3000.300
  • 0.3330.3330.333

Step 3: Compare and revert to original forms. Smallest is 0.3000.3000.300, then 0.3330.3330.333, then 0.3500.3500.350, then 0.3750.3750.375.

Final Answer: 30%,13,0.35,3830\%, \quad \frac{1}{3}, \quad 0.35, \quad \frac{3}{8}30%,31​,0.35,83​
  • 📝 Question 3: Ordering with Negatives (Extended)
  • ✅ Solution 3

Write the following in descending order (largest to smallest): −0.5,−34,−0.09,−51%-0.5, \quad -\frac{3}{4}, \quad -0.09, \quad -51\%−0.5,−43​,−0.09,−51%

Step 1: Convert to decimals.

  • −0.5→−0.50-0.5 \rightarrow -0.50−0.5→−0.50
  • −34→−0.75-\frac{3}{4} \rightarrow -0.75−43​→−0.75
  • −0.09→−0.09-0.09 \rightarrow -0.09−0.09→−0.09
  • −51%→−0.51-51\% \rightarrow -0.51−51%→−0.51

Step 2: Sort from largest (closest to zero, furthest right) to smallest (most negative, furthest left). Largest is −0.09-0.09−0.09, then −0.50-0.50−0.50, then −0.51-0.51−0.51, then −0.75-0.75−0.75.

Final Answer: −0.09,−0.5,−51%,−34-0.09, \quad -0.5, \quad -51\%, \quad -\frac{3}{4}−0.09,−0.5,−51%,−43​
