1.9 Estimation

Estimation is the “common sense” check of mathematics. In a high-pressure exam, it is easy to type a number into your calculator incorrectly and get an answer that is 10 times too large. Estimation gives you the “ballpark” figure so you can spot those mistakes instantly.

1. Place Value: The Red vs. Blue Divide

Section titled “1. Place Value: The Red vs. Blue Divide”

To round correctly, you must know the name of the “target” column. Think of the decimal point as a mirror. As you move left, numbers get 10x larger (the Red Side). As you move right, they get 10x smaller (the Blue Side).

The Decimal Mirror

Section titled “The Decimal Mirror”
  • Whole Numbers (Red Side): These end in -s (Tens, Hundreds, Thousands, Millions).
  • Decimal Parts (Blue Side): These end in -ths (Tenths, Hundredths, Thousandths).

2. Rounding: The “Next Door” Rule

Section titled “2. Rounding: The “Next Door” Rule”

Rounding is the process of making a number “simpler” while keeping its value close to the original.

  1. Find your target: Underline the digit in the column you are rounding to.
  2. Look next door: Look at the digit immediately to the right.
  3. The Decision: * If it is 5, 6, 7, 8, or 9, round the target digit UP.
    • If it is 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4, keep the target digit THE SAME.
  4. Fill the gaps: For whole numbers, fill any remaining columns before the decimal with placeholder zeros.

The “Chain Reaction” (Rounding 9s)

Section titled “The “Chain Reaction” (Rounding 9s)”

When you round a 999 up, it becomes a 101010. You put down the 000 and “carry” the 111 to the next column on the left.

  • Example: Round 0.0960.0960.096 to 2 decimal places.
  • The 2nd d.p. is 999. Next door is 666 (round up).
  • 999 becomes 101010. The 111 carries to the first d.p.
  • Answer: 0.100.100.10
Steve's Exam Wisdom

The Essential Zero

If a question asks for hundredths (2 d.p.) and your answer is 4.54.54.5, you must write 4.504.504.50. In science and maths, 4.54.54.5 means “somewhere between 4.45 and 4.55,” but 4.504.504.50 means “exactly 4.50.” The zero proves your level of accuracy.

3. Significant Figures (s.f.)

Section titled “3. Significant Figures (s.f.)”

Significant figures (s.f.) are used to show the precision of a measurement. Unlike decimal places, they don’t care where the decimal point is—they care about the digits themselves.

The Three Types of Zeros

Section titled “The Three Types of Zeros”
  1. Leading Zeros (The Placeholders): 0.00040.00040.0004
    • These are NEVER significant. They just tell you how small the number is.
  2. Trapped Zeros (The Sandwiches): 400540054005
    • These are ALWAYS significant. They are part of the actual measurement.
  3. Trailing Zeros (The Precision-Makers): 5.2005.2005.200
    • In a decimal, these ARE significant because they show exactly how precisely you measured the number.

4. Estimation Strategies

Section titled “4. Estimation Strategies”

In the IGCSE, “Estimate” is code for: Round everything to 1 significant figure first.

Steve's Exam Wisdom

The 1 s.f. Method Mark

If you calculate 19.24.1\frac{19.2}{4.1}4.119.2​ on your calculator (4.68...4.68...4.68...) and then round it to 555, you get 0 marks. You must show the rounded values first: 204=5\frac{20}{4} = 5420​=5. The examiner is testing your ability to simplify the problem, not your ability to use a calculator.

Advanced Estimation Tricks

Section titled “Advanced Estimation Tricks”
  • Roots: Don’t round 35\sqrt{35}35​ to 40\sqrt{40}40​ (1 s.f.) if you can’t solve it. Round it to the nearest square number (36=6\sqrt{36} = 636​=6).
  • Division by Decimals: * Dividing by 0.50.50.5 is the same as doubling (×2\times 2×2).
    • Dividing by 0.20.20.2 is the same as multiplying by 5.
    • Dividing by 0.10.10.1 is the same as multiplying by 10.

Part B: Significant Figures

Section titled “Part B: Significant Figures”
  • 📝 Question 3: Leading Zeros
  • ✅ Solution 3

Write 0.0040960.0040960.004096 correct to 2 significant figures.

  1. Skip the leading zeros. The 1st s.f. is 4.
  2. The 2nd s.f. is 0 (trapped zeros count).
  3. The digit “next door” is 9. Round the 0 up to 1. Answer: 0.00410.00410.0041
  • 📝 Question 4: Trailing Zeros
  • ✅ Solution 4

Round 8.0028.0028.002 correct to 3 significant figures.

  1. The first three digits are 8, 0, and 0.
  2. The digit “next door” is 2.
  3. Keep the digits as they are. You must keep the zeros to show the 3 s.f. accuracy. Answer: 8.008.008.00
