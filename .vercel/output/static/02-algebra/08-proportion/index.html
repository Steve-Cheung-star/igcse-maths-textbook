2.8 Proportion

In mathematics, proportion (or variation) describes how one variable changes in relation to another. We use the proportional symbol ∝\propto∝ to show this relationship.

To solve proportion problems, we always replace the ∝\propto∝ symbol with an equals sign (===) and a constant of proportionality, usually represented by the letter kkk.

1. Translating Proportionality Statements

Section titled “1. Translating Proportionality Statements”

Exams will use specific phrasing to tell you exactly what power or root to use in your equation. You must memorize these translations before you can set up your algebra:

English PhrasingAlgebraic TranslationProportionality Equation
”…is proportional to xxx"xxxy=kxy = kxy=kx
"…the square of xxx"x2x^2x2y=kx2y = kx^2y=kx2
"…the cube of xxx"x3x^3x3y=kx3y = kx^3y=kx3
"…the square root of xxx"x\sqrt{x}x​y=kxy = k\sqrt{x}y=kx​
"…the cube root of xxx”x3\sqrt[3]{x}3x​y=kx3y = k\sqrt[3]{x}y=k3x​
Steve's Exam Wisdom

The Golden Rule of Proportion

Every standard proportion problem follows the exact same three steps:

  1. Set it up: Write the equation with kkk using the correct translation from the table above.
  2. Find kkk: Substitute the known pair of values to calculate your constant kkk.
  3. Solve: Rewrite the full equation with your new kkk value, then use it to find the final unknown quantity.

2. Direct Proportion

Section titled “2. Direct Proportion”

If two quantities are in direct proportion, as one increases, the other increases at the same relative rate.

Algebraic Direct Proportion

Section titled “Algebraic Direct Proportion”

Variables aren’t always proportional to just xxx. They can be proportional to powers or roots of xxx, as shown in the translation table.

Example: yyy is directly proportional to the square of xxx. When x=3x = 3x=3, y=36y = 36y=36. Find the value of yyy when x=5x = 5x=5.

Step 1: Set it up

y∝x2y=kx2\begin{aligned} y &\propto x^2 \\ y &= kx^2 \end{aligned}yy​∝x2=kx2​

Step 2: Find kkk Substitute x=3x = 3x=3 and y=36y = 36y=36:

36=k(3)236=9kk=4\begin{aligned} 36 &= k(3)^2 \\ 36 &= 9k \\ k &= 4 \end{aligned}3636k​=k(3)2=9k=4​

So, the exact formula connecting xxx and yyy is y=4x2y = 4x^2y=4x2.

Step 3: Solve for the unknown Substitute x=5x = 5x=5 into your new formula:

y=4(5)2y=4(25)y=100\begin{aligned} y &= 4(5)^2 \\ y &= 4(25) \\ y &= 100 \end{aligned}yyy​=4(5)2=4(25)=100​

Direct Proportion in Word Problems

Section titled “Direct Proportion in Word Problems”

Real-world scenarios often use direct proportion. Common examples include currency conversion (more USD = more EUR), or mass and volume (more volume of a liquid = more mass).

Example: The distance a heavy ball falls (ddd) is directly proportional to the square of the time (ttt) it has been falling. If it falls 202020 m in 222 seconds, how far does it fall in 555 seconds?

Step 1: Set up

d∝t2d=kt2\begin{aligned} d &\propto t^2 \\ d &= kt^2 \end{aligned}dd​∝t2=kt2​

Step 2: Find kkk

20=k(2)220=4kk=5\begin{aligned} 20 &= k(2)^2 \\ 20 &= 4k \\ k &= 5 \end{aligned}2020k​=k(2)2=4k=5​

The equation is d=5t2d = 5t^2d=5t2.

Step 3: Solve

d=5(5)2d=5(25)d=125\begin{aligned} d &= 5(5)^2 \\ d &= 5(25) \\ d &= 125 \end{aligned}ddd​=5(5)2=5(25)=125​

The ball falls 125125125 m.

3. Inverse Proportion

Section titled “3. Inverse Proportion”

If two quantities are in inverse proportion, as one increases, the other decreases at a proportional rate. We express this by making the variable a denominator (the reciprocal).

  • Standard Inverse: y∝1xy \propto \frac{1}{x}y∝x1​ which becomes y=kxy = \frac{k}{x}y=xk​
  • Inverse Square: y∝1x2y \propto \frac{1}{x^2}y∝x21​ which becomes y=kx2y = \frac{k}{x^2}y=x2k​

Algebraic Inverse Proportion

Section titled “Algebraic Inverse Proportion”

Example: ppp is inversely proportional to the square root of qqq. When q=25q = 25q=25, p=4p = 4p=4. Find the value of qqq when p=10p = 10p=10.

Step 1: Set it up

p∝1qp=kq\begin{aligned} p &\propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{q}} \\ p &= \frac{k}{\sqrt{q}} \end{aligned}pp​∝q​1​=q​k​​

Step 2: Find kkk Substitute q=25q = 25q=25 and p=4p = 4p=4:

4=k254=k5k=20\begin{aligned} 4 &= \frac{k}{\sqrt{25}} \\ 4 &= \frac{k}{5} \\ k &= 20 \end{aligned}44k​=25​k​=5k​=20​

The exact formula is p=20qp = \frac{20}{\sqrt{q}}p=q​20​.

Step 3: Solve for the unknown Substitute p=10p = 10p=10:

10=20qq=2010q=2\begin{aligned} 10 &= \frac{20}{\sqrt{q}} \\ \sqrt{q} &= \frac{20}{10} \\ \sqrt{q} &= 2 \end{aligned}10q​q​​=q​20​=1020​=2​

Square both sides to find qqq:

q=22q=4\begin{aligned} q &= 2^2 \\ q &= 4 \end{aligned}qq​=22=4​

Inverse Proportion Word Problems (The “Man-Power” Rule)

Section titled “Inverse Proportion Word Problems (The “Man-Power” Rule)”

The most common real-world application of inverse proportion is “Work and Time” problems involving man-power or speed.

Steve's Exam Wisdom

The More-is-Less Logic

Think logically about the scenario: If you have more workers on a job, it will take less time to finish it. If you drive at a higher speed, it will take less time to reach your destination. This “more is less” relationship is the definition of inverse proportion.

Example: A team of 555 decorators can finish painting a house in 121212 days. Assuming they all work at the exact same rate, how many decorators are needed to finish the house in 444 days?

Step 1: Set up Time (TTT) is inversely proportional to Workers (WWW).

T=kW\begin{aligned} T &= \frac{k}{W} \end{aligned}T​=Wk​​

Step 2: Find kkk Here, kkk represents the total number of “man-days” required for the whole job.

12=k5k=60\begin{aligned} 12 &= \frac{k}{5} \\ k &= 60 \end{aligned}12k​=5k​=60​

The equation is T=60WT = \frac{60}{W}T=W60​.

Step 3: Solve We need the job done in T=4T = 4T=4 days.

4=60W4W=60W=15\begin{aligned} 4 &= \frac{60}{W} \\ 4W &= 60 \\ W &= 15 \end{aligned}44WW​=W60​=60=15​

You will need 151515 decorators.

4. Linking Multiple Proportionality Models

Section titled “4. Linking Multiple Proportionality Models”

In harder higher-tier questions, you may be given two separate proportionality relationships that share a common variable. You will need to find the equations for both separately, and then substitute them into a combined algebraic expression.

These questions heavily test your ability to simplify algebraic fractions, use the rules of indices, and manipulate surds.

Example: Combining and Simplifying

Section titled “Example: Combining and Simplifying”

Question: xxx is directly proportional to the square of www. When w=2w = 2w=2, x=12x = 12x=12. yyy is inversely proportional to the fourth power of www. When w=2w = 2w=2, y=3y = 3y=3. Find an expression for xy\sqrt{xy}xy​ in its simplest form, in terms of www.

Step 1: Find the equation for xxx

x=k1w212=k1(2)212=4k1k1=3\begin{aligned} x &= k_1 w^2 \\ 12 &= k_1 (2)^2 \\ 12 &= 4k_1 \\ k_1 &= 3 \end{aligned}x1212k1​​=k1​w2=k1​(2)2=4k1​=3​

Equation 1: x=3w2x = 3w^2x=3w2

Step 2: Find the equation for yyy

y=k2w43=k2243=k216k2=48\begin{aligned} y &= \frac{k_2}{w^4} \\ 3 &= \frac{k_2}{2^4} \\ 3 &= \frac{k_2}{16} \\ k_2 &= 48 \end{aligned}y33k2​​=w4k2​​=24k2​​=16k2​​=48​

Equation 2: y=48w4y = \frac{48}{w^4}y=w448​

Step 3: Substitute both into the required expression and simplify We need to find xyxyxy. First, find xyxyxy:

xy=(3w2)×(48w4)xy=144w2w4xy=144w2\begin{aligned} xy &= (3w^2) \times \left(\frac{48}{w^4}\right) \\[1ex] xy &= \frac{144w^2}{w^4} \\[1ex] xy &= \frac{144}{w^2} \end{aligned}xyxyxy​=(3w2)×(w448​)=w4144w2​=w2144​​

Now, take the square root of the whole expression. Remember to square root both the numerator and the denominator:

xy=144w2xy=12w\begin{aligned} \sqrt{xy} &= \sqrt{\frac{144}{w^2}} \\[1ex] \sqrt{xy} &= \frac{12}{w} \end{aligned}xy​xy​​=w2144​​=w12​​

5. Identifying the Best Variation Model

Section titled “5. Identifying the Best Variation Model”

Sometimes, instead of being told the relationship, you are given a table of data and asked to find the model that fits.

To do this, you test different formulas to see which one produces a constant value for kkk across all pairs of data.

  • If y=kxy = kxy=kx, then k=yxk = \frac{y}{x}k=xy​ (Direct Linear)
  • If y=kx2y = kx^2y=kx2, then k=yx2k = \frac{y}{x^2}k=x2y​ (Direct Square)
  • If y=kxy = \frac{k}{x}y=xk​, then k=yxk = yxk=yx (Inverse Linear)

Example: Finding the Model from Data

Section titled “Example: Finding the Model from Data”

Identify the correct variation model for the data below:

xxx234
yyy122748

Let’s test the data against different models to see which gives a constant kkk.

Test 1: Linear (k=yxk = \frac{y}{x}k=xy​)

  • 12÷2=612 \div 2 = 612÷2=6
  • 27÷3=927 \div 3 = 927÷3=9

This is not constant. The relationship is NOT y=kxy = kxy=kx.

Test 2: Square (k=yx2k = \frac{y}{x^2}k=x2y​)

  • 12÷22=12÷4=312 \div 2^2 = 12 \div 4 = 312÷22=12÷4=3
  • 27÷32=27÷9=327 \div 3^2 = 27 \div 9 = 327÷32=27÷9=3
  • 48÷42=48÷16=348 \div 4^2 = 48 \div 16 = 348÷42=48÷16=3

Because yx2\frac{y}{x^2}x2y​ always equals 333, the constant of proportionality is k=3k = 3k=3. The correct variation model is y∝x2y \propto x^2y∝x2 (or y=3x2y = 3x^2y=3x2).
