6.2 Area and Perimeter

Understanding how to calculate the perimeter and area of 2D shapes is a core skill in geometry. In the IGCSE Extended exam, you will not be given the formulas for rectangles, parallelograms, or trapeziums—you must memorise them!

Perimeter vs. Area

  • Perimeter: The total distance around the outside of a 2D shape. Imagine walking along the boundary. It is measured in 1D units (e.g., cm\text{cm}cm, m\text{m}m).
  • Area: The amount of flat space inside the shape. Imagine painting the surface. It is measured in 2D squared units (e.g., cm2\text{cm}^2cm2, m2\text{m}^2m2).
Steve's Exam Wisdom

The Perpendicular Height Rule

For areas of triangles, parallelograms, and trapeziums, you must always use the perpendicular height (the height that meets the base at exactly 90∘90^\circ90∘). Examiners will frequently give you the “slant height” as a trap. Do not use the slanted edge for area!

1. Rectangles and Triangles

Section titled “1. Rectangles and Triangles”

These are your foundational shapes. All other area formulas are effectively derived from the rectangle.

The Rectangle

Section titled “The Rectangle”
  • Perimeter: Add all four sides together: 2×base+2×height2 \times \text{base} + 2 \times \text{height}2×base+2×height
  • Area: Area=base×height\text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{height}Area=base×height

The Triangle

Section titled “The Triangle”

Every triangle is exactly half of a rectangle (or half of a parallelogram).

  • Perimeter: Simply add the lengths of the three outer sides.
  • Area: Area=12×base×perpendicular height\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}Area=21​×base×perpendicular height

2. Parallelograms

Section titled “2. Parallelograms”

A parallelogram is like a rectangle that has been pushed over. If you chop off the triangular end of a parallelogram and move it to the other side, it forms a perfect rectangle.

Because of this, the area formula is exactly the same as a rectangle, provided you use the perpendicular height.

  • Perimeter: Add the four outer sides (2×base+2×slant edge2 \times \text{base} + 2 \times \text{slant edge}2×base+2×slant edge).
  • Area: Area=base×perpendicular height\text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{perpendicular height}Area=base×perpendicular height

3. Trapeziums

Section titled “3. Trapeziums”

A trapezium is a quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides. To find the area, we take the average of the two parallel sides and multiply it by the perpendicular height.

  • Perimeter: Add the four outer sides together.
  • Area: Area=12×(a+b)×h\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (a + b) \times hArea=21​×(a+b)×h (where aaa and bbb are the parallel sides, and hhh is the perpendicular height).

Section titled “Practice Questions”
  • 📝 Question 1: The Parallelogram Trap (Core)
  • ✅ Solution 1

A parallelogram has a base of 12 cm12\text{ cm}12 cm. Its slanted edge measures 7 cm7\text{ cm}7 cm, and its perpendicular height is 5 cm5\text{ cm}5 cm.

  1. Calculate the perimeter of the parallelogram.
  2. Calculate the area of the parallelogram.
  1. Perimeter: The perimeter requires the lengths of the outer boundaries. A parallelogram has two pairs of equal opposite sides (two bases of 12, two slanted edges of 7). Perimeter=12+12+7+7=38 cm\text{Perimeter} = 12 + 12 + 7 + 7 = 38\text{ cm}Perimeter=12+12+7+7=38 cm
  2. Area: The area requires the base and the perpendicular height. We ignore the slanted edge of 7cm for this calculation! Area=12×5=60 cm2\text{Area} = 12 \times 5 = 60\text{ cm}^2Area=12×5=60 cm2

  1. Identify the values: a=8a = 8a=8, b=14b = 14b=14, h=6h = 6h=6.
  2. Write down the trapezium area formula: Area=12×(a+b)×h\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (a + b) \times hArea=21​×(a+b)×h
  3. Substitute the values into the formula: Area=12×(8+14)×6\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (8 + 14) \times 6Area=21​×(8+14)×6
  4. Calculate the brackets first (BIDMAS): Area=12×(22)×6\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (22) \times 6Area=21​×(22)×6
  5. Complete the multiplication: Area=11×6=66 m2\text{Area} = 11 \times 6 = 66\text{ m}^2Area=11×6=66 m2

  • ✅ Solution 3

The area of a triangle is 45 cm245\text{ cm}^245 cm2. Its perpendicular height is 9 cm9\text{ cm}9 cm. Calculate the length of its base.

  1. Write down the formula for the area of a triangle: Area=12×base×height\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}Area=21​×base×height
  2. Substitute the known values into the equation: 45=12×base×945 = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times 945=21​×base×9
  3. Multiply both sides by 2 to remove the fraction: 90=base×990 = \text{base} \times 990=base×9
  4. Divide by 9 to isolate the base: base=90÷9=10 cm\text{base} = 90 \div 9 = 10\text{ cm}base=90÷9=10 cm
