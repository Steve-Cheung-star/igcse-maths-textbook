6.3 Circles, Arcs and Sectors

Circles are a massive part of the IGCSE geometry syllabus. In this section, you will extend your knowledge from basic whole circles to calculating fractions of a circle, known as arcs and sectors.

Circle Hierarchy & Anatomy

  • Circumference: The perimeter (total distance around the outside) of the full circle.
  • Arc: A partial curve along the circumference.
  • Sector: A 2D slice of the circle’s area, bounded by two radii and an arc (like a slice of pizza).

1. The Full Circle: Area and Circumference

Section titled “1. The Full Circle: Area and Circumference”

Even though the formulas for the area and circumference of a circle are often given in the exam’s formula sheet, you should know them by heart to save time.

  • Circumference: C=πdorC=2πrC = \pi d \quad \text{or} \quad C = 2\pi rC=πdorC=2πr
  • Area: A=πr2A = \pi r^2A=πr2
Steve's Exam Wisdom

Leaving answers 'In terms of π'

If a question asks for the answer “in terms of π\piπ”, do not use the π\piπ button on your calculator. Treat π\piπ exactly like an algebra variable (e.g., xxx). For example, if r=5r = 5r=5, the area is simply 25π25\pi25π. Do not write 78.5478.5478.54!

2. Minor vs. Major Arcs and Sectors

Section titled “2. Minor vs. Major Arcs and Sectors”

When you slice a circle using two radii, you don’t just create one sector—you create two!

  • Minor Sector/Arc: The smaller slice (subtended by an angle less than 180∘180^\circ180∘).
  • Major Sector/Arc: The larger remaining slice (subtended by a reflex angle greater than 180∘180^\circ180∘).

3. Calculating Arc Length and Sector Area

Section titled “3. Calculating Arc Length and Sector Area”

An arc is just a fraction of the full circumference. A sector is just a fraction of the full area. Because a full circle is 360∘360^\circ360∘, the fraction of the circle you are dealing with is always exactly θ360\frac{\theta}{360}360θ​, where θ\thetaθ is the angle at the centre.

Arc Length and Sector Area Formulas

  • Arc Length: Arc Length=θ360×2πr\text{Arc Length} = \frac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi rArc Length=360θ​×2πr
  • Sector Area: Sector Area=θ360×πr2\text{Sector Area} = \frac{\theta}{360} \times \pi r^2Sector Area=360θ​×πr2

The Perimeter Trap

Section titled “The Perimeter Trap”

Common Misconception

Arc Length is NOT the same as Sector Perimeter! Arc length is only the curved edge. If an exam asks for the perimeter of a sector, you must calculate the arc length and then add the two straight radii that form the straight edges!

Perimeter of Sector=Arc Length+2r\text{Perimeter of Sector} = \text{Arc Length} + 2rPerimeter of Sector=Arc Length+2r

Example: Area and Perimeter of a Sector

Section titled “Example: Area and Perimeter of a Sector”

Let’s see this in action. Consider a minor sector with a radius of 8 cm8 \text{ cm}8 cm and an angle of 45∘45^\circ45∘.

  1. Calculate the Area: Area=45360×π×82\text{Area} = \frac{45}{360} \times \pi \times 8^2Area=36045​×π×82 Area=18×64π=8π≈25.1 cm2\text{Area} = \frac{1}{8} \times 64\pi = 8\pi \approx 25.1 \text{ cm}^2Area=81​×64π=8π≈25.1 cm2
  2. Calculate the Arc Length: Arc Length=45360×2×π×8\text{Arc Length} = \frac{45}{360} \times 2 \times \pi \times 8Arc Length=36045​×2×π×8 Arc Length=18×16π=2π≈6.28 cm\text{Arc Length} = \frac{1}{8} \times 16\pi = 2\pi \approx 6.28 \text{ cm}Arc Length=81​×16π=2π≈6.28 cm
  3. Calculate the Total Perimeter: Add the arc length to the two straight radii. Perimeter=6.28+8+8\text{Perimeter} = 6.28 + 8 + 8Perimeter=6.28+8+8 Perimeter=22.28 cm\text{Perimeter} = 22.28 \text{ cm}Perimeter=22.28 cm
