3.2 Using a Graphic Display Calculator (TI-Nspire)

The Graphic Display Calculator (GDC) is the defining tool of the IGCSE 0607 Extended syllabus. Exams will frequently present you with unfamiliar, complex equations and expect you to find their exact features. You are not expected to use algebra for these; you are expected to use your TI-Nspire efficiently.

1. Setting Up Your TI-Nspire for Exams

Section titled “1. Setting Up Your TI-Nspire for Exams”

Before you sketch a single graph, you must ensure your calculator is correctly set up. A misconfigured GDC will give you wildly incorrect answers, especially in trigonometry.

The Degree vs. Radian Trap

By default, or after a reset, the TI-Nspire often switches to Radian mode. The IGCSE 0607 course uses Degrees. Look at the top right of your screen. If it says RAD, you must change it immediately! Go to Doc > 7: Settings & Status > 2: Document Settings and change the Angle to Degree. Make sure to select “Make Default”.

Resetting and Clearing

Section titled “Resetting and Clearing”

Before a real exam, it is best practice to start with a completely clean slate so previous variables don’t mess up your current calculations.

  • To clear all documents: Press Home (On button) > 2: My Documents > Press Menu > C: Clear My Documents.
  • To close a current document quickly: Press Ctrl + W and select “No” when asked to save. This gives you a fresh start.

Deleting Graphs

Section titled “Deleting Graphs”

If your graph screen is getting cluttered, you have two ways to clean it up:

  1. Delete a single graph: Press Tab to open the entry line, press the up arrow (↑) to highlight the function you want to remove (e.g., f1(x)f1(x)f1(x)), and press the Del key.
  2. Delete everything on the graph: Press Menu > 1: Actions > 6: Delete All.

2. Sketching, Tables, and Windows

Section titled “2. Sketching, Tables, and Windows”

Sketching a Graph

Section titled “Sketching a Graph”

When you enter a function into a new Graphs document, the curve might not appear on the screen. This means the graph exists outside your current Viewing Window. Always check the domain given in the question (e.g., −5≤x≤5-5 \leq x \leq 5−5≤x≤5) and set your window to match:

  • Press Menu > 4: Window / Zoom > 1: Window Settings and adjust your XMinXMinXMin, XMaxXMaxXMax, YMinYMinYMin, and YMaxYMaxYMax.

Producing a Table of Values

Section titled “Producing a Table of Values”

If an exam asks you to plot points on a grid, do not calculate them manually!

  1. Enter the function and draw the graph.
  2. Press Ctrl + T to bring up the split-screen table.
  3. To change what the table counts by, press Menu > 2: Table > 5: Edit Table Settings. Change the “Table Step”.
  4. Press Ctrl + T again to hide the table when you are done.

3. Finding Zeros, Maxima, and Minima

Section titled “3. Finding Zeros, Maxima, and Minima”

You must be able to instruct your TI-Nspire to locate specific coordinates on a curve using the Analyze Graph menu.

  • Zeros (Roots or x-intercepts): The points where the graph crosses the horizontal x-axis (y=0y = 0y=0).
  • Local Maximum: A “peak” or hilltop on the graph.
  • Local Minimum: A “trough” or valley on the graph.

Let’s look at the graph of the cubic function f(x)=x3−3x+1f(x) = x^3 - 3x + 1f(x)=x3−3x+1:

Steve's Exam Wisdom

The Lower Bound / Upper Bound Swipe

When you select Menu > 6: Analyze Graph > 1: Zero (or Minimum/Maximum), the Nspire will ask for a Lower Bound. Click your trackpad to the left of the point you want. It then asks for an Upper Bound. Swipe and click to the right of the point. A shaded region will appear, and the calculator will find the exact point inside that region.

4. Finding Intersections of Functions

Section titled “4. Finding Intersections of Functions”

To solve simultaneous equations graphically, or to find where two lines meet, graph both functions and use the intersection tool.

Let’s look at the intersection between the quadratic f(x)=−x2+4f(x) = -x^2 + 4f(x)=−x2+4 and the linear graph g(x)=x+2g(x) = x + 2g(x)=x+2.

Finding Multiple Intersections

Go to Menu > 6: Analyze Graph > 4: Intersection. You can only find one intersection at a time. You must do the “Lower Bound / Upper Bound” swipe around the first intersection point to get its coordinates, and then completely repeat the process (Menu > 6 > 4) for the second intersection.

5. Finding the Vertex of a Quadratic

Section titled “5. Finding the Vertex of a Quadratic”

The vertex of a quadratic is simply its turning point. You do not need a special “vertex” button. Just use the Minimum function if the quadratic is positive (a ‘U’ shape), or the Maximum function if the quadratic is negative (an ‘n’ shape).

Vertex and Symmetry

The x-coordinate of the vertex also gives you the equation for the vertical line of symmetry of the quadratic graph. In the example above, the line of symmetry is exactly x=2x = 2x=2.

6. Practice Problems

Section titled “6. Practice Problems”
  • 📝 Question 1: Finding Extrema
  • ✅ Solution 1

A function is given by f(x)=x3−5x2+2x+8f(x) = x^3 - 5x^2 + 2x + 8f(x)=x3−5x2+2x+8. Using a graphic display calculator, find the coordinates of the local maximum and the local minimum. Round your answers to 2 decimal places.

  1. Open a new Graphs document and enter f1(x)=x3−5x2+2x+8f1(x) = x^3 - 5x^2 + 2x + 8f1(x)=x3−5x2+2x+8.
  2. Adjust your Window Settings (Menu > 4 > 1) if you cannot see the distinct peaks and troughs.
  3. Press Menu > 6: Analyze Graph > 3: Maximum. Select your lower and upper bounds around the peak. The calculator will output x=0.2137...x = 0.2137...x=0.2137... and y=8.211...y = 8.211...y=8.211...
  4. Press Menu > 6: Analyze Graph > 2: Minimum. Select bounds around the trough. The calculator will output x=3.1196...x = 3.1196...x=3.1196... and y=−4.063...y = -4.063...y=−4.063...

Answers: Local Maximum: (0.21,8.21)(0.21, 8.21)(0.21,8.21) Local Minimum: (3.12,−4.06)(3.12, -4.06)(3.12,−4.06)
